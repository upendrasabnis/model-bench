**1. Unincorporated groups under council auspices**

The grant programme allows applications from unincorporated groups provided they are formally linked to a legally recognised body. In your case, because the local council holds insurance and provides governance, you can apply as an “affiliated community group.” The council must:
- Issue a written endorsement letter confirming its support,
- Provide a copy of the council’s constitution or by‑laws that lists the garden committee as a recognised sub‑committee or project team, and
- Agree to act as the legal entity for the grant (i.e., sign the award agreement and manage the funds_whatever).

If the council is unwilling or unable to act as the grant holder, you can register as a provisional charitable association (or form a company limited by guarantee) before the application deadline. The fundraising and operational budget you’ve described falls well within the typical size of projects we fund.

**2. Grant amount limits**

The Community Resilience Grant offers a minimum award of $5,000 and a maximum of $25,000 per project. Your planned expenditure on rainwater tanks and a shade structure is likely to be fully covered within this range, provided you can demonstrate a clear cost‑benefit analysis and a phased implementation plan. We encourage you to prepare a detailed budget (see the attached sample) so we can assess the request against the funding ceiling.

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs**

All infrastructure projects that enhance the community’s resilience to climate change are eligible. The installation of rainwater tanks and a shade structure to maintain operations during hotter summers squarely meets the “infrastructure” criteria, provided the costs are directly linked to the garden’s core mission of food production and community engagement.
