Dear Jamie,

Thank you for your enquiry regarding the Community Resilience Grant round. I appreciate you reaching out before investing time in a full application, and I am pleased to provide clarification on each of your questions.

**Eligibility of Auspiced Groups:** Unincorporated groups operating under a formal auspicing arrangement with an eligible legal entity—such as a local government authority—are eligible to apply, provided the auspicing organisation meets the program’s eligibility criteria (e.g., incorporated not-for-profit, local government, or other eligible entity). The auspicing organisation must submit the application on your behalf, enter into the funding agreement, and assume legal and financial responsibility for the grant. Please ensure you have a written auspicing agreement in place that clearly outlines the roles, responsibilities, and liability arrangements between your garden group and the council.

**Grant Amounts:** For this round, the minimum grant request is $5,000 and the maximum is $50,000. Your project budget should fall within this range. There is no requirement for matched funding, although evidence of in-kind contributions or co-contributions will strengthen your application.

**Eligible Costs – Infrastructure:** Capital infrastructure that directly enhances community resilience is an eligible cost category. Rainwater tanks and permanent shade structures that enable continued operation during extreme heat events align with the program’s objectives. Eligible expenditure includes purchase, delivery, installation, and any necessary site preparation or plumbing/electrical work certified by a licensed tradesperson. Ongoing maintenance costs, however, are not eligible.

**Supporting Documents to Prepare:**
1. **Auspicing agreement** signed by both parties.
2. **Letter of support** from the council confirming the arrangement and insurance coverage.
3. **Project plan** with timeline, risk management, and volunteer capacity.
4. **Detailed budget** with at least two quotes for each major cost item (tanks, structure, installation).
5. **Evidence of land tenure or permission** to install infrastructure on the site.
6. **Most recent financial statement** or treasurer’s report showing the ~$40,000 turnover.
7. **Incorporation/ABN details** of the auspicing council.

Applications close at 5:00 pm AEST on 28 June 2024. I recommend starting the draft in our online portal early, as the system requires the auspicing organisation’s authorised officer to submit. If you would like a brief pre-application discussion, please reply with a few suggested times next week.
