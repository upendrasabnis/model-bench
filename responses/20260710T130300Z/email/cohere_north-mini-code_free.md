**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our grant program welcomes applications from a broad spectrum of community‑based organisations, and unincorporated groups that operate under the formal auspice of a local government entity are indeed eligible, provided that the council can demonstrate its endorsement of the project and assume financial and legal responsibility for any grant‑related obligations. In practice, this usually means that the council must sign a statement of support (or a memorandum of understanding) confirming that it will hold any necessary insurances, manage the grant funds, and act as the primary point of contact should issues arise. If your council is willing to provide such documentation, your group can proceed with confidence.  

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The Community Resilience Grant does not prescribe a fixed minimum or maximum award. Funding levels are determined on a case‑by‑case basis, reflecting the scope, impact, and budget of the proposed project. Historically, awards have ranged from approximately $5,000 to $150,000, but applications that fall outside this range are still considered if they demonstrate strong community benefit and a realistic implementation plan. The key is to align your requested amount with the actual costs of the rainwater tanks, shade structure, and any ancillary works.  

**Eligibility of infrastructure such as rainwater tanks and shade structures**  
Yes, capital infrastructure that directly supports the sustainability and resilience of community operations is an eligible cost. Rainwater harvesting systems and shade structures clearly fall within our “climate‑adaptation and environmental stewardship” category because they:  

1. Reduce water consumption and reliance on municipal supplies, thereby enhancing the garden’s resilience during periods of heat stress.  
2. Provide a controlled micro‑climate that protects both produce and volunteers, extending the growing season and improving safety.  

Both items must be essential to the project’s objectives, reasonably priced, and include a clear specification (e.g., tank capacity, material, durability). If you can provide quotations or provisional estimates, that will help the review panel assess the cost‑effectiveness of your proposal.  

**Supporting documents to prepare**  
To strengthen your application, we recommend assembling the following documentation:  

1. **Council endorsement letter** – a formal statement from your local council confirming its auspice status, insurance coverage, and willingness to administer the grant if awarded.  
2. **Organisation profile** – a brief constitution or set of operating principles outlining your volunteer structure, governance, and any recent activities.  
3. **Financial statements** – the most recent audited or reviewed financial statements (or a simple profit‑and‑loss summary if an audit is not feasible) showing the $40,000 turnover you referenced, along with a breakdown of revenue sources.  
4. **Budget and cash‑flow forecast** – a detailed budget for the rainwater tanks, shade structure, installation, and any ongoing maintenance costs, accompanied by a one‑year cash‑flow projection.  
5. **Project plan** – a timeline (including milestones, deliverables, and responsible parties), a description of the installation process, and a risk‑mitigation strategy (e.g., contingency plans for supply chain delays).  
6. **Community impact assessment** – evidence of need (such as usage statistics, local climate data, or surveys of garden users) and measurable outcomes you expect to achieve (e.g., water savings, reduced heat exposure).  
7. **Quotes and specifications** – at least two independent quotations for the tanks and shade structure, together with technical datasheets that confirm capacity, material quality, and warranty.  
8. **Letters of support** – endorsements from partner organisations, local businesses, or community members that highlight the broader significance of the project.
