## 1. Introduction – The Grant Lifecycle in Modern Funding  

**Line 34**  
The grant lifecycle traditionally consists of five core phases: **application intake**, **assessment**, **disbursement**, **reporting**, and **impact measurement**.  

**Line 35**  
Each phase involves multiple stakeholders — program officers, reviewers, finance teams, and the grantees themselves — who exchange documents, data, and decisions across disparate systems.  

**Line 36**  
Without a unified platform, these exchanges become siloed, leading to delays, version‑control errors, and compliance risks.  

**Line 37**  
Grant management software (GMS) consolidates all activities into a single, configurable environment, enabling **end‑to‑end automation** and **real‑time visibility** for both funders and grantees.  

**Line 38**  
This article dissects how a modern GMS streamlines each lifecycle stage, illustrates the process with concrete examples, and equips you with a **feature‑comparison framework** to select the right solution.

## 9. Best Practices for Funders & Grantees  

**Line 108**

**Line 109**

- Build **rubrics that align with strategic priorities**; involve diverse reviewers.  
- Set **milestone thresholds** that are realistic and measurable.  
- Use **automated compliance checks** to avoid manual errors.

**Line 110**  
### 9.2. Grantee‑Side Checklist  

**Line 111**  
- Complete the **pre‑submission eligibility quiz** to avoid disqualification.  
- Populate the **budget spreadsheet** with line‑item detail; use the built‑in validation tool.  
- Submit **all required attachments** (letters of support, tax‑exempt status) in the prescribed format.  
- Monitor the **milestone dashboard** and upload supporting documents promptly.  
- Leverage the **impact KPI library** to prepare concise outcome statements for final reporting.
