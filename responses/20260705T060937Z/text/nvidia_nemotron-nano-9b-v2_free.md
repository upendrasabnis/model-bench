### **I. Overview of the CDBG Program and Eligibility Criteria**  

#### **A. What is the CDBG Program?**  
- **Purpose**: The Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) program, administered by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), provides funding to support community development projects that address critical needs in economically distressed areas.  
- **Focus Areas**: CDBG funds are used for projects that foster economic growth, reduce poverty, improve infrastructure, and create affordable housing.  
- **Administration**: Grants are awarded through a competitive process at the state or local level, with HUD providing guidelines and oversight.  

#### **B. Eligibility Criteria for Small Towns**  
- **Population Threshold**: Towns with populations under 50,000 may qualify if they demonstrate significant economic or social challenges.  
- **Economic Distress Indicators**:  
  - High unemployment rates (e.g., >10%).  
  - Declining tax bases due to loss of manufacturing jobs.  
  - Limited access to affordable housing or essential services.  
- **Community Need**: Projects must address specific, measurable needs identified through public input or data analysis.  
- **Matching Funds Requirement**: Applicants must commit to a 20–50% local match (cash or in-kind), though exemptions may apply for distressed communities.

### **II. Step-by-Step Guide to Preparing a Competitive CDBG Application**  

#### **A. Step 1: Conduct a Community Needs Assessment**  
- **Objective**: Identify critical gaps in infrastructure, housing, or economic opportunities.  
- **Tools**:  
  - Surveys of residents and businesses.  
  - Analysis of unemployment data, property values, and economic trends.  
  - SWOT analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats).  

#### **B. Step 2: Form a Coalition of Stakeholders**  
- **Key Members**: Local government officials, business leaders, nonprofits, and residents.  
- **Role**: Ensure alignment of priorities and build community buy-in.  

#### **C. Step 3: Develop a Comprehensive Project Plan**  
- **Components**:  
  - Clear objectives (e.g., create 50 jobs, build 20 affordable housing units).  
  - Detailed budget and timeline.  
  - Alignment with CDBG priorities (e.g., revitalization, economic diversification).  

#### **D. Step 4: Gather Supporting Data and Documentation**  
- **Examples**:  
  - Economic impact studies.  
  - Letters of support from stakeholders.  
  - Maps or diagrams of proposed projects.  

#### **E. Step 5: Prepare the Application**  
- **Key Sections**:  
  - Narrative detailing the community’s needs and proposed solutions.  
  - Budget breakdown with matching fund commitments.  
  - Evaluation plan to measure success.  

#### **F. Step 6: Submit the Application and Engage with Reviewers**  
- **Tip**: Attend HUD webinars or consult local CDBG coordinators for feedback.

### **III. Required Matching Funds and Cost-Benefit Analysis**  

#### **A. Matching Fund Requirements**  
- **Minimum Match**: 20% of the project cost (cash or in-kind).  
- **Maximizing Match**:  
  - Leverage local tax revenues.  
  - Partner with private entities for in-kind contributions (e.g., labor, materials).  
  - Seek additional grants or loans to supplement the match.  

#### **B. Cost-Benefit Analysis Framework**  
- **Quantitative Metrics**:  
  - Job creation (e.g., 100 jobs over 5 years).  
  - Tax revenue generated (e.g., $500,000 annual increase).  
- **Qualitative Benefits**:  
  - Improved quality of life.  
  - Enhanced community pride.

### **V. Case Studies: Successful CDBG Implementations in the Midwest**  

#### **Case Study 1: Gary, Indiana**  
- **Challenge**: Post-millennial factory closures led to population decline.  
- **CDBG Use**: $2.5M for downtown revitalization, including:  
  - Infrastructure repairs (roads, utilities).  
  - 30 affordable housing units.  
- **Outcome**: 40 new jobs, 15% population growth.  

#### **Case Study 2: Toledo, Ohio**  
- **Challenge**: Decline in manufacturing jobs.  
- **CDBG Use**: $3M for a tech-focused small-business incubator.  
- **Outcome**: 100 startups launched, 200 jobs created.  

#### **Case Study 3: Saginaw, Michigan**  
- **Challenge**: Aging infrastructure and housing shortage.  
- **CDBG Use**: $4M for mixed-use development and transit upgrades.  
- **Outcome**: $10M in private investment attracted to the area.
