FDI actively encourages proposals that combine direct program implementation with complementary capacity‑building activities. Mentor‑training modules, data‑driven impact evaluation, and sustainability planning are viewed as value‑added components that strengthen long‑term outcomes. The Foundation’s current Call for Collaborative Grants is open to nonprofit organizations operating within the United States, with a particular emphasis on projects that serve historically underserved communities in Texas. Joint proposals should be submitted via FDI’s online grants portal as a single PDF package not exceeding 25 MB. Required sections include: (a) a project narrative (max 5 pages), (b) a detailed budget narrative and line‑item budget, (c) a logic model or theory of change, (d) letters of commitment from each collaborating entity (school districts, trainer organizations, etc.), and (e) a signed Memorandum of Understanding outlining the co‑funding arrangement. The deadline for Year 1 submissions is **15 March 2025**, with award notifications expected by **30 April 2025**. Awardees are expected to submit quarterly financial reports (using FDI’s standard expense tracking template) and semi‑annual progress reports that outline output metrics (e.g., number of participants trained, equipment deployed) and outcome metrics (e.g., STEM enrollment changes, pre‑/post‑assessment scores). A mid‑term review call is scheduled at the six‑month mark to assess budget adherence, data collection quality, and any needed adjustments. Final reporting, including a comprehensive impact evaluation and an audited financial statement, is due within 90 days of project closure.
