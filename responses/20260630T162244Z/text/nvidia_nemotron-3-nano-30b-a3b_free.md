### 4. Challenges Unique to Small Towns  

#### 4.1. Limited Administrative Capacity  
- Small staff, lack of grant‑writing expertise, outdated IT systems  

#### 4.2. Fragmented Needs & Priorities  
- Housing, water infrastructure, economic development, mental‑health services often treated as separate silos  

#### 4.3. Rapid Response vs. Long‑Term Resilience  
- Immediate relief funds (e.g., FEMA PA) are time‑bound; resilience funds require longer planning horizons  

#### 4.4. Matching‑Fund Constraints  
- Many programs require 25‑50 % local match — difficult when tax base is thin  

#### 4.5. Regulatory & Compliance Burdens  
- Complex cost‑allowability rules, procurement thresholds, environmental reviews

### 7. Real‑World Case Studies (≈ 1,200‑1,500 words each)  

#### Case Study 1 – **Holly Springs, NC** (Hurricane Florence, 2018)  
- **Context**: Population ≈ 5,000; 30 % of homes damaged  
- **Funding Mix**: FEMA PA + HMGP + Kresge Foundation “Resilient Communities” grant + private utility contribution  
- **Strategy Highlights**:  
  - Early stakeholder advisory board formed within 2 weeks  
  - Leveraged 1 : 4 match using volunteer labor (valued at $30/hr)  
  - Developed a 5‑year resilience plan with upgraded storm‑water system  
- **Outcomes**: 95 % of damaged homes rebuilt to flood‑resistant standards; 30 % reduction in future flood risk rating  

#### Case Study 2 – **Pine Ridge, AZ** (Wildfire 2020)  
- **Context**: Rural tribal community; 40 % of structures lost  
- **Funding Mix**: USDA Rural Development Community Facilities Grant + State Emergency Management Grant + private foundation “FireSafe” award  
- **Strategy Highlights**:  
  - Integrated cultural‑preservation goals into rebuild (e.g., traditional building materials)  
  - Used a “grant‑stacking” approach: FEMA mitigation grant → State grant → private matching  
  - Established a community land trust for long‑term land security  
- **Outcomes**: 80 % of homes rebuilt; new fire‑break network; capacity‑building for future grant applications  

#### Case Study 3 – **Riverbend, IA** (Flood 2023)  
- **Context**: Small town (pop ≈ 3,200) on the Mississippi; repeated flooding  
- **Funding Mix**: FEMA Public Assistance + State CDBG‑DR + corporate “Bank of America Community Recovery” grant + local bond issuance (matched)  
- **Strategy Highlights**:  
  - Developed a “Resilience Scorecard” to prioritize projects with highest risk reduction per dollar  
  - Used a Gantt chart to coordinate simultaneous debris removal and levee reinforcement  
  - Implemented a transparent public dashboard for real‑time reporting  
- **Outcomes**: Levee upgraded to 100‑year floodplain standard; 100 % of critical infrastructure restored within 18 months
