Under federal guidelines, indirect costs must be based on the Negotiated Indirect Cost Rate Agreement (NICRA) of the specific entity incurring the costs. Therefore, each partner must apply their own respective negotiated rate to their own portion of the direct costs. Riverside Community Health Center will apply its 14% rate, while partners without a NICRA should apply the 10% *de minimis* rate, as permitted under 2 CFR § 200.414. Averaging rates across partners is not an allowable practice.

Second, regarding the budget narrative: To remain compliant, your narrative should clearly delineate the indirect cost calculations for each partner. Please include a table or a detailed breakdown specifying the direct costs for each organization and the corresponding rate applied. This transparency ensures that auditors can easily verify that each partner is charging their own approved rate, regardless of differing audit cycles.

Third, concerning the budget threshold: For awards exceeding $500,000, there are no automatic ceiling limits on indirect costs provided the rates used are federally negotiated or the *de minimis* rate is applied. Given your projected budget of $1.2 million, we recommend a thorough internal review to ensure that indirect charges do not overlap with direct costs (e.g., ensuring administrative salaries are not charged both as a direct cost and within the indirect pool).
