### 1. Unified Indirect Cost Rate for the Consortium

Under OMB Uniform Guidance § 215.304, each institution that is a fiscal sponsor or a direct participant in a collaborative grant must attach its own indirect cost rate agreement (ICRA) or a provisional rate agreement to the proposal. The rate you may apply in the consolidated budget can only be a single rate if **all** consortium partners have the same negotiated rate and have agreed to use that rate for the award.  

In CHOC’s case, Riverside Community Health Center (RCHC) has a 14 % negotiated rate, while the other partners are operating under a provisional 10 % rate. Because the rates differ, the safest and most compliant approach is to **use each partner’s individual rate** in the budget. If you wish to present a single consolidated number for simplicity, you may do so only after every partner signs a formal agreement to adopt RCHC’s 14 % rate for this specific award. Until that agreement is in place, the budget must reflect the true rate of each institution.

---

### 2. Budget Narrative Presentation

* **Separate Line Items** – List each partner on the budget narrative with its own indirect cost line (e.g., “RCHC – 14 % of MTDC” and “Partner A – 10 % of MTDC”).  
* **Provisional Rate Note** – For partners who have not yet completed negotiations, add a footnote explaining that the 10 % rate is provisional and will be updated once the final ICRA is signed.  
* **Audit Cycle Variance** – OMB allows varying audit cycles; simply state that each partner’s audit cycle is “as per its own internal controls” and that the indirect rates are attached per the most recent ICRA or provisional agreement.  
* **Consistency** – Ensure that the percentages shown in the narrative match the percentages used in the detailed budget tables.

---

### 3. Ceiling Limits & Large‑Award Considerations

There is no specific “ceiling” on indirect costs for awards over $500,000 beyond the negotiated rate. The indirect cost must not exceed the rate established in the ICRA. Because your projected direct costs are $1.2 million, the indirect cost will be limited to the sum of each partner’s negotiated rate applied to their share of MTDC.  

The only additional consideration is that the award must be “cost‑allowable” under the Uniform Guidance; that is, the indirect cost must be a reasonable, negotiated rate. As long as each partner’s rate is documented and attached, there is no separate limit beyond the negotiated rate.

---

### 4. Supporting Documentation

* **Lead Applicant (RCHC)** – Attach a copy of RCHC’s signed ICRA (or provisional rate agreement) as a separate attachment.  
* **Other Partners** – Each partner must attach its own ICRA or provisional rate agreement. If a partner has not yet negotiated a rate, they must attach a provisional rate agreement that clearly states the rate and the expected date of finalization.  
* **Consolidated Statement** – If all partners sign a single “consortium rate” agreement (e.g., agreeing to use RCHC’s 14 % rate), you can provide a consolidated statement from the lead applicant. However, until such an agreement exists, the individual attachments are required.
