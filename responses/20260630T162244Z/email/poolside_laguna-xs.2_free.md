Thank you for your email and for reaching out with these important questions regarding the indirect cost rate allocation for your FY 2027 Health Equity Innovation Grant application. To address your specific questions:

**Regarding the use of a single, unified indirect cost rate:** Under OMB Uniform Guidance (2 CFR Part 200), each constituent organization in a consortium must apply its own currently negotiated indirect cost rate to its direct costs. Unfortunately, the consortium cannot average the rates or apply the lead applicant’s 14% rate across all partners. Each organization should use the rate that applies to its own costs: Riverside Community Health Center would use 14%, while your smaller partners should use their provisional 10% rates until they complete their negotiations. This approach ensures compliance with the cost principles established for each individual entity.

**For the budget narrative presentation:** Your narrative should clearly delineate each partner organization’s direct costs and corresponding indirect costs, specifying which rate applies to each. Include a brief explanation that rates are applied in accordance with each organization’s most recently negotiated or provisional rates as established under Uniform Guidance. For partners with pending negotiations, note that the provisional rate will be honored for the grant period, with any adjustments handled through the standard federal rate modification process.

**Regarding the $500,000 threshold:** While there is no specific ceiling on indirect costs for awards exceeding this amount, you should be aware that cost reasonableness and allocability remain paramount. The total indirect cost proposal must still adhere to the organization’s approved rate and cannot exceed what is necessary to meet project objectives.

**For supporting documentation:** Each partner organization must provide documentation of their respective indirect cost rates. This means submitting Riverside’s rate agreement as well as documentation supporting the 10% provisional rates for your smaller partners. A consolidated statement from the lead applicant alone will not suffice, as each entity’s rates must be verifiable independently.
