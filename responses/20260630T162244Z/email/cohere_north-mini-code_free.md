Dear Ms. Hernandez,

Thank you for sharing the details of your upcoming FY 2027 Health Equity Innovation Grant application. I appreciate the thoroughness with which CHOC is approaching the indirect‑cost (IDC) questions and am pleased to provide clarification on each of the points you raised.

### 1. Applying a Single Unified Indirect‑Cost Rate  
Under the OMB Uniform Guidance, each *distinct* legal entity that is the recipient of federal funds must use its own negotiated indirect‑cost rate. If CHOC is submitting a **single‑entity** application (i.e., the Consortium itself is incorporated as a separate legal entity and will administer the award), you may adopt a single rate—typically the **lead organization’s negotiated rate (14 %)**—provided that the Consortium’s governing documents authorize it to absorb and allocate indirect costs on behalf of its partners.  

If, however, the award will be **distributed to each partner as a sub‑award**, each sub‑awardee must apply its own negotiated (or provisional) rate (10 %–18 %). In that scenario an “average” rate is not permissible; the budget must reflect the individual rates for each organization’s portion of the work.

### 2. Budget Narrative Presentation and Audit‑Cycle Considerations  
When a single rate is used, the narrative should explain the rationale (central administration of the consortium) and describe how the IDC amount was calculated (e.g., “14 % of the total modified direct costs for the Consortium’s administrative activities”). If sub‑awards are employed, the narrative should break out the IDC for each partner, citing the specific rate and the corresponding direct‑cost base. This approach satisfies auditors who review each organization’s fiscal year separately.

### 3. Ceiling Limits for Awards Exceeding $500,000  
The Uniform Guidance does **not impose a statutory ceiling** on indirect costs for nonprofit organizations, regardless of total direct costs. The only agency‑specific caps (e.g., a 10 % limit for certain HHS grants) apply only when explicitly stated in the funding announcement. Since the Health Equity Innovation Grant does not list such a cap, your projected $1.2 million budget can accommodate the negotiated rate(s) without additional approval, though you should be prepared to demonstrate reasonableness during audit.

### 4. Documentation Requirements  
To ensure compliance, we recommend providing:

* **Lead‑organization rate agreement** (the 14 % negotiation letter from the cognizant agency).  
* For each partner that will receive a sub‑award, the **most recent negotiated rate agreement** or, if still provisional, the documentation that established the 10 % provisional rate.  

A consolidated statement from the lead applicant is acceptable **only** if the consortium itself is the sole recipient and the rate agreement for the lead organization clearly reflects that the consortium will absorb all indirect costs on behalf of its members.
