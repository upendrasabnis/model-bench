**1. Application of Indirect Cost Rates**
In a multi-partner collaborative submission, the Consortium may not apply a single, unified indirect cost rate across the entire budget. Under OMB Uniform Guidance, indirect costs must be applied based on the specific negotiated rate of the entity incurring the expense. Therefore, you should not average the rates or apply the lead applicant’s rate to the sub-recipients. Riverside Community Health Center should apply its 14% negotiated rate to its portion of the budget, while the partner organizations should apply their respective provisional rates (e.up., 10%) to their specific allocations.

**2. Budget Narrative Presentation**
To ensure compliance and facilitate the review process, the budget narrative must clearly delineate indirect costs by partner organization. We recommend a structured approach where each partner’s direct costs are listed, followed by a specific line item for their indirect costs, explicitly stating the rate being utilized (e.g., "10% Provisional Indirect Cost Rate"). This transparency will mitigate concerns regarding differing audit cycles, as it demonstrates that each entity is adhering to its currently authorized rate.

**3. Thresholds and Ceiling Limits**
While the projected budget of $1.2 million is substantial, the current grant guidelines do not impose a specific ceiling on indirect cost percentages for awards exceeding $500,000 in direct costs. However, please ensure that all indirect costs are calculated against the Modified Total Direct Cost (MTDC) base, as per standard federal requirements, to avoid over-recovery.

For the initial application, a consolidated statement from Riverside Community Health Center outlining the budget structure is sufficient. However, should your proposal be selected for funding, we will require the formal Negotiated Indirect Cost Rate Agreement (NICRA) for the lead applicant and documentation of the provisional rate determinations for the partner organizations during the pre-award audit phase.
