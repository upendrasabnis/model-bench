DearMs. Hernandez,

Thank you for reaching out and for the detailed description of CHOC’s upcoming FY 2027 Health Equity Innovation Grant application. I have reviewed your questions against the OMB Uniform Guidance (2 CFR § 200.414) and the specific provisions of the Health Equity Innovation Grant (CFDA 93.987). Below I address each point in turn.

**1. Single, unified indirect cost rate**  
Uniform Guidance permits a consortium to use a single indirect cost rate only when the lead applicant acts as the fiscal agent and has a negotiated rate that covers all subrecipients’ indirect costs. If Riverside Community Health Center (RCHC) will serve as the fiscal agent and its negotiated 14 % rate is approved by its cognizant agency to apply to the entire consortium, you may apply that rate to the total modified direct costs (MTDC) of the joint budget. Should RCHC’s rate not be authorized to cover the partners, each organization must use its own negotiated (or provisional) rate. In that case you may present a weighted‑average rate in the budget summary for transparency, but the actual indirect cost charges must be calculated separately for each partner using their respective rates.

**2. Presentation in the budget narrative**  
The budget narrative should clearly state:  
- The legal relationship (lead applicant/fiscal agent vs. subrecipient).  
- The indirect cost rate applied to each entity (e.g., “RCHC: 14 % negotiated rate; Partner A: 10 % provisional rate; Partner B: 12 % negotiated rate,” etc.).  
- The basis for any provisional rates (e.g., “pending final negotiation with cognizant agency; subject to adjustment upon receipt of final rate agreement”).  
- A note that audit cycles differ, but that each organization will provide its most recent Single Audit or financial statement as required by the grant’s reporting schedule.  
This approach satisfies the cost‑allowability requirement that indirect costs be “reasonable, allocable, and consistently applied.”

**3. Ceiling limits for awards > $500,000 MTDC**  
The Uniform Guidance does not impose a statutory ceiling on indirect costs; the limitation is that the rate must be the one negotiated with the cognizant agency (or the de minimis 10 % rate if no negotiation exists). Therefore, even with a projected $1.2 million MTDC, you may apply the approved rates without an additional cap, provided the rates are justified and documented. If any partner elects to use the de minimis rate, it must be justified that they have never received a negotiated rate.

**4. Supporting documentation**  
Each organization that claims an indirect cost rate must submit its current rate agreement (or provisional rate approval) with the application. If RCHC is the fiscal agent and its rate is authorized to cover the consortium, you may submit RCHC’s rate agreement together with a statement from the cognizant agency confirming that the rate applies to all subrecipients. Otherwise, attach the individual agreements for Riverside and each partner. Include a consolidated indirect cost schedule that shows the calculation for each entity and the total indirect cost amount requested.
