Dear Ms. Hernandez,

Thank you for your detailed inquiry regarding the indirect cost rate allocation for the Community Health Outreach Consortium’s (CHOC) upcoming application. I appreciate the thoroughness with which you have outlined the consortium’s structure and the specific compliance concerns. Below, I address each of your questions in turn to assist you in finalizing a compliant budget.

1. **Unified Indirect Cost Rate for the Collaborative Budget**  
Under 2 CFR 200.414 and the specific terms of the FY 2027 Health Equity Innovation Grant, a consortium submission must reflect the actual negotiated rate of each participating entity. A single, blended rate applied across all partners is not permissible. Riverside Community Health Center, as the lead applicant, must use its current federally negotiated rate of 14 % of modified total direct costs (MTDC). Each subrecipient partner must apply its own applicable rate: those with finalized agreements use their negotiated rates, while partners still operating under a provisional 10 % rate must use that provisional rate until a final rate is established. The budget should present these rates separately for each organization within the consolidated budget workbook.

2. **Budget Narrative Presentation and Audit Cycle Differences**  
In the budget narrative, clearly delineate indirect costs by organization. Provide a table that lists each entity, its applicable rate (negotiated or provisional), the MTDC base for that entity, and the resulting indirect cost amount. Reference the cognizant agency and the date of the most recent rate agreement or provisional approval. Differing audit cycles do not affect the rate used for the application; however, you must note in the narrative that final indirect costs will be reconciled to actual audited rates during post‑award closeout, consistent with 2 CFR 200.332.

3. **Ceiling Limits and Special Considerations Above $500,000 in Direct Costs**  
The program announcement for CFDA 93.987 does not impose a separate indirect cost ceiling beyond the statutory limits in 2 CFR 200.414. Because your projected total direct costs exceed $500,000, ensure that the MTDC base excludes capital expenditures, participant support costs, and the portion of each subaward exceeding $25,000, as required by the Uniform Guidance. No additional program‑specific cap applies, but the total indirect costs claimed must be reasonable, allocable, and consistently applied across all partners.

4. **Supporting Documentation Requirements**  
You must attach a copy of Riverside Community Health Center’s current negotiated rate agreement (or the most recent provisional approval) as part of the lead applicant’s documentation. For each subrecipient, include either their final negotiated rate agreement or, where negotiations are incomplete, a signed letter from the cognizant agency confirming the provisional 10 % rate and the anticipated timeline for finalization. A consolidated statement from the lead applicant alone is insufficient; the reviewers require the underlying agreements for every entity receiving indirect cost reimbursement.
