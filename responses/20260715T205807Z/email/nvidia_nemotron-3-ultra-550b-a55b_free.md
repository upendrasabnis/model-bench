Subject: Re: Question about eligibility for the Community Resilience Grant

Dear Jamie,

Thank you for your enquiry regarding the Community Resilience Grant round. I appreciate you reaching out before investing time in a full application, and I am happy to provide clarity on each of your questions.

**Eligibility of Auspiced Groups:** Yes, unincorporated groups operating under a formal auspice arrangement with an eligible legal entity—such as your local council—are eligible to apply. The auspicing organisation must be an incorporated body (e.g., local government, incorporated association, or company limited by guarantee) that holds current public liability insurance and agrees to accept legal and financial responsibility for the grant. You will need to provide a signed auspice agreement or letter from the council confirming this arrangement, including their ABN and insurance details.

**Grant Amounts:** For this round, the minimum grant request is $5,000 and the maximum is $50,000. Your project budget should fall within this range. Given your annual turnover of approximately $40,000, please ensure the requested amount is proportionate to your organisational capacity and the project scale.

**Eligible Costs – Infrastructure:** Capital infrastructure that directly supports community resilience is an eligible cost category. Rainwater tanks and a permanent shade structure to mitigate heat impacts and ensure continued operation during extreme weather events align squarely with the program objectives. Eligible expenditure includes purchase, delivery, installation, and any necessary site preparation or plumbing/electrical work. Ongoing maintenance costs, however, are not funded.

**Supporting Documents to Prepare:** In addition to the auspice agreement, please gather:
- A detailed project plan with timelines, risk management, and community benefit statements.
- Two written quotes for each major cost item (tanks, structure, installation).
- Your group’s most recent financial statement (income/expenditure) and a simple budget for the current financial year.
- Evidence of land tenure or permission to install infrastructure on the site (e.g., council licence or lease).
- Letters of support from at least two community stakeholders demonstrating broader benefit.

Applications close at 5:00 pm AEST on 30 November. I encourage you to start the online application early so you can save drafts and request any additional clarification well before the deadline. Please do not hesitate to contact me if you need further guidance.
