Subject: Re: Question about eligibility for the Community Resilience Grant  

Dear Jamie,

Thank you for reaching out and for your thoughtful enquiry about the Community Resilience Grant. I’m pleased to provide clarification on each of the points you raised so that you can assess the fit of your project before committing to a full application.

**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our funding guidelines allow organisations that are not formally incorporated but operate under the umbrella of a local government entity to apply, provided the council holds the necessary insurance and can confirm the group’s legal capacity to receive and manage grant monies. Since your garden operates under the auspices of the municipal council and the council’s insurances are in place, you meet this eligibility criterion. We do ask that you supply a letter from the council confirming its support and outlining the scope of its oversight.

**2. Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The Community Resilience Grant offers funding between **$5,000 and $50,000**. Projects that fall below the minimum are not eligible, while those exceeding the maximum may be considered only if they demonstrate exceptional community benefit and have a compelling justification. Your proposed investment in rain‑water tanks and a shade structure comfortably sits within this range.

**3. Eligibility of the proposed infrastructure**  
Infrastructure that enhances the resilience of a community asset—such as water‑capture systems, shading, or other adaptations that enable continued operation under climate stress—is expressly eligible. The rain‑water tanks and shade structure you describe qualify as eligible costs, provided they are directly tied to the garden’s core function and are documented as such in the budget.

**4. Supporting documentation to prepare**  
To strengthen your application, please include the following:  

- A signed letter of endorsement from the council confirming its auspicing role and insurance coverage.  
- A detailed project budget breaking down the $40,000 annual turnover and outlining how grant funds will be allocated.  
- Evidence of community need, such as local climate impact data or testimonials from regular users of the garden.  
- Proof of non‑profit status or equivalent governance documents (e.g., council resolution that authorises the group to hold funds).  
- A risk‑management plan that demonstrates how the proposed infrastructure will improve resilience.

Should you decide to proceed, we would be happy to arrange a brief call to walk through the application form and answer any further questions you may have.
