**Eligibility of Unincorporated Groups Auspiced by a Council**  
Our grant program welcomes applications from a broad spectrum of community‑based organisations. Unincorporated groups that are formally auspiced—or “sponsored”—by a local government entity are indeed eligible, provided that the auspicing council can demonstrate that it will assume financial and legal responsibility for the grant funds and any associated liabilities. In practice, this usually means the council will act as the primary applicant and grant‑recipient, with the garden group serving as the implementing partner. To confirm eligibility, we will need a letter of auspice from your council, outlining the scope of its support (including insurance coverage, financial oversight, and decision‑making authority). Once we receive that documentation, we can proceed with the full application.

**Minimum and Maximum Grant Amounts**  
The Community Resilience Grant does not prescribe a rigid minimum or maximum award. Instead, we assess each proposal on its merit, community impact, and alignment with program objectives. Typical awards range from $5,000 to $50,000, but we have funded projects both below and above this band in previous rounds. For your rainwater‑tank and shade‑structure project, the total cost estimate will be a key factor in determining the appropriate grant size. We encourage you to provide a detailed budget that reflects realistic figures for equipment, installation, and any ancillary works.

**Eligibility of Infrastructure Costs**  
Yes, capital‑improvement items such as rainwater harvesting systems and shade structures are considered eligible expenses under the “Infrastructure and Equipment” category of the grant. Eligible costs generally include:

1. Purchase and delivery of tanks, filters, pumps, and associated plumbing.  
2. Procurement of high‑quality shade structures (e.g., pop‑up tents, permanent awnings, or greenhouse‑type covers).  
3. Installation labour, provided it is performed by qualified tradespeople.  
4. Site preparation work (e.g., foundations, drainage, and minor earthworks).  

We do ask that all items be clearly justified in terms of how they will enhance the garden’s resilience to heat stress and support year‑round operation.

**Supporting Documents to Prepare**  
To strengthen your application, we recommend assembling the following materials:

* **Project Narrative** – a concise description of the problem you are addressing, the proposed solution, expected outcomes, and a timeline with milestones.  
* **Detailed Budget and Budget Narrative** – a line‑item breakdown of all costs, indicating which items are capital versus operational, and any in‑kind contributions.  
* **Letter of Auspice** – an official statement from your council confirming its role as the grant holder, its insurance coverage, and its willingness to oversee the project.  
* **Organizational Profile** – a short overview of your garden’s mission, volunteer base, and current operations, including the $40,000 annual turnover figure you mentioned.  
* **Financial Statements** – the most recent audited or reviewed financial statements (or, for unincorporated groups, a set of simplified financial records) to verify revenue sources and demonstrate fiscal responsibility.  
* **Insurance Certificate** – confirmation that the council’s insurance policy extends to cover the proposed works and any public liability associated with the new infrastructure.  
* **Site Plans or Sketches** – diagrams showing the current layout of the garden and the intended placement of rainwater tanks and shade structures.  
* **Letters of Support** – endorsements from local businesses, community groups, or other stakeholders that highlight the broader community benefit.  

We advise you to gather these documents early, as compiling them can take several weeks.
