14. **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**  
155.  14.1 Q: How long should I keep acquittal records?  
156.    A: Typically 3‑7 years, depending on funder and jurisdiction.  
157.  14.2 Q: Can I submit an interim acquittal before the project ends?  
158.    A: Some funders allow “interim” or “partial” acquittals for multi‑year grants.  
159.  14.3 Q: What if I discover an unallowable cost after submission?  
160.    A: Notify the funder promptly, propose repayment, and document the corrective action.  
161.  14.4 Q: Are in‑kind contributions reported in the same way as cash?  
162.    A: Yes, they must be documented, valued, and included in the matching‑fund calculations.  
163.  14.5 Q: Do I need a separate audit for each grant?  
164.    A: Not necessarily; a single “single audit” can cover multiple federally‑funded programs if they meet the threshold.  
165.  14.6 Q: How do I handle confidential data in an acquittal report?  
166.    A: Aggregate data, remove personally identifiable information, and follow data‑use agreements.  
167.  14.7 Q: What are the penalties for non‑compliance?  
168.    A: Penalties range from repayment of funds, suspension of funding, debarment, to legal sanctions.  
169.  14.8 Q: Can I use a template from a previous grant?  
170.    A: Templates are acceptable if they meet the current funder’s requirements and are customized.  
171.  14.9 Q: Is there a standard format for narrative outcomes?  
172.    A: No universal format, but many funders request a logical‑framework style (inputs‑outputs‑outcomes‑impacts).  
173.  14.10 Q: How do I prove compliance with “effort” requirements?  
174.    A: Provide signed time‑cards, FTE calculations, and supervisor attestations.
