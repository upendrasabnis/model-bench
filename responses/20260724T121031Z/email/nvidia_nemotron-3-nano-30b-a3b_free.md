**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
The CRG expressly welcomes applications from unincorporated groups that operate under the auspices of a local government authority, provided the council holds the requisite public liability and workers‑compensation insurances on the group’s behalf. Our eligibility checklist confirms that such arrangements satisfy the “registered under a recognized governmental body” criterion. To demonstrate this, you will need to supply a copy of the council’s insurance certificates and a letter of endorsement from the council confirming your group’s status and operational authority.

**2. Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The current funding envelope for the CRG ranges from **$5,000 to $50,000** per successful applicant. Projects that propose a total budget below $5,000 are ineligible, while those exceeding $50,000 will require a separate co‑funding arrangement. Your proposed works — rainwater tanks and a shade structure — fall comfortably within this range, especially given your projected total cost of approximately $12,000–$15,000.

**3. Eligibility of the proposed infrastructure**  
Both rainwater harvesting systems and shade structures are classified as “eligible capital works” under the CRG guidelines, provided they contribute directly to climate‑resilience outcomes (e.g., water security and heat mitigation). We encourage you to articulate the resilience benefits clearly in your budget narrative.

**4. Supporting documentation**  
To support your application, please prepare the following:  

- A signed endorsement letter from the council confirming your auspiced status and insurance coverage.  
- Copies of the council’s public liability and workers‑compensation certificates.  
- A detailed project budget breaking down costs for the rainwater tanks, shade structure, site preparation, and any contingency.  
- Evidence of community need, such as local climate data or stakeholder letters.  
- Your most recent financial statements or a summary of annual turnover ($40,000) and sources of funding.
