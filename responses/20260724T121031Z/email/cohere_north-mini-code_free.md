Thank you for reaching out and for the detailed description of your community garden’s vision. I’m pleased to provide clarification on the eligibility criteria and application requirements for the current Community Resilience Grant round.

**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our grant programme does accept applications from unincorporated community groups, provided the group operates under the formal auspices of a local government entity that can demonstrate legal responsibility for the project. In practice, this means that if your garden is formally recognised by the council—through a memorandum of understanding, a formal agreement, or a delegated authority—the council’s legal standing and existing insurance coverage generally satisfy the applicant‑organisation requirement. Because you are not yet a registered charity, you will not need to provide a charity’s registration number, but you will need to submit a letter from the council confirming its support and its willingness to act as the primary point of contact for the grant, should the application be successful.

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The grant is designed to fund projects of varying scale. The minimum award is $5,000, while the maximum is $50,000 per fiscal year. Your proposed infrastructure (rainwater tanks and a shade structure) is estimated to fall comfortably within this range, and we would expect a grant of roughly $30,000‑$35,000 to be appropriate for the scope you have described.

**Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Yes—capital infrastructure such as rainwater harvesting systems and permanent shade structures is an eligible expense. Both items directly support the garden’s resilience to climate stress and align with the grant’s objective of enhancing community capacity to cope with extreme weather events. Please note that any costs related to ongoing maintenance or consumables (e.g., water usage fees) are not considered eligible under this grant.

**Supporting documents to prepare**  
To strengthen your application, we recommend assembling the following documents:

1. **Council Support Letter** – a formal letter confirming the council’s endorsement of the project and its responsibility for insurance and legal matters.  
2. **Project Plan** – a concise narrative outlining the problem you are addressing, the proposed solution, implementation timeline, and expected outcomes.  
3. **Budget Breakdown** – a detailed spreadsheet itemising each line item (e.g., tank purchase, contractor fees, permits) with quotations where possible.  
4. **Financial Statements** – the most recent two years of audited or reviewed accounts, plus a current cash‑flow statement, to demonstrate fiscal stability.  
5. **Insurance Certificate** – a copy of the council’s liability insurance policy, confirming coverage for the proposed works.  
6. **Letters of Support** – at least two endorsements from local businesses, community organisations, or individuals who can attest to the garden’s impact.  
7. **Site Plans/Photos** – sketches or aerial photos of the garden site that illustrate where the tanks and shade structure will be placed.
