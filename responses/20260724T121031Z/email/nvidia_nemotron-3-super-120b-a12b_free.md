Dear Jamie,

Thank you for reaching out and for the thoughtful work you are doing with the community garden. I appreciate your diligence in confirming eligibility before investing time in a full application, and I am happy to address each of your questions in turn.

**1. Auspiced, unincorporated groups**  
The Community Resilience Grant requires that the legal applicant be an incorporated entity, a registered charity, or a local government body that can accept funding and assume fiscal responsibility. An unincorporated group may apply **only** if it is auspiced by an eligible entity that will act as the grant recipient and will be responsible for the financial acquittal. In your case, the local council can serve as the auspicing body, provided they issue a formal letter of support confirming that they will hold the grant funds, manage reporting, and maintain the required insurances for the project. The council must also be named as the applicant on the application form.

**2. Grant size**  
For the current round, the minimum award is **$5,000** and the maximum is **$50,000** (GST exclusive). Applications are assessed on a competitive basis, and the requested amount should be commensurate with the scope of works and the demonstrated need. Given your projected turnover of approximately $40,000, a request in the mid‑range (e.g., $15,000–$25,000) would be viewed favorably if it aligns with a detailed budget.

**3. Eligible costs – infrastructure**  
Capital works such as rainwater tanks and shade structures are eligible **provided** they are directly linked to the grant’s objectives of enhancing community resilience to climate impacts (e.g., reducing heat stress, securing water supply). The costs must be itemised, justified, and supported by written quotes or tenders. Ongoing operational expenses (e.g., stall supplies, membership administration) are not eligible unless they are demonstrably necessary for the delivery of the funded infrastructure (e.g., installation labour, project management).

**4. Supporting documentation**  
To strengthen your application, please prepare the following:

- **Auspice letter** from the local council on official letterhead, stating their agreement to administer the grant, accept financial responsibility, and maintain relevant insurances.  
- **Proof of council’s eligibility** (e.g., ABN, confirmation of local government status).  
- **Project plan** outlining the need, objectives, timeline, and expected resilience outcomes.  
- **Detailed budget** with itemised costs, including at least two written quotes for each major component (tanks, shade structure, foundations, labour).  
- **Financial statements** for the garden (even if informal) showing the $40,000 turnover and sources of income, to demonstrate fiscal capacity.  
- **Risk management and safety plan** (especially for any construction work).  
- **Letters of support** from community members, local schools, or other stakeholders illustrating broad backing and anticipated benefit.  
- **Evidence of any existing insurance** held by the council that covers volunteers and public liability for the site.

Once these documents are assembled, you can proceed to the online application portal, selecting the council as the applicant and uploading the auspice letter as part of the eligibility checklist.
