Subject: Re: Question about eligibility for the Community Resilience Grant

Dear Jamie,

Thank you for your enquiry regarding the Community Resilience Grant round. I appreciate you reaching out before investing time in a full application, and I am happy to address each of your questions in turn.

First, regarding organisational structure: unincorporated groups operating under the auspices of a local government authority are eligible to apply, provided the auspicing body (in this case, your local council) is a legal entity that can enter into a funding agreement on your behalf. The council would need to sign the grant agreement, assume legal and financial responsibility for the project, and provide a letter of support confirming their willingness to auspice the grant, including evidence of their current public liability insurance and ABN.

Second, the current grant round offers funding between $5,000 and $50,000 per project. There is no minimum project budget, but the requested amount must fall within this range. Given your described project scope, it appears you would be well within these parameters.

Third, capital infrastructure such as rainwater tanks and permanent shade structures is considered an eligible cost under the "Community Infrastructure and Climate Adaptation" stream, provided the assets are fixed, remain the property of the auspicing organisation or the community group, and demonstrate a clear resilience benefit. Installation costs, engineering certification (if required by council), and associated plumbing or electrical work are also eligible. However, ongoing maintenance costs, consumables, and operational expenses (such as stall supplies or volunteer reimbursements) are not funded through this grant.

Finally, to support your application, you should prepare: (1) a letter of auspice from the council on official letterhead; (2) the council’s Certificate of Currency for public liability insurance; (3) a detailed project budget with at least two quotes for each major cost item; (4) a project plan with timelines and risk management strategies; (5) evidence of community need and support (letters from members, local schools, or health services); and (6) your group’s most recent financial statement or treasurer’s report showing the $40,000 turnover. If your group has a constitution, rules, or terms of reference—even informally documented—please include those as well.

I encourage you to review the full Program Guidelines and Application Form on our website, as they contain the assessment criteria and mandatory declarations. The online portal opens two weeks before the closing date, and I recommend starting the application early to allow time for council sign-off. Please do not hesitate to contact me if you require further clarification as you prepare your submission.
