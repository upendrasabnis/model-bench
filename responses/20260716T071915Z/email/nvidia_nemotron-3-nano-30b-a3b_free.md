Thank you for reaching out and for the detailed description of your community‑garden project. I have reviewed the eligibility criteria for the current Community Resilience Grant round and am pleased to provide the following guidance to help you prepare a strong application.

**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our funding rules allow organisations that are *not* incorporated societies or charities to apply, provided they are formally *auspiced* by a recognized public‑sector entity such as a local council. The council must hold the appropriate insurance and be listed as the primary *responsible entity* in the application. Since your garden operates under the council’s auspices and the council retains the necessary coverage, you meet this requirement.

**2. Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The current round offers grants ranging from **$5,000 to $25,000**. Projects that demonstrate a clear community‑wide impact and a realistic budget are encouraged to apply for the upper tier, but any amount within the stated range is permissible. There is no separate “minimum” threshold beyond the $5,000 floor.

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs (rainwater tanks, shade structures)**  
Infrastructure that enhances the resilience of community facilities is an eligible cost category. Both rainwater harvesting systems and shade‑providing structures qualify, provided they are directly related to adapting the garden to climate‑related stresses (e.g., heatwaves, water restrictions). Costs must be *reasonable, demonstrable, and clearly tied to the project’s resilience objectives*.

**4. Supporting documentation to prepare**  
To substantiate your application, please include the following:  

- A copy of the council’s letter confirming the auspicing arrangement and insurance coverage.  
- A detailed project budget that itemises the rainwater tanks, shade structure, associated civil works, and any monitoring or maintenance costs.  
- Evidence of community need, such as demographic data, usage statistics, or letters of support from council members or local residents.  
- A risk‑assessment and climate‑impact analysis that explains how the proposed infrastructure will mitigate identified climate‑related vulnerabilities.  
- Your organisation’s constitution or governance documents (if available) and proof of annual turnover (e.g., audited financial statements or bank statements).
