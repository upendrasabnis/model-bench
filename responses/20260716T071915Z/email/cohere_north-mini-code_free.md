**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our grant program welcomes applications from a broad range of applicants, including unincorporated community groups, provided they are formally endorsed or “auspiced” by a legal entity that can assume financial and legal responsibility for the grant. In your case, having your garden operate under the auspices of your local council satisfies this requirement, as the council can act as the primary applicant and will hold the requisite insurances. It is not necessary for the garden itself to be a registered charity, but we do require a signed letter of support from the council confirming that it will oversee the project, manage any funds awarded, and be accountable for the outcomes.

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The Community Resilience Grant does not prescribe a rigid floor or ceiling; instead, awards are determined by the scope, impact, and budget of each proposal. Historically, grants in this round have ranged from approximately $10,000 to $75,000, depending on the complexity of the infrastructure and the projected community benefit. Your project’s focus on water‑saving tanks and a shade structure—key components for climate‑resilient gardening—fits well within this range. If you anticipate total project costs of around $45,000, a grant request of $35,000–$40,000 would be a reasonable proportion to propose, leaving the remaining balance to be covered by in‑kind contributions or other funding sources.

**Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Yes, the types of infrastructure you describe are fully eligible under the “Climate Adaptation and Resilience” category of the grant. Specifically, the following costs are covered:  
- Purchase and installation of rainwater harvesting systems (tanks, gutters, pumps, and filtration where required)  
- Design, construction, and materials for shade structures (e.g., pergolas, shade cloths, or permanent shelters)  
- Site preparation, earthworks, and any necessary groundwork to support the installations  

We also allow modest ancillary expenses such as project management fees, community outreach, and monitoring & evaluation activities, provided they are justified in the budget.
