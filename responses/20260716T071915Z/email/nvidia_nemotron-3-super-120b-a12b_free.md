Dear Jamie,

Thank you for your interest in the Community Resilience Grant and for taking the time to outline your garden’s circumstances and proposed project. I am pleased to provide the information you requested to help you decide whether to proceed with a full application.

**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
The grant program expressly allows applications from not‑for‑profit organisations that are either incorporated associations, registered charities, or unincorporated groups operating under an auspicing arrangement with a local government authority. Your situation—being volunteer‑run, having an annual turnover of approximately $40,000, and being auspiced by the regional council that holds the necessary public liability and volunteer insurance—meets the auspice‑eligibility criteria. The council will need to provide a signed Auspice Agreement confirming its willingness to administer the grant funds on your behalf and to maintain the required insurances for the duration of the project.

**Grant size**  
For the current funding round, the minimum award is $5,000 and the maximum award is $30,000 (GST‑exclusive). Applications are assessed on a competitive basis, and the amount awarded will reflect the scope, demonstrated need, and budget realism of the proposed activities. Your outlined budget for rainwater tanks and a shade structure would comfortably fall within this range, provided you can show detailed cost estimates and any co‑contributions or in‑kind support.

**Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Capital works that directly enhance community resilience—such as water‑harvesting systems, shade structures, and other climate‑adaptation improvements—are explicitly listed as eligible expenditures. Both the purchase and installation of rainwater tanks and the erection of a shade structure are therefore permissible, as long as the items are new, not previously funded by another government program, and are essential to achieving the project’s resilience outcomes.

**Supporting documentation to prepare**  
To substantiate your application, please gather the following items:

1. **Auspice Agreement** – a signed letter from the regional council confirming its auspice role, insurance coverage, and willingness to manage the grant funds.  
2. **Proof of nonprofit status** – although you are not a registered charity, provide any governing documents (e.g., a memorandum of understanding with the council, meeting minutes showing volunteer governance, or a constitution).  
3. **Financial statements** – a recent profit‑and‑loss statement or cash‑flow summary demonstrating the ≈ $40,000 annual turnover and the sources of income (produce stall, membership fees).  
4. **Detailed project budget** – itemised quotes for the rainwater tanks (including capacity, fittings, and installation) and the shade structure (materials, labour, engineering certification if required). Include any anticipated co‑funding or volunteer labour valued at market rates.  
5. **Project plan and timeline** – a brief narrative outlining the need (projected temperature increases, current water‑usage challenges), objectives, implementation steps, responsible parties, and a schedule showing completion within the grant period (typically 12 months).  
6. **Letters of support** – optional but recommended; statements from council officers, local residents, or partner organisations endorsing the project’s community benefit.  
7. **Insurance certificates** – copies of the public liability and volunteer insurance policies held by the council, confirming coverage for the proposed works.

Once you have assembled these documents, you can proceed to the online application portal. Should you encounter any difficulties or require clarification on specific budget lines, please feel free to contact me directly at [grants@agency.gov.au] or call (02) 1234 5678.
