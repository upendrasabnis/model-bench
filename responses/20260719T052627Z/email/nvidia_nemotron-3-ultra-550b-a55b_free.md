**Eligibility of Auspiced Groups**  
Unincorporated groups that operate under a formal auspicing arrangement with an eligible legal entity—such as a local council—are eligible to apply, provided the auspicing organisation meets the program’s eligibility criteria (e.g., it is an incorporated not‑for‑profit, local government body, or other approved entity). The council must sign the grant agreement on your behalf and assume legal and financial responsibility for the funded activities. Please ensure you have a written auspicing agreement that clearly outlines the roles, responsibilities, and insurance coverage; a copy of this agreement will be required with your application.

**Grant Amount Limits**  
For this round, the Community Resilience Grant offers a minimum request of $5,000 and a maximum of $50,000 per project. Your proposed budget for rainwater tanks and a shade structure should fall within this range. If the total project cost exceeds $50,000, you may still apply for the maximum grant amount and demonstrate how the remaining funds will be sourced (e.g., in‑kind contributions, other grants, or community fundraising).

**Eligible Infrastructure Costs**  
Capital infrastructure that directly supports community resilience—such as rainwater harvesting systems and shade structures to mitigate heat impacts—is considered an eligible expenditure. Eligible costs include purchase, delivery, installation, and any necessary site preparation. Ongoing maintenance costs, however, are not funded under this grant. Please provide itemised quotes from at least two suppliers for each major component to demonstrate value for money.

**Supporting Documents to Prepare**  
To streamline your application, please gather the following:
1. A signed auspicing agreement with the council, including their ABN and insurance certificates.
2. Your group’s most recent financial statement (or a simple income/expenditure summary for the last 12 months).
3. A project plan outlining objectives, timeline, risk management, and community benefit.
4. Itemised quotes for the rainwater tanks and shade structure.
5. Letters of support from the council and at least two community stakeholders.
6. Evidence of land tenure or permission to install infrastructure on the garden site.
