Dear Jamie,  

Thank you for reaching out and for your interest in the Community Resilience Grant. I have reviewed the details of your project and am pleased to provide clarification on the points you raised.  

**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
The Community Resilience Grant is open to unincorporated community groups that are formally auspiced by a local council, provided the council’s insurances and financial oversight arrangements are documented in the application. Your arrangement—operating under the council’s umbrella with the council holding the necessary public liability coverage—meets this requirement. However, the application must include a written confirmation from the council that it is acting as your auspicing body, together with a copy of the council’s insurance schedule covering your activities.  

**2. Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The current funding round offers grants ranging from **$5,000 to $50,000**. Applications that request less than $5,000 are not considered, while requests exceeding $50,000 are ineligible for this round. Your proposed project, which involves the installation of rain‑water tanks and a shade structure, is well‑suited to the $10,000–$30,000 bracket typically awarded for small‑scale infrastructure upgrades.  

**3. Eligibility of the proposed infrastructure**  
Infrastructure that enhances the long‑term operational capacity of a community‑run facility—such as water‑saving hardware, shade structures, or other built‑environment improvements—is expressly eligible under the “Resilience‑building Infrastructure” category. The key criteria are that the works must be:  

- Directly related to climate‑resilience or sustainability,  
- Clearly defined with a detailed scope and cost breakdown, and  
- Completed within the grant‑funded timeframe (typically 12–18 months).  

Your rain‑water tanks and shade structure satisfy these conditions, as they will improve water security and protect volunteers and garden beds from extreme heat.  

**4. Supporting documentation to prepare**  
To submit a complete application, please include the following:  

- A signed **Letter of Confirmation** from the council confirming its role as the auspicing body and outlining the insurance coverage.  
- A **Project Plan** (maximum two pages) that outlines objectives, timelines, and expected outcomes.  
- A **Detailed Budget** showing line‑item costs for the tanks, shade structure, installation, and any associated engineering or design fees.  
- **Quotes or tenders** from at least two qualified suppliers or contractors for the major works.  
- Evidence of **ongoing community engagement**, such as letters of support from local residents, schools, or neighbouring businesses.  
- Your most recent **financial statements** or a summary of income and expenditure (the $40,000 turnover figure you mentioned will be useful).  
- A **risk‑management plan** that identifies potential hazards (e.g., extreme weather) and the mitigation measures you will employ.
