**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
The grant is open to a broad spectrum of community‑based organisations, and we expressly permit unincorporated groups that operate under the auspices of a local government body. In such cases, the council acts as the primary legal entity for the purposes of contracting, insurance and financial accountability. Because your garden is already supported by the council’s insurance and administrative frameworks, you meet the organisational eligibility requirement. You will, however, need to provide a formal letter of support (or auspice agreement) from the council confirming that they will be the contracting party for the grant funds.

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
Our grant program does not prescribe a strict minimum or maximum award; rather, the funded amount is determined on the basis of the overall project scope, demonstrated community benefit, and the proportion of eligible costs that the applicant can realistically deliver. Typical awards for infrastructure projects of the type you describe range from $15,000 to $75,000, but applications that present a compelling case for larger investments are also considered. Please note that the total grant cannot exceed 100 % of the eligible project costs.

**Eligibility of rainwater tanks and shade structures**  
Yes – both rainwater harvesting equipment and permanent shade structures are considered eligible capital expenses under the “Climate‑Resilient Infrastructure” component of the grant. Eligible costs must be directly attributable to the project, be necessary for its operation, and have a useful life of at least five years. We encourage you to include detailed specifications, quotations, and installation plans for these items in your budget.

**Supporting documents to prepare**  
To strengthen your application, we recommend assembling the following documentation:

1. **Project Narrative** – a concise description of the problem (e.g., heat stress impacts on garden productivity), the proposed solution, expected outcomes, and a timeline with milestones.  
2. **Budget and Finance Sheet** – a line‑item breakdown of all costs, including equipment quotations, installation labour, contingency, and any in‑kind contributions.  
3. **Letter of Support / Auspice Agreement** – an official letter from your local council confirming that they will act as the contracting entity, hold insurance, and oversee financial reporting.  
4. **Organizational Profile** – a short overview of your group’s volunteer composition, governance structure, and any past community projects (even if unincorporated).  
5. **Site Plans / Drawings** – scaled diagrams showing the placement of rainwater tanks, shade structure, and any necessary site modifications.  
6. **Risk Assessment and Safety Plan** – documentation that demonstrates how you will manage installation safety, maintenance, and emergency procedures.  
7. **Financial Statements** – the most recent audited or reviewed financial statements (or, for a small operation, a simple profit‑and‑loss statement and cash‑flow summary) covering the period that includes your $40,000 annual turnover.  
8. **Letters of Commitment** – any pledged contributions from local businesses, community members, or other partners.  

We also advise you to review the full grant guidelines (available on our website) for any additional requirements, such as environmental impact assessments or compliance with local planning regulations.
