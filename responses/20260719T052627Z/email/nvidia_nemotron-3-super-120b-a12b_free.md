Dear Jamie,

Thank you for reaching out and for your interest in the Community Resilience Grant program. I appreciate the thoughtful work you and your volunteers are doing to strengthen your community garden, and I’m happy to provide the clarification you need before you begin preparing a full application.

**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
The grant guidelines expressly allow applications from unincorporated community groups that are auspiced by an eligible local government entity, such as a municipal council. As long as the council signs the auspice agreement and accepts responsibility for the grant funds, reporting, and any associated legal obligations, your garden can proceed with an application under its auspice. Please ensure the auspice letter is on official council letterhead, signed by an authorised officer (e.g., the Community Services Manager or equivalent), and includes a statement confirming the council’s willingness to administer the grant on your behalf.

**Grant amount range**  
For the current funding round, the minimum award is $5,000 and the maximum award is $50,000 (GST‑exclusive). Applications are assessed on a competitive basis, and the requested amount should be commensurate with the scope and expected impact of the proposed activities. Given your estimated turnover of approximately $40,000, a request in the $15,000–$30,000 range would be typical for a project of the scale you described, but you are free to request any amount within the stated limits that reflects your actual budget.

**Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Capital works that enhance the resilience of community facilities—such as the installation of rainwater harvesting tanks and shade structures—are explicitly listed as eligible expenses under the “Infrastructure Improvement” category. Costs may include the purchase of materials, professional installation, and any necessary engineering or design fees. Ongoing operational expenses (e.g., water usage, routine maintenance) are not covered, but you may include a modest contingency (up to 10 % of the total infrastructure budget) to address unforeseen site‑specific conditions.

**Supporting documentation to prepare**  
To strengthen your application, please have the following ready for upload with your online submission:

1. **Auspice agreement** – a signed letter from the local council confirming its auspice role, including contact details of the council officer responsible for grant administration.  
2. **Project plan** – a brief (one‑ to two‑page) description outlining the objectives, activities, timeline, and how the rainwater tanks and shade structure will increase resilience to hot summers.  
3. **Detailed budget** – an itemised spreadsheet showing quantities, unit costs, and total costs for each component (tanks, pumps, fittings, shade structure, labour, design, contingency). Clearly indicate which items are grant‑funded and which, if any, are contributed in‑kind or by other sources.  
4. **Evidence of community support** – letters of support from at least two local stakeholders (e.g., neighbouring residents, a local school, or the council’s community development officer) and/or photographs showing current site conditions and the proposed installation locations.  
5. **Organisational information** – a short profile of your volunteer group (mission, regular activities, membership numbers) and a copy of any existing insurance certificates held by the council that cover the garden site.  
6. **Financial statements** – although you are not a registered charity, a simple profit‑and‑loss statement or bank statement showing the $40,000 annual turnover helps demonstrate financial viability.
