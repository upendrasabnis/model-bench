**Eligibility of groups auspiced by a council**  
Our funding guidelines expressly include “organizations operating under the auspices of a local government authority” as eligible applicants, provided they can demonstrate a formal arrangement (e.g., a memorandum of understanding or service agreement) that confirms the council’s role in administering insurance, financial oversight, and compliance obligations. Because your garden is formally recognised by the council and the council holds the necessary public liability and workers‑compensation insurances on your behalf, you meet this criterion.  

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The current round offers grants ranging from **$5,000 to $50,000**. Projects that request less than $5,000 are ineligible, while applications exceeding $50,000 will not be considered for funding in this cycle. Your proposed work—installing rainwater tanks and a shade structure—fits comfortably within the $5,000–$50,000 bracket, assuming the total project cost falls within that range.  

**Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Infrastructure that directly supports climate‑resilience outcomes is an eligible cost category. This includes, but is not limited to, water capture and storage systems (e.g., rainwater tanks), shading structures, solar panels, and other physical assets that enhance the organisation’s ability to continue operations under adverse environmental conditions. The key requirement is that the infrastructure must be **new, purpose‑built, and demonstrably linked to resilience objectives**; routine maintenance or standard landscaping does not qualify.  

**Supporting documentation you should prepare**  
To substantiate your eligibility and the project’s merit, we recommend the following documents:  

1. **Letter of endorsement** from the sponsoring council confirming the auspice arrangement, insurance coverage, and any financial oversight responsibilities.  
2. **Project budget** outlining all anticipated expenditures, clearly distinguishing eligible infrastructure costs from non‑eligible items.  
3. **Detailed work plan** (including timelines, milestones, and responsible parties) that shows how the rainwater tanks and shade structure will be procured, installed, and commissioned.  
4. **Risk assessment and mitigation strategy** addressing potential implementation risks, especially those related to weather events and project safety.  
5. **Evidence of community benefit**, such as letters of support from local residents, council community services, or partner organisations, to demonstrate the broader impact of the resilience work.  
6. **Copy of your most recent financial statements** (or a summary of income and expenditure) to verify the $40,000 annual turnover figure.
