Dear Jamie,

Thank you for contacting the Grants Administration Team and for the interest you and your volunteers are showing in the Community Resilience Grant (CRG). I’m happy to address each of the points you raised so that you can decide whether to proceed with a full application.

**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
The CRG is open to a range of community‑based organisations, including unincorporated groups, provided they can demonstrate a formal relationship with a recognised public sector body that can act as a guarantor for the grant. Because your garden operates under the auspices of the local council and the council holds the required public liability and workers’ compensation insurances on your behalf, you meet the primary eligibility criterion for unincorporated entities. When you submit the application you will need to include a signed letter of support from the council confirming:  
- the nature of the relationship (e.g., “host” arrangement);  
- the council’s willingness to act as the “grant‑holding entity” for the duration of the project; and  
- confirmation that the council’s insurance policies cover the garden’s activities.

**2. Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
For this funding round the grant size ranges from **$5,000 to $50,000** per project. The amount awarded is proportionate to the total eligible costs and the demonstrated impact on community resilience. Given your projected budget for rainwater tanks and a shade structure, you are comfortably within the ceiling. If your total eligible costs exceed $50,000, you may consider splitting the project into two phases or seeking complementary funding from other sources.

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Capital items that directly contribute to the garden’s ability to operate under adverse weather conditions are expressly eligible under the “Infrastructure and Asset Development” category. This includes:  

- Rainwater harvesting tanks (including delivery, installation and commissioning);  
- Shade structures (e.g., pergolas, shade sails, or modular roofing) that are permanently fixed to the site; and  
- Associated civil works such as foundations, drainage and site preparation.  

Please note that any items classified as “luxury” or “non‑essential aesthetic upgrades” would be excluded. All infrastructure costs must be supported by itemised quotations from licensed suppliers.

**4. Supporting documentation**  
To streamline the assessment process, we recommend preparing the following documents before you begin the online application:  

1. **Council Support Letter** (as described above).  
2. **Proof of identity and authority** – a copy of your volunteer coordinator’s photo ID and a signed declaration that you have authority to act on behalf of the garden.  
3. **Financial statements** – your most recent audited or reviewed financial report (or a simple cash‑flow statement) showing the $40,000 turnover and sources of income.  
4. **Project Business Case** – a brief (1–2 page) description of the problem (heat stress, water scarcity), the proposed solution, expected outcomes, and how you will measure success (e.g., increased planting days, water saved).  
5. **Detailed Cost Breakdown** – itemised quotations for the rainwater tanks, shade structure, installation labour, and any professional fees.  
6. **Risk Management Plan** – outlining how you will maintain the new infrastructure, including routine inspections and who will be responsible for any future repairs.  
7. **Insurance Certificate** – a copy of the council’s public liability and workers’ compensation certificates that reference your garden as an insured activity.

Once you have these documents ready, the online system will allow you to upload them directly to the application portal.
