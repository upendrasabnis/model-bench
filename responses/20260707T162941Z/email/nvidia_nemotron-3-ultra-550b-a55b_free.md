Subject: Re: Question about eligibility for the Community Resilience Grant

Dear Jamie,

Thank you for your enquiry regarding the Community Resilience Grant round. I appreciate you reaching out before investing time in a full application, and I am pleased to provide clarification on each of your questions.

First, regarding organisational structure: unincorporated groups operating under a formal auspicing arrangement with a local government authority are absolutely eligible to apply. The auspicing body—in your case, the local council—must be a legally constituted entity (such as an incorporated association, company limited by guarantee, or statutory authority) that holds appropriate public liability insurance and agrees to accept legal and financial responsibility for the grant. You will need to provide a signed auspicing agreement or letter of support from the council confirming this arrangement, along with the council’s ABN and insurance certificates of currency.

Second, the grant round offers funding between $5,000 and $50,000 per project. There is no requirement to request the maximum amount; we encourage applicants to apply for the precise sum needed to deliver the proposed activities. Your project budget should clearly itemise all costs, including any in-kind contributions or volunteer labour valued at current market rates.

Third, capital infrastructure such as rainwater tanks and permanent shade structures is considered an eligible cost, provided the assets are directly linked to the project’s resilience outcomes and remain the property of the auspicing organisation or the community group for the duration of their useful life. Please note that routine maintenance, operational expenses, and consumables are generally not funded unless they are essential to the initial installation and commissioning phase.

Finally, in addition to the auspicing documentation mentioned above, you should prepare: a detailed project plan with timeline and risk management strategy; a fully costed budget with at least two quotes for major expenditure items; evidence of land tenure or permission to install infrastructure on the site; your group’s most recent financial statement (even if unaudited); and letters of support from key stakeholders or partner organisations. The application portal also requires a brief project summary, outcomes framework, and sustainability plan demonstrating how the garden will maintain the assets post-grant.

I trust this information assists your planning. Please do not hesitate to contact me if you require further guidance as you develop your application.
