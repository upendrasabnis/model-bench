Dear Jamie,

Thank you for reaching out and for your interest in the Community Resilience Grant (CRG). I appreciate the time you are taking to verify eligibility before preparing a full application, and I am happy to address each of the points you raised.

**Auspice arrangements**  
The CRG expressly permits unincorporated community groups to apply when they are auspiced by an eligible legal entity, such as a local council, incorporated association, or registered charity. Your council’s formal auspice letter—detailing that the council will act as the legal and financial sponsor for the project, hold the necessary insurances, and assume responsibility for acquittal—satisfies this requirement. Please ensure the letter is on council letterhead, signed by an authorised officer, and includes the council’s ABN.

**Grant size**  
For the current funding round, the minimum grant amount is $5,000 and the maximum is $50,000 (GST‑exclusive). Applications requesting amounts outside this range will be deemed ineligible. Given your project scope, a request in the $15,000–$30,000 bracket would be typical, but you should align the budget with the actual costs of the rain‑water tanks and shade structure.

**Eligible costs**  
Capital infrastructure that directly enhances community resilience—such as rainwater harvesting systems, shade structures, solar‑powered irrigation, and related site works—is an eligible expense. The grant can cover the purchase and installation of the tanks and shade structure, associated engineering or design fees, and necessary permits. Ongoing operational costs (e.g., staff wages, routine maintenance) are not fundable, though a modest contingency (up to 10 % of the total project cost) for unforeseen installation expenses may be included.

**Supporting documentation**  
To strengthen your application, please prepare the following items:

1. **Auspice letter** from the local council (as described above).  
2. **Proof of ABN/ACN** for the council (or a copy of its certificate of incorporation).  
3. **Recent financial statement** (e.g., a simple income‑and‑expenditure summary) showing the $40,000 annual turnover and confirming that the group is not profit‑distributing.  
4. **Project plan** outlining the objectives, timeline, milestones, and how the infrastructure will improve resilience to heat and water scarcity.  
5. **Detailed budget** with itemised quotes (or supplier estimates) for the rainwater tanks, shade structure, foundations, plumbing, and any required council permits.  
6. **Letters of support** from at least two community stakeholders (e.g., a local school, neighbourhood association, or council representative) attesting to the garden’s value and the need for the proposed upgrades.  
7. **Risk management statement** describing any health‑and‑safety considerations during installation and how they will be mitigated.

Once you have assembled these documents, you can proceed to the online application portal. Should you encounter any technical difficulties or need clarification on specific budget lines, please do not hesitate to contact me directly at grants@agency.gov.au or on 02 1234 5678.

We look forward to the possibility of supporting your garden’s efforts to build resilience against a changing climate.
