## 3.  Stage‑by‑Stage Deep Dive

### 3.1. Application Intake

#### 3.1.1. Traditional Workflow
1. **Emailrance**: Applicants send PDFs to a generic inbox.  
2. **Manual Capture**: Staff manually copy data into a spreadsheet.  
3. **Duplicate Efforts**: Applicants re‑enter data for subsequent rounds.

#### 3.1.2. Software‑Driven Workflow
| Feature | How It Works | Benefit |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| **Unified Application Portal** | Applicants log in via SSO, fill a digital form, upload attachments. | Reduces form fatigue, improves data quality. |
| **Form Builder & Conditional Logic** | Drag‑and‑drop fields, “show next question if X is true.” | Keeps forms concise, ensures eligibility filtering early. |
| **Document Parsing & OCR** | AI extracts key fields from PDFs/Word files. | Eliminates manual চেয়ারম্যান, speeds up data entry. |
| **Applicant Dashboard** | Applicants see status, pending items, and can edit. | Enhances transparency, reduces support tickets. |
| **Duplicate Detection** | Cross‑checks applicant ID, email, or organization name. | Cuts down on repeated submissions. |

#### 3.1.3. Example: *Fluxx Grant Lifecycle*
- **Scenario**: A mid‑size foundation receives 3,000 applications per cycle.  
- **Result**: Document parsing cut manual data entry by **70%**, and the portal’s conditional logic flagged 15% of ineligible proposals before submission, saving reviewers hours.

- **Result**: Scoring engine standardized decisions, reducing variance by **25%**. The audit trail helped meet SOX compliance.
