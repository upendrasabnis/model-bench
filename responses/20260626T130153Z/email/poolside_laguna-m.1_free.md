1. **Regarding the Use of Actual Overhead (12.3%):**  
   In the absence of a federally negotiated indirect cost rate, your organization must adhere to the 10% default rate specified in the grant guidelines. The 12.3% actual overhead figure from your audited financial statements cannot be applied directly unless it has been formally negotiated and approved by the federal agency. This ensures consistency with federal regulations and equitable treatment across all applicants. However, if you can provide sufficient documentation to justify a higher rate (as outlined below), you may request provisional approval, subject to final negotiation before the award is executed.

2. **Required Documentation for a Higher Rate:**  
   To substantiate a request for a rate higher than the default 10%, you would need to submit the following:  
   - A **cost allocation plan** detailing how your actual overhead costs are distributed across projects.  
   - A **supplemental audit** or financial documentation demonstrating that the 12.3% overhead aligns with modified total direct costs (MTDC) as defined in the grant guidelines.  
   - A **justification letter** explaining why the 10% rate is insufficient to cover your organization’s legitimate indirect costs and how the proposed rate reflects your actual operations.  
   - Evidence of any prior federal or state indirect cost rate negotiations, even if not federally approved, to support your methodology.  

3. **Additional Steps Before Submission:**  
   Before finalizing your application, ensure that your budget narrative explicitly states that the indirect costs are calculated using the 10% default rate. If time permits, consider reaching out to the federal agency’s grants office to discuss your organization’s eligibility for expedited rate negotiation.

   Yes, you may submit an amendment to update your indirect cost rate if a federally negotiated rate is obtained before the award is finalized. To avoid delays, I recommend initiating the negotiation process as early as possible and coordinating closely with the grants office to align timelines.
