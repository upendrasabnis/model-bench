Dear Ms. Patel,

Thank you for your detailed inquiry regarding the indirect‑cost provisions for the FY2027 Federal Community Health Services Grant (CHS‑2027‑08). I have addressed each of your questions below, referencing the applicable Uniform Guidance (2 CFR §200.414) and the specific language in the grant announcement.

**1. Allowable indirect‑cost rate**  
In the absence of a federally negotiated indirect‑cost rate agreement (ICRA), the regulation permits a *de minimis* rate of **10 % of modified total direct costs (MTDC)**. The 12.3 % actual overhead ratio reflected in your audited financial statements may not be used as the claimed rate unless you first obtain a negotiated rate through the indirect‑cost rate proposal process. Therefore, for this application you must adhere to the 10 % default rate unless a formal ICRA is in place at the time of award.

**2. Documentation required to support a higher rate**  
If you wish to pursue a rate above the de minimis 10 %, you will need to submit an Indirect Cost Rate Proposal (ICRP) to your cognizant federal agency (typically the Department of Health and Human Services’ Division of Cost Allocation). The proposal should include:  

* A documented cost allocation plan that clearly separates direct and indirect costs and shows how the indirect pool is derived.  
* The most recent audited financial statements (as you already have) and a reconciliation of the indirect pool to those statements.  
* A justification narrative explaining why the negotiated rate is appropriate for your organization’s cost structure.  
* Any supporting schedules (e.g., fringe‑benefit rates, facility costs) and a signed certification from an authorized official.

Only after the cognizant agency approves the ICRP and issues a negotiated rate may you apply that rate to the MTDC in your budget.

**3. Pre‑submission compliance steps**  
Before finalizing the budget section of your application, please verify the following:  

* Calculate MTDC by excluding equipment, capital expenditures, patient care revenues, subcontracts exceeding $25,000, and other items specified in the Uniform Guidance.  
* Apply the 10 % de minimis rate (or your approved negotiated rate, if already in place) to the MTDC figure.  
* Ensure the budget narrative explicitly states the indirect‑cost rate used and cites the governing authority (e.g., “10 % de minimis rate per 2 CFR §200.414(f)”).  
* Attach a copy of your current indirect‑cost rate agreement (if any) or a statement confirming that no negotiated rate exists and that the de minimis rate is being applied.

**4. Post‑submission amendment possibilities**  
Should you obtain a negotiated indirect‑cost rate after the initial submission but before the award is finalized, you may submit a revised budget as a *budget amendment* in accordance with the agency’s pre‑award revision procedures. Most federal grant‑making offices allow budget revisions prior to award, provided the change does not alter the scope of work or the total amount requested. You would need to:  

* Notify the grants officer in writing, attaching the newly approved ICRA.  
* Submit an updated SF‑424 budget form and revised budget justification reflecting the new rate.  
* Await written confirmation that the amendment has been accepted; do not assume the change is effective until you receive that acknowledgment.
