To address your specific inquiries regarding the indirect cost rate for Reference: CHS-2027-08, please see our guidance below:

**1. Allowable Indirect Cost Rate**
Per the grant guidelines and standard federal cost principles (2 CFR § 200), in the absence of a federally negotiated indirect cost rate agreement (NICRA), applicants are restricted to the de minimis rate. While we acknowledge that your audited financial statements reflect an actual overhead expense ratio of 12.3%, you must adhere strictly to the 10% rate of modified total direct costs (MTDC). Federal regulations do not permit the use of an organization's actual overhead percentage as a substitute for a negotiated rate; the 10% rate is a fixed ceiling designed to simplify administration for organizations without a formal NICRA.

**2. Documentation Requirements**
Because the 10% de minimis rate is applied automatically when no NICRA is present, you are not required to submit a cost allocation plan or supplemental audits to justify this specific rate. However, we recommend maintaining your internal cost allocation documentation and audited statements on file should you be selected for an award, as these may be reviewed during a post-award audit to ensure direct costs are properly distinguished from indirect costs.

**3. Compliance Steps for Budget Submission**
Before submitting your budget, please ensure that your indirect costs are calculated based on "Modified Total Direct Costs" (MTDC) rather than total direct costs. This means you must exclude certain items from the base, such as equipment, capital expenditures, rental costs, and the portion of subawards that exceed the specified threshold (typically the first $25,000 of each subaward). Applying the 10% to the wrong base is a common error that can lead to non-compliance.

**4. Amendments and Future Negotiated Rates**
Regarding your question on amendments, should your organization successfully negotiate a NICRA with a cognizant agency after your initial submission but prior to the formal award announcement, you may request a budget revision. Such requests are evaluated on a case-by-case basis and are subject to the discretion of the awarding officer. We recommend notifying your assigned Program Officer as soon as a formal agreement is executed.
