Subject: Re: Clarification Needed on Indirect Cost Rate Allocation for FY2027 Community Health Services Grant  

Dear Ms. Patel,

Thank you for your detailed inquiry regarding the allowable indirect cost rate for your upcoming FY2027 Federal Community Health Services Grant (CHS‑2027‑08). I appreciate the thoroughness of your budgeting approach and am pleased to clarify the relevant policy points.

**1. Use of an actual overhead ratio versus the default 10 % rate**  
The Uniform Guidance (OMB Circular A‑122) permits an organization that lacks a federally negotiated indirect cost rate to apply the “default” rate of 10 % of Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC). However, the guidance also allows the use of an *actual* indirect cost rate when the organization can substantiate that rate with a documented cost‑allocation plan and supporting financial evidence. Consequently, you may propose the 12.3 % overhead ratio you have calculated, provided you attach the requisite documentation and obtain prior approval from the funding agency.

**2. Documentation required to support a higher rate**  
To have the 12.3 % rate accepted, please submit the following items with your budget package:  

- A **Cost Allocation Plan** that delineates how indirect costs are computed, including personnel, facilities, equipment, and administrative expenses.  
- A **Supplemental Financial Statement** (or an updated audited financial report) that reconciles the 12.3 % ratio with the MTDC base used in the calculation.  
- A **Justification Letter** signed by a senior official (e.g., Executive Director or Chief Financial Officer) explaining why the actual ratio is more representative of your organization’s cost structure than the statutory 10 % default.  
- If available, a **Negotiated Indirect Cost Rate Agreement (NICRA)** draft or a statement indicating that a formal negotiation is in progress.

All of these documents should be labeled “Indirect Cost Rate Submission – FY2027” and attached as Appendix X to the budget narrative.

**3. Additional compliance steps before final submission**  
- Ensure the indirect cost rate is entered on the **“Budget – Indirect Costs”** line of the SF‑424 and the accompanying budget spreadsheet, using the MTDC figure that the agency will calculate.  
- Include a **cover sheet** that references the OMB Uniform Guidance citation (2 CFR 200.430) and confirms that the rate has been reviewed and approved by your internal audit function.  
- Verify that the **budget period** and **total award amount** do not exceed the limits specified in the FOA; any deviation may trigger a compliance review.  
- Finally, retain a copy of all submitted materials for your internal records, as the agency may request them during the award negotiation phase.

**4. Amending the application after submission**  
If you later secure a formally negotiated indirect cost rate before the award is finalized, you may submit a **post‑submission amendment** to the budget section. The amendment must be accompanied by the finalized NICRA (or a letter confirming the negotiated rate) and a brief cover memo stating the purpose of the change. The agency will review the amendment for consistency with the original application and may require a revised budget justification. Amendments received after the award is made are generally not permitted unless a special waiver is granted.
