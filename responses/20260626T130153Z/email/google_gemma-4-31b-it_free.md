To address your first point, because Community Health Horizons does not currently possess a federally negotiated indirect cost rate agreement (NICRA), you must adhere strictly to the 10% *de minimis* rate of modified total direct costs (MTDC). While we acknowledge that your audited financial statements reflect an actual overhead expense ratio of 12.3%, federal guidelines generally do not permit the use of actual historical overhead figures as a substitute for a negotiated rate. Consequently, the 12.3% figure cannot be applied to this proposal; the 10% default rate is the maximum allowable limit in the absence of a NICRA.

Regarding your second question, since the 10% rate is a standard default, no additional documentation—such as a cost allocation plan or supplemental audit—is required to substantiate the use of this rate. However, if you wish to utilize a rate higher than 10% in future applications, I strongly recommend that your organization begin the process of negotiating a NICRA with your cognizant federal agency.

To ensure full compliance before submitting your budget, I suggest reviewing the "Modified Total Direct Costs" definition in the grant guidelines to ensure that excluded costs (such as equipment or tuition) are properly removed from the base before calculating the 10% indirect charge. Ensuring this calculation is precise will prevent any delays during the technical review process.

Finally, regarding your inquiry about amendments: once an application is submitted, the budget is generally locked for the review period. However, if you obtain a negotiated rate before the award is finalized, you may notify the grants management officer during the pre-award negotiation phase. At that time, the Department may allow a budget revision to reflect the new rate, provided the total funding ceiling for the award is not exceeded.
