Below I address each of the four questions you raised and outline the steps you should follow to remain fully compliant with the Department’s indirect‑cost policy.

**1. Applicable indirect‑cost rate in the absence of a federally negotiated rate**  
When an organization does not have a federally negotiated indirect‑cost rate (FIRR), the federal regulations (2 CFR 200.414) require that the indirect cost be limited to the lesser of (a) the rate that has been negotiated with a cognizant federal agency, or (b) the de facto rate of 10 % of Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC). Because CHS‑2027‑08 is a federal award, the 10 % default applies unless you obtain a negotiated rate prior to the award. Consequently, the 12.3 % actual overhead you calculated from your audited statements cannot be applied to this award at this stage.

**2. Possibility of using a higher rate and required documentation**  
If you wish to use a rate higher than 10 %, you must first obtain a FIRR from the Department of Health and Human Services (or the appropriate cognizant agency). The documentation required for that request includes:  

- A completed **Negotiated Indirect Cost Rate Agreement (NICRA) application** (Form F‑424).  
- A **Cost Allocation Plan (CAP)** that details the methodology used to allocate indirect costs to final cost objectives.  
- The most recent **audit report** (the one showing the 12.3 % overhead) to support the reasonableness of the proposed rate.  
- A **justification letter** explaining why the 10 % default would be insufficient to cover your organization’s actual indirect expenses.  

Only after the cognizant agency approves the rate and issues a NICRA may you apply that higher percentage to the grant budget.

**3. Additional steps to ensure compliance before budget submission**  
To avoid any compliance issues, I recommend the following actions before you finalize the budget for the October 15 deadline:  

1. **Calculate MTDC** precisely, excluding items such as equipment, capital expenditures, patient‑care costs, and subawards over $25,000, as defined in the grant guidance.  
2. **Apply the 10 % default rate** to the MTDC and document that this rate is being used because no FIRR exists. Include a brief statement in the budget narrative referencing 2 CFR 200.414.  
3. **Prepare a contingency note** in the narrative indicating that you are in the process of pursuing a negotiated rate and that, should a FIRR be awarded prior to the award notice, an amendment will be submitted to reflect the approved rate.  
4. **Maintain all supporting documents** (audit, financial statements, and any correspondence with the cognizant agency) in your grant file for future audit review.

**4. Submitting an amendment after the initial application**  
Yes, you may submit a post‑submission amendment if you obtain a negotiated rate before the award is finalized. The amendment should be filed through the same electronic portal used for the original application and must include:  

- The approved NICRA and associated CAP.  
- A revised budget showing the new indirect‑cost amount.  
- A concise cover letter explaining that the amendment reflects an updated indirect‑cost rate approved by the cognizant agency.  

The award officer will review the amendment, and, provided the rate has been properly negotiated, the revised indirect‑cost amount will be incorporated into the final award.
