Dear Ms. Patel,

Thank you for sharing the details of your budgeting question and for the thoughtful approach you are taking to ensure full compliance with the FY2027 Federal Community Health Services Grant guidelines. I have reviewed the applicable federal guidance (2 CFR 200, the Uniform Guidance on Grants and Agreements) and the specific language you cited in the solicitation. Below is a point‑by‑point response to each of your inquiries, followed by the actions we recommend to move forward confidently.

**1. Use of the 12.3 % actual overhead figure**  
In the absence of a federally negotiated indirect cost rate, the Uniform Guidance permits institutions to claim indirect costs **only up to the statutory default of 10 % of Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC)**. The 12.3 % figure you have derived from your audited financial statements is therefore **not allowable** for this federal award unless you obtain a negotiated rate that specifically covers the project. The default 10 % cap is a regulatory ceiling; it cannot be exceeded merely because your organization’s historical overhead expenses are higher.

**2. Documentation required to request a higher rate**  
If you wish to pursue a negotiated indirect cost rate that reflects your actual overhead, the standard pathway is to submit a **formal indirect cost rate proposal** to the cognizant federal agency (in this case, the Health Resources and Services Administration, HRSA). Required documentation typically includes:

- **Cost Allocation Plan (CAP)** – a detailed narrative that explains how indirect costs are allocated to each function (research, patient care, administration, etc.) and justifies the proposed rate.
- **Latest audited financial statements** – demonstrating the basis for the overhead percentage and the allocation methodology.
- **Supporting schedules** – such as a breakdown of facility, equipment, administrative, and other indirect cost pools.
- **Management’s discussion and analysis** – a brief justification of why the proposed rate is equitable and consistent with the organization’s cost‑sharing policies.
- **Any prior negotiated rates** (if applicable) to show continuity or changes.

HRSA’s Office of Grants Management will review the proposal, may request an on‑site audit, and will issue a **Negotiated Indirect Cost Rate Agreement** (Form HUD‑2530 or the HRSA equivalent) before the award can be processed.

**3. Additional steps before submitting the budget**  
To ensure compliance, we recommend the following pre‑submission checklist:

1. **Finalize your indirect cost rate** – either lock in the 10 % default (which requires no additional documentation) or complete the negotiation process for a higher rate.
2. **Prepare a concise budget narrative** that clearly states the indirect cost rate you are claiming and references the applicable federal guidance or negotiated agreement.
3. **Attach the supporting documentation** (CAP, audit, etc.) as an exhibit to the budget section of the application, even if the rate is the default 10 % (this demonstrates due diligence).
4. **Obtain internal sign‑off** from your finance department and, if required, your organization’s Business Ethics/Compliance officer.
5. **Verify that any cost‑sharing or matching contributions** are calculated on the correct base (MTDC excludes certain categories such as equipment, subawards, and tuition).

Following these steps will streamline the review process and reduce the risk of a “budgetary noncompliance” finding.

**4. Submitting an amendment after the initial submission**  
Yes, it is possible to submit an amendment to the application **prior to the award’s finalization** if you obtain a negotiated indirect cost rate after the initial submission. However, the amendment must be submitted **within the same application cycle** and must be approved by the program office before the award is issued. The amendment should include:

- A clear statement that the change pertains to the indirect cost rate.
- The new negotiated rate agreement (or a copy of the executed rate agreement).
- A revised budget narrative that reflects the updated rate.
- A brief justification for the change.

Please note that amendments submitted after the award date are generally treated as **post‑award modifications** and may be subject to different regulatory requirements and documentation standards.
