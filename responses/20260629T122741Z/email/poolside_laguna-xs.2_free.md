Thank you for your thoughtful inquiry and for your interest in the Rural Renewable Energy Innovation Grant. I am pleased to provide the following clarifications to assist GreenFields Community Alliance in preparing a compliant and competitive application.

**1. Eligibility of Joint Applications:** The RREI program actively encourages collaborative projects that bring together diverse institutional strengths. Your proposed partnership involving a nonprofit organization, a utility cooperative, and an academic institution is not only permissible but highly valued. In multi-partner proposals, the nonprofit organization must serve as the lead applicant. The utility cooperative and university research lab may participate as sub-recipients or partners, provided they submit commitment letters and demonstrate their capacity to fulfill their respective roles. All partners must satisfy baseline eligibility requirements, including active status in good standing with their respective regulatory bodies.

**2. Co-Funding Requirements:** In-kind contributions are indeed acceptable toward meeting the required 30% co-funding match. This includes donated equipment, volunteer labor with documented market-rate valuations, and the use of existing facilities. All in-kind contributions must be formally documented through appraisals, donation agreements, or time-tracking systems with supervisor verification. These valuations must reflect fair market rates and cannot include items already funded by federal sources. Detailed documentation must be submitted with your application and will be subject to audit throughout the project lifecycle.

**3. Project Duration and Extension Policy:** While the standard project period is 24 months, formal requests for timeline extensions may be submitted for review and approval. Extension requests must be made no later than 90 days prior to the original project end date and should include justification (such as documented permitting delays or supply chain disruptions), a revised timeline, and an updated budget impact assessment. Approval is granted on a case-by-case basis and requires written authorization before any cost incurrence beyond the original timeframe.

**4. Reporting Cadence and Metrics:** Successful RREI recipients typically report quarterly using standardized templates that track energy production metrics, community engagement numbers, and economic impact assessments. Key performance indicators often include kilowatt-hours generated, households served, carbon emissions reduced, and job-years created. Reporting templates and sample KPIs are available on our website and will be provided upon request. We encourage applicants to review previous awardees' public reports for additional insights.
