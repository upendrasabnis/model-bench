Regarding your first point, the RREI program highly encourages multi-sectoral collaboration. Your proposed consortium—comprising a nonprofit, a utility cooperative, and an academic institution—is an ideal structure for this grant, as it demonstrates the cross-disciplinary approach we seek. While all partners are encouraged to participate, the lead applicant must be a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization. The lead applicant will serve as the primary fiscal agent, responsible for all financial management, reporting, and compliance throughout the grant lifecycle.

In response to your question on co-funding, we do allow for a combination of cash and in-kind contributions to meet the 30% match requirement. In-kind contributions, such as donated equipment, professional services, or the use of existing facilities, are acceptable. However, please note that volunteer labor is only eligible if it is provided by individuals whose professional services would otherwise be billed at a market rate. All in-kind contributions must be meticulously documented using our standardized "In-Kind Contribution Valuation Form," which requires a written letter of commitment from the donor and a detailed breakdown of the valuation method used.

Concerning project duration, while the standard period is 24 months, we recognize that external factors like permitting or supply chain disruptions can occur. There is a formal No-Cost Extension (NCE) process. Should you encounter delays, you must submit a written request at least 60 days prior to the current project end date. This request must include a revised project timeline, a justification for the delay, and a detailed plan outlining how the remaining milestones will be achieved within the extended period.

Finally, regarding reporting, successful applicants typically focus on metrics such as kilowatt-hours (kWh) generated, carbon offset tonnage, and the number of rural households served. While we do not provide specific templates in this preliminary stage, all grantees are required to use our centralized Grant Management Portal to submit quarterly progress reports and annual impact statements. These reports must align with the specific Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) outlined in your approved budget and work plan.

I hope this information proves helpful as you structure your application. Please do not hesitate to reach out if you require further assistance.
