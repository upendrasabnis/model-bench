Thank you for your interest in the Rural Renewable Energy Innovation Grant (RREI) and for the clear outline of your questions. I am pleased to provide the detailed information you need so that GreenFields Community Alliance can prepare a competitive and compliant application.

**1. Eligibility of Joint Applications**  
The RREI program explicitly encourages collaborative proposals that bring together nonprofit organizations, utility cooperatives, and academic institutions, provided that the partnership demonstrates a clear division of responsibilities and a shared commitment to the program’s objectives. The lead applicant must be a U.S.‑based 501(c)(3) nonprofit, which GreenFields qualifies as. As lead, you will be responsible for:  

* Submitting the primary application and all required certifications.  
* Managing the award agreement and ensuring that all partners comply with federal and program‑specific regulations.  
* Consolidating financial reporting and serving as the primary point of contact for the RREI Office.  

Both the electric cooperative and the university research lab must submit a signed **Letter of Commitment** that outlines their specific roles, the resources they will contribute, and their agreement to abide by the terms of the grant. These letters become part of the application package (see the “Partner Documentation” section of the application guide).

**2. Co‑Funding Requirements**  
The 30 % match may be satisfied with a combination of cash, in‑kind, and leveraged contributions. Acceptable in‑kind contributions include:  

* Volunteer labor (valued at the prevailing market rate for the skill set).  
* Donated equipment, materials, or software.  
* Use of existing facilities, such as laboratory space or field sites, provided the fair‑market rental value is documented.  

To document in‑kind match, we require a **Co‑Funding Schedule** that lists each contribution, its estimated dollar value, the source, and a supporting certification (e.g., a signed donation receipt, a volunteer hour log, or a written appraisal of equipment). All in‑kind values must be reasonable, verifiable, and consistent with the Department of Energy’s cost‑allowability guidelines (see Attachment A of the RREI handbook).

**3. Project Duration and Extension Policy**  
The baseline project period is 24 months. If unforeseen circumstances such as permitting delays, supply‑chain interruptions, or adverse weather events affect the schedule, you may request a formal extension. The process is as follows:  

1. Submit a **Request for Extension** form at least 30 days before the anticipated end date.  
2. Include a narrative description of the delay, a revised timeline with milestone dates, and any supporting documentation (e.g., correspondence with permitting agencies or supplier lead‑time notices).  
3. Provide a revised budget, if the extension alters cost allocations.  

Extensions may be granted for up to an additional 12 months, subject to fund availability and a determination that the project remains aligned with RREI goals.

**4. Reporting Cadence and Metrics**  
RREI recipients submit quarterly progress reports and a final technical and financial report. The quarterly report consists of:  

* **Performance Metrics** – electricity generated (kWh), percentage of renewable energy in the micro‑grid, number of households served, greenhouse‑gas emissions avoided (CO₂e), and number of jobs created.  
* **Financial Summary** – expenditures to date versus budget, and a statement of any unspent match funds.  
* **Narrative Update** – milestones achieved, challenges encountered, and mitigation actions.  

We provide a **Reporting Template** (Excel for financials, Word for narrative) that previous awardees have used successfully; I have attached the latest version to this email. In addition, we encourage grantees to incorporate a **Monitoring & Evaluation Plan** that outlines data‑collection methods, verification procedures, and stakeholder feedback mechanisms. Examples from the 2023‑2024 cycle are available on our website under “Resources → Grant Recipients.”
