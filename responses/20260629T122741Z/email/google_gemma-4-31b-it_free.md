Subject: Re: Inquiry Regarding Eligibility and Co‑Funding Options for the "Rural Renewable Energy Innovation Grant" (Ref: RREI‑2026‑02)

Dear Mr. Mendoza,

Thank you for your interest in the Rural Renewable Energy Innovation Grant (RREI-2026-02). It is encouraging to see the GreenFields Community Alliance pursuing a multi-sector approach to sustainable energy solutions in the Midwest. We appreciate the opportunity to clarify these requirements to ensure your proposal is both compliant and competitive.

Regarding your first inquiry on **joint applications**, the RREI program strongly encourages consortia that leverage the strengths of diverse partners. A partnership involving a nonprofit, a utility cooperative, and an academic institution is fully permissible and often viewed favorably due to the blend of community outreach, technical infrastructure, and research rigor. Regarding the lead applicant, the organization must be the primary legal entity responsible for the financial management of the grant and the primary point of contact for reporting. We require the lead applicant to provide a signed Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) outlining the roles, responsibilities, and resource contributions of all participating partners.

In response to your question on **co-funding**, we do accept in-kind contributions toward the 30% match. This includes volunteer labor (valued at current fair market rates), donated equipment, and the use of existing facilities. To document these contributions, you must include a detailed "In-Kind Contribution Ledger" in your budget narrative. This ledger should specify the source of the contribution, the method used to determine the valuation, and a supporting letter of commitment from the donor confirming the value of the asset or service.

Regarding **project duration and extensions**, while the standard period is 24 months, we recognize that infrastructure projects are often subject to external delays. A formal request for a No-Cost Extension (NCE) may be submitted to the Grants Management Office. Such requests must be submitted at least 30 days prior to the original end date and must be accompanied by a revised project timeline and a written justification detailing the specific unforeseen circumstances (e.g., permitting delays or supply chain disruptions) that necessitated the extension.

Finally, concerning **reporting cadence and metrics**, successful applicants typically report on a quarterly basis. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) generally focus on energy yield (kWh generated), the number of rural households served, and the reduction in carbon emissions. I have attached our "Standard Reporting Framework" and a sample "Impact Metrics Template" from a previous cycle to assist you in structuring your monitoring and evaluation plan.

I hope this information provides the necessary clarity for your planning process. Please do not hesitate to reach out if you have further questions as you develop your proposal.
