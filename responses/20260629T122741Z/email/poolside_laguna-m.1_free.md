Dear Mr. Mendoza,  

Thank you for your email and for your interest in the Rural Renewable Energy Innovation Grant (RREI). I appreciate the thoroughness of your questions and am happy to provide clarification to support GreenFields Community Alliance in preparing a strong application.  

**1. Eligibility of Joint Applications:**  
The RREI program does permit joint proposals, provided that the **lead applicant is a nonprofit organization** as defined in the eligibility criteria. In your case, GreenFields Community Alliance would serve as the lead, with the electric cooperative and university research lab listed as collaborative partners. All partners must contribute meaningfully to the project, and their roles, responsibilities, and resource commitments must be clearly outlined in the proposal. Please ensure that the cooperative and academic institution provide letters of support detailing their involvement and any financial or technical contributions.  

**2. Co-Funding Requirements:**  
Yes, in-kind contributions are acceptable toward meeting the 30% co-funding match. This includes volunteer labor, donated equipment, and use of existing facilities. However, all in-kind contributions must be **quantified and documented** with fair market valuations or third-party appraisals where applicable. For volunteer labor, a detailed log of hours contributed and the hourly rate used for valuation is required. Donated equipment must include a letter from the donor specifying the item’s value and its intended use. These contributions should be summarized in a dedicated budget section of your proposal.  

**3. Project Duration and Extension Policy:**  
The standard 24-month project period is firm, but extensions may be granted under exceptional circumstances. If delays arise due to permitting or supply chain issues, you would need to submit a **written extension request** to the grants office at least 30 days prior to the project’s end date. This request must include: (a) a detailed explanation of the delay, (b) a revised project timeline, and (c) documentation supporting the cause (e.g., correspondence with permitting agencies or suppliers). Approval is not guaranteed and will depend on the justification provided.  

**4. Reporting Cadence and Metrics:**  
Successful applicants in prior cycles have tracked KPIs such as energy generation capacity (e.g., kilowatt-hours produced), number of households served, community training hours delivered, and carbon emissions reduced. Quarterly progress reports are required, including financial updates and narrative summaries of project milestones. Example templates are available on our website under the RREI program page, and I will also send you a copy directly. For monitoring and evaluation, we strongly recommend aligning your metrics with the grant’s stated outcomes, such as scalability of the micro-grid model and long-term community impact.  

Please don’t hesitate to reach out if you need further guidance or additional resources as you develop your proposal. We look forward to reviewing your application and supporting your innovative work in rural sustainability.
