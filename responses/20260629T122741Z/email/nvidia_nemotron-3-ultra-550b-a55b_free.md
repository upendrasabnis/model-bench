Thank you for your detailed inquiry and for the important work GreenFields Community Alliance is undertaking in the Midwest. We appreciate your interest in the Rural Renewable Energy Innovation Grant (RREI‑2026‑02) and are pleased to provide the clarifications you requested. Below, I address each of your points in turn.

**1. Eligibility of Joint Applications**  
The RREI program explicitly encourages collaborative proposals that leverage complementary expertise. A consortium comprising a nonprofit organization (as lead applicant), an electric cooperative, and a university research lab is fully eligible. The lead applicant must be a tax‑exempt 501(c)(3) entity or a public institution with demonstrated fiscal capacity to manage federal funds. The lead organization will be responsible for all reporting, financial management, and compliance obligations. Each partner should submit a signed Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) outlining roles, resource commitments, and intellectual‑property arrangements. Letters of commitment from the cooperative and the university must accompany the application.

**2. Co‑Funding Requirements**  
The 30 % cost‑share may be satisfied through a combination of cash and in‑kind contributions. Acceptable in‑kind items include volunteer labor (valued at prevailing wage rates for the skill level), donated equipment (supported by a fair‑market‑value appraisal or vendor quote), and use of existing facilities (documented via a rental‑equivalency schedule). All in‑kind contributions must be tracked in a separate cost‑share ledger, supported by timesheets, donation letters, or facility‑use agreements, and made available for audit. The budget narrative should clearly distinguish cash match from in‑kind match.

**3. Project Duration and Extension Policy**  
The standard performance period is 24 months from the award start date. A formal no‑cost extension (NCE) of up to 12 months may be requested once, provided the request is submitted at least 60 days before the current end date. The request must include: (a) a revised project timeline with milestones, (b) a justification detailing the cause of delay (e.g., permitting, supply‑chain disruptions), (c) an updated budget showing no increase in total federal funds, and (d) a certification that the extension will not alter the project’s scope or objectives. Extensions beyond 12 months require prior written approval from the Program Officer and are granted only under exceptional circumstances.

**4. Reporting Cadence and Metrics**  
Successful awardees submit quarterly progress reports (narrative and financial) and a final comprehensive report. Key performance indicators (KPIs) typically include: installed capacity (kW), annual energy production (MWh), greenhouse‑gas reductions (metric tons CO₂e), number of households or farms served, jobs created or retained, and leveraged private investment. We have attached a sample reporting template and a KPI matrix from the RREI‑2024 cycle for your reference. Applicants are encouraged to propose additional project‑specific metrics that align with the program’s logic model.

Please feel free to reach out if you need further guidance on budget preparation, the MOU template, or the online submission portal. We look forward to receiving a strong proposal from GreenFields Community Alliance and its partners.
