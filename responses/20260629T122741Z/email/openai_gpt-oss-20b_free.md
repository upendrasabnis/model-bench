Subject: Re: Inquiry Regarding Eligibility and Co‑Funding Options for the RREI‑2026‑02 Grant  

Dear Mr. Mendoza,

Thank you for reaching out and for your interest in the Rural Renewable Energy Innovation Grant (RREI). I’m pleased to clarify the points you raised so you can structure a compliant and competitive application.

1. **Eligibility of Joint Applications**  
The RREI program explicitly welcomes multi‑sector collaborations. A joint proposal may include a nonprofit, a utility cooperative, and an academic institution, provided that the *Lead Applicant* is a 501(c)(3) or an equivalent nonprofit entity with a valid tax ID. The lead applicant must assume overall responsibility for budget management, reporting, and compliance. All partners must submit a *Collaborator Statement* (Form RREI‑Collab 1) outlining roles, cost‑sharing, and intellectual property arrangements.

2. **Co‑Funding Requirements**  
The 30 % match can be satisfied with **in‑kind contributions**—volunteer labor, donated equipment, or use of existing facilities—provided they are quantifiable and directly linked to the project. Documentation should include:  
   - A signed *Contribution Agreement* from each partner.  
   - A *Labor Log* with hours, staff titles, and cost per hour.  
   - Receipts or vouchers for donated equipment and a *Facility Usage Agreement* indicating capacity and duration.  
   These materials should be submitted in the *Financials* section of the budget narrative.

3. **Project Duration and Extension Policy**  
The standard baseline is 24 months. If unforeseen delays arise, you may request a **project extension** up to an additional 12 months. Submit a *Extension Request Form* (RREI‑Ext) at least 90 days before the current deadline, accompanied by:  
   - A written justification (max 500 words).  
   - Updated milestone schedule.  
   - Revised budget summary if applicable.  
The Grants Office will review the request within 30 days.

4. **Reporting Cadence and Metrics**  
Reporting is quarterly (Month 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21, 24). We provide a *Monitoring & Evaluation Toolkit* (attached) that includes:  
   - **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**  
     - % of projected renewable energy output achieved.  
     - Reduction in grid‑dependent energy consumption (kWh).  
     - Number of community members trained.  
     - Cost per kilowatt‑hour of renewable energy produced.  
   - **Reporting Templates:**  
     - *Quarterly Progress Report* (Form RREI‑QPR).  
     - *Annual Impact Summary* (Form RREI‑AIS).  

These templates have been used successfully by prior recipients in RREI‑2024 and RREI‑2025 cycles.
