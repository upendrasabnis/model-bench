**Subject:** Re: Eligibility and Co‑Funding Options for the Rural Renewable Energy Innovation Grant (Ref: RREI‑2026‑02)

Dear Mr. Mendoza,

Thank you for your thoughtful inquiry and for the opportunity to assist GreenFields Community Alliance as you prepare a joint proposal for the Rural Renewable Energy Innovation Grant (RREI‑2026‑02). I have reviewed your questions in detail and provide the following clarifications:

1. **Eligibility of Joint Applications**  
   The RREI program explicitly encourages collaborative projects that involve multiple stakeholder categories, including nonprofit organizations, utility cooperatives, and academic institutions. Joint proposals are permissible provided that **one of the partners is designated as the lead applicant**—the entity that will submit the application, sign the grant agreement, and assume primary fiscal responsibility. The lead applicant must be a nonprofit organization that meets the standard eligibility criteria (registered 501(c)(3) status, demonstrated project‑management capacity, and a clear mission alignment with rural renewable energy development). The cooperative and university may serve as sub‑recipients or partners, contributing technical expertise, in‑kind resources, or implementation support. A Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) outlining each party’s roles, decision‑making authority, and financial commitments is required at the time of submission.

2. **Co‑Funding Requirements**  
   The 30 % co‑funding match may be satisfied through a combination of cash and **in‑kind contributions**. Acceptable in‑kind items include:  
   - Volunteer labor (valued at a reasonable market rate, documented with time‑sheets and supervisor verification)  
   - Donated equipment or materials (receipts, donor letters, and a valuation schedule)  
   - Use of existing facilities or infrastructure (cost‑share based on fair market rental rates)  
   To document these contributions, you should prepare a **Co‑Funding Schedule** that lists each source, the dollar value assigned, and supporting evidence (e.g., donation letters, invoices, or attendance logs). This schedule is attached to the budget narrative and must be signed by all contributing parties.

3. **Project Duration and Extension Policy**  
   The standard award period is 24 months. Should unforeseen circumstances—such as permitting delays, supply‑chain disruptions, or other force‑majeure events—arise, you may request a **timeline extension** of up to an additional 6 months. The request must be submitted in writing **no later than 30 days before the scheduled project end date**, accompanied by:  
   - A concise justification narrative  
   - A revised work plan with milestones  
   - Evidence of the delay (e.g., permit letters, supplier correspondence)  
   The review committee will evaluate the request and notify you of the approved extension within 15 days of receipt.

4. **Reporting Cadence and Metrics**  
   Successful applicants typically submit **quarterly progress reports** and a final report at the conclusion of the award period. Key performance indicators (KPIs) commonly tracked include:  
   - Megawatts (MW) of renewable capacity installed  
   - Number of households or businesses served  
   - Energy generated versus projected output (kWh)  
   - Community engagement metrics (e.g., workshops held, volunteers trained)  
   - Greenhouse‑gas emissions avoided (tCO₂e)  
   - Financial performance relative to the approved budget  
   The grant office provides a **Reporting Template** (PDF and Excel versions) on our website; the template includes sections for narrative updates, KPI tables, financial reconciliation, and a risk‑mitigation log. Completed templates should be uploaded via the online portal within 10 days after the close of each reporting period.
