**1. Eligibility of Joint Applications**  
The RREI program explicitly encourages collaborative proposals that bring together nonprofit, utility‑cooperative, and academic expertise. A joint application is permissible, provided that one organization serves as the **lead applicant** and assumes primary responsibility for administrative tasks, sub‑award management, and communication with the funding agency. The lead applicant must be the entity that signs the grant agreement, submits the application, and is ultimately accountable for compliance, reporting, and any required audits. In practice, most successful consortia designate the nonprofit as the lead when its mission aligns closely with the grant’s community‑impact objectives, but the program does not prescribe a specific entity type for this role. We recommend that your consortium draft a formal Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) that outlines each partner’s contributions, responsibilities, and governance structure; a copy of this MOU should be included as an attachment to the application.

**2. Co‑Funding Requirements**  
The grant requires a **30 % cash or in‑kind match** of the total project budget. The program’s financial‑assistance policy expressly permits in‑kind contributions, which may include:

- **Volunteer labor** (valued at the prevailing market rate for the type of work performed)  
- **Donated equipment or materials** (documented with itemized receipts or letters of donation)  
- **Use of existing facilities** (supported by a facility‑use agreement that outlines depreciation or opportunity cost)

To satisfy the match, each in‑kind item must be **quantified in dollars**, **explained**, and **supported by verifiable documentation** (e.g., signed donation letters, time‑sheet summaries, facility‑use agreements, and cost‑allocation statements). We advise that you maintain a separate “Match Documentation Worksheet” that links each contribution to a specific budget line and includes the required supporting evidence. The final application will need to attach this worksheet to the Budget Narrative.

**3. Project Duration and Extension Policy**  
The standard project period is **24 months**. Should unforeseen circumstances—such as permitting delays, supply‑chain disruptions, or weather‑related setbacks—prevent timely completion, the RREI program provides a formal extension process. To request an extension, the lead applicant must submit a **written request** at least **60 days** before the original project end date. The request should include:

- A concise description of the circumstances causing the delay  
- The proposed revised timeline with specific milestones  
- Evidence of mitigation actions already taken (e.g., permits in‑process, vendor confirmations)  
- An updated project‑management plan reflecting the new schedule  

Extensions are granted on a case‑by‑case basis and are subject to approval by the program’s technical review panel. If the extension is approved, the grant agreement will be amended accordingly, and any additional reporting periods will be incorporated into the final performance report.

**4. Reporting Cadence and Metrics**  
Robust monitoring and evaluation are central to RREI’s mission. Successful applicants have typically submitted **quarterly progress reports** (PDF format) and a **final deliverable report** at project close. The reporting templates are available on our grants portal (https://grants.rrei.gov/templates). Key performance indicators (KPIs) commonly used across previous cycles include:

| KPI | Description | Target (example) |
|-----|-------------|------------------|
| **Energy Generation (kWh)** | Total renewable electricity produced by the micro‑grid | 150,000 kWh over 24 months |
| **Carbon‑Emission Reduction** | CO₂ equivalents avoided compared to fossil‑fuel baseline | 120 tCO₂e |
| **Community Engagement** | Number of households or farms directly connected to the micro‑grid | 250 households |
| **Job Creation** | Full‑time equivalents (FTEs) generated during implementation | 3 FTEs for 12 months |
| **Technology Adoption Rate** | Percentage of pilot participants adopting the technology long‑term | 80 % retention after 12 months |

The quarterly template requires fields for **project status**, **milestones achieved**, **budget expenditures**, **KPIs progress**, and **risks/challenges**. The final report expands on each KPI with detailed analysis, lessons learned, and recommendations for scaling. We have also observed that including **photographic evidence**, **stakeholder testimonials**, and **third‑party verification letters** (e.g., from the electric cooperative) strengthens the narrative and demonstrates impact.

Please note that the full set of reporting guidelines, the most recent successful proposals, and the template repository are accessible once you create a login on the RREI portal.
