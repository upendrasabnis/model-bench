**1. Eligibility of Joint Applications**  
The RREI program expressly encourages collaborative projects that leverage the strengths of multiple sectors. A joint proposal comprising a nonprofit organization, an electric‑utility cooperative, and an academic research laboratory is permissible, provided that one entity is designated as the **lead applicant**. The lead applicant must be an eligible organization under the grant’s eligibility criteria (i.e., a nonprofit, tribal government, or public‑sector entity) and will be responsible for:  
- Submitting the application through the grants portal,  
- Managing the award funds and financial reporting,  
- Ensuring that all partners sign a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) outlining roles, responsibilities, and intellectual‑property arrangements, and  
- Serving as the primary point of contact for the grant‑making agency.  
The MOU should be uploaded as an attachment to the application and will be reviewed during the eligibility screening.

**2. Co‑Funding Requirements**  
A 30 % co‑funding match is required, and the program accepts **in‑kind contributions** toward this match. Eligible in‑kind items include volunteer labor (valued at the prevailing wage rate for similar work), donated equipment (fair‑market value at the time of donation), and the use of existing facilities (reasonable rental value). To document these contributions, you must:  
- Provide a detailed valuation methodology (e.g., wage surveys, equipment appraisal quotes, or facility rental rates),  
- Include letters of commitment from the donor specifying the nature and value of the contribution, and  
- Summarize all in‑kind amounts in the budget narrative and the “Match Funding” table of the application.  
All valuations must be reasonable, verifiable, and consistent with federal cost‑principles (2 CFR 200).

**3. Project Duration and Extension Policy**  
The standard performance period is 24 months. Should unforeseen circumstances (e.g., permitting delays, supply‑chain disruptions) necessitate a schedule adjustment, grantees may submit a **formal extension request** no later than 60 days before the current end date. The request must include:  
- A revised project timeline with milestone dates,  
- A justification explaining the cause of the delay and why it was beyond the grantee’s control,  
- An impact assessment on the budget (if any), and  
- Updated risk‑mitigation plans.  
Extensions are typically granted in increments of up to 6 months, subject to the availability of funds and the continued alignment with program goals. The grant officer will review the request and issue a formal amendment if approved.

**4. Reporting Cadence and Metrics**  
Successful RREI awardees submit:  
- **Quarterly Progress Reports** (narrative + financial) detailing activities completed, expenditures incurred, and any deviations from the work plan,  
- An **Annual Performance Report** that aggregates quarterly data and highlights outcomes, and  
- A **Final Report** at project close‑out, which includes a comprehensive evaluation of results and lessons learned.  

Key performance indicators (KPIs) commonly used in past cycles include:  
- **Installed renewable capacity** (kW/MW of solar and biogas),  
- **Number of households or businesses served** with reliable electricity,  
- **Estimated annual greenhouse‑gas emissions avoided** (metric tons CO₂e),  
- **Jobs created or retained** (direct and indirect), and  
- **Community engagement metrics** (workshops held, participants trained, stakeholder meetings).  

The reporting templates and a KPI guidance document are available in the “Applicant Resources” section of the RREI webpage; I have attached the latest versions for your convenience.
