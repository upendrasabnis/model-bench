**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
The Community Resilience Grant is open to a broad spectrum of community‑based organisations, and unincorporated groups that operate under the auspices of a local council are indeed eligible, provided that the council acts as the fiscal sponsor. In such arrangements the council is responsible for holding any required insurances, charitable status, and financial oversight. Your garden’s arrangement satisfies this criterion, so you may proceed with confidence that the structural framework of your group is acceptable for funding consideration.

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
Our grant programme does not prescribe a rigid minimum or maximum award. Instead, we evaluate each proposal on its merit, community impact, and alignment with the programme’s strategic objectives. Applications typically request amounts ranging from $5,000 to $50,000, but we have funded projects both below and above these benchmarks where the case for funding was particularly strong. When you draft your budget, you may propose the total cost of the rainwater tanks and shade structure; we will then assess the portion that is deemed fundable against the overall scope and available budget pools for the round.

**Eligibility of infrastructure such as rainwater tanks and shade structures**  
Infrastructure that directly enhances the long‑term sustainability and resilience of community assets is fully permissible under the grant’s eligible costs. Rainwater harvesting systems and climate‑responsive shade structures are explicitly listed as allowable expenses because they:  

1. Reduce operational costs (e.g., water usage, cooling requirements).  
2. Mitigate the impacts of extreme weather events, aligning with the grant’s resilience focus.  
3. Provide tangible, lasting benefits to a wide user base, including volunteers, members, and the broader regional community.  

You should ensure that any equipment or construction work complies with relevant local building codes, environmental regulations, and council procurement policies.

**Supporting documents to prepare**  
To strengthen your application, we recommend assembling the following documents:  

* **Project Description** – a clear narrative outlining the problem you are addressing, the proposed solution, and the expected outcomes.  
* **Budget and Narrative** – a detailed line‑item budget that breaks down costs for tanks, shade structure, installation, and any ancillary expenses (e.g., design fees, permits).  
* **Proof of Auspices** – a letter from your local council confirming that it will act as the fiscal sponsor, hold insurance, and be accountable for any grant funds disbursed.  
* **Organizational Profile** – a brief overview of your volunteer team, governance structure, and any past community projects you have delivered.  
* **Financial Statements** – the most recent audited or reviewed financial statements (or, for a small unincorporated group, a simple income‑and‑expenditure statement) demonstrating your $40,000 turnover and the sources of revenue.  
* **Risk Assessment and Management Plan** – documentation outlining how you will manage health‑and‑safety risks, maintenance responsibilities, and contingency plans for the infrastructure.  
* **Community Support** – letters of support from local businesses, resident groups, or other stakeholders, as well as any evidence of community need (e.g., attendance data, surveys).
