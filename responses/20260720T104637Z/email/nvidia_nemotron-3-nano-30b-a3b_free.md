Dear Jamie,  

Thank you for reaching out with your questions regarding the Community Resilience Grant. I appreciate the thoughtful preparation you are undertaking before committing resources to a full application. Below I address each of the points you raised in detail.

1. **Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
   Applications are accepted from incorporated not‑for‑profit organisations and from unincorporated groups that are formally auspiced by a local government authority. Because your garden operates under the auspices of the council and the council holds the necessary insurance, you meet the eligibility criteria for this grant round. However, the application must be submitted in the name of the council as the auspicing body, with the council’s written confirmation of your project’s scope and budget attached.

2. **Minimum or maximum grant amounts**  
   The current funding envelope for the Community Resilience Grant ranges from **$5,000 to $50,000**. Projects that demonstrate a clear link to climate‑adaptation or disaster‑risk reduction are eligible for any amount within this band, subject to the availability of funds and the merit of the proposal. There is no separate “minimum” threshold beyond the $5,000 floor; however, the council may request a brief justification if your requested amount falls near the lower end.

3. **Eligible infrastructure costs**  
   The grant expressly covers capital works that enhance community resilience, including the installation of **rainwater harvesting systems** and **shade structures**. Both of these items are classified as eligible infrastructure costs, provided they are directly tied to mitigating exposure to extreme heat and water scarcity. You will need to supply detailed costings, quotations, and a brief rationale explaining how each component contributes to the garden’s ability to continue operating during hotter summers.

4. **Supporting documentation to prepare**  
   To submit a complete application, please have the following items ready:  
   - A signed **Letter of Support** from the council confirming the auspicing arrangement and outlining any in‑kind contributions.  
   - A **Project Narrative** (max 2 pages) that describes the problem, objectives, expected outcomes, and the link to resilience.  
   - **Itemised budget** showing total project costs, the portion you are seeking from the grant, and any matched funding or in‑kind contributions.  
   - **Quotes or tenders** from approved suppliers for the rainwater tanks, shade structure, and any associated installation works.  
   - Evidence of **insurance coverage**, either through the council’s policy or a separate arrangement, covering public liability and works.  
   - If available, **letters of endorsement** from community groups or local residents who will benefit from the project.
