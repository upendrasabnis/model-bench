Subject: Re: Question about eligibility for the Community Resilience Grant

Dear Jamie,

Thank you for your enquiry regarding the Community Resilience Grant round. I appreciate you reaching out before investing time in a full application, and I am happy to address each of your questions in turn.

First, regarding your organisational structure: **unincorporated groups auspiced by a local government authority are eligible to apply**, provided the auspicing body (in this case, your local council) meets our eligibility criteria for auspicing organisations—specifically, that they are a legal entity with an ABN, hold appropriate public liability insurance, and are willing to accept legal and financial responsibility for the grant. Since you have confirmed the council already holds the relevant insurances on your behalf, this arrangement satisfies our requirements. You will need to provide a formal letter of support from the council confirming their auspicing role, their ABN, and their agreement to the grant terms and conditions.

Second, **there is no minimum grant amount**, however the maximum request for this round is **$50,000 (GST exclusive)**. Given your project scope and annual turnover of approximately $40,000, a request within this ceiling would be appropriate. Please note that we generally expect applicants to demonstrate some capacity for co-contribution (cash or in-kind), though this is not a strict eligibility requirement for grants under $20,000.

Third, **infrastructure such as rainwater tanks and shade structures is considered an eligible capital cost**, provided the items are permanently affixed, essential to the project’s resilience objectives, and comply with relevant Australian Standards and local planning regulations. You should include itemised quotes from at least two suppliers for each major component.

Finally, **supporting documents you should prepare** include:
- Council auspicing letter (as above)
- Your group’s most recent financial statement or treasurer’s report
- Evidence of land tenure or council permission to install infrastructure on the site
- Two written quotes for each capital item
- A simple project plan with timeline, risk management, and maintenance arrangements
- Letters of community support (minimum two)

Applications close at 5:00 pm AEST on 28 June. I encourage you to start drafting in our online portal early, as the system allows you to save and return. If you would like a brief phone call to walk through the budget template or discuss co-contribution options, please let me know a few suitable times.

We value the work your garden does for community resilience and wish you the best with your application.
