**1. Auspicing arrangement**  
The CRG expressly permits applications from unincorporated groups that are auspiced by an eligible legal entity, such as a local government council. To satisfy this requirement, you will need to submit a signed letter of auspice from the council confirming that:  
- the council will act as the legal applicant and will hold the grant funds on your behalf;  
- the council maintains the necessary public liability and volunteer insurance covering the garden’s activities; and  
- the council endorses the specific project (rainwater tanks and shade structure) as aligned with its own resilience objectives.  

If the council holds an Australian Business Number (ABN) or is registered for GST, that ABN should be used on the application form; otherwise, the council’s entity details will suffice.

**2. Grant size**  
The CRG offers funding ranging from a minimum of **$5,000** to a maximum of **$50,000** per successful applicant. There is no prescribed “average” award; assessment panels consider the scale, impact, and cost‑effectiveness of each proposal. Given your estimated turnover of $40,000, a request within the mid‑range (e.g., $15,000–$25,000) would be competitive, provided the budget is well justified.

**3. Eligible costs**  
Infrastructure that directly enhances a community’s capacity to withstand climate‑related stresses is an eligible cost category. Rainwater harvesting tanks and shade structures are both explicitly listed under the “adaptation infrastructure” line item in the grant guidelines, as they:  
- reduce reliance on mains water during drought periods;  
- mitigate heat stress for volunteers and visitors; and  
- extend the garden’s operational season, thereby strengthening local food security.  

Please ensure that any quoted costs are itemised, include GST (if applicable), and are supported by at least two written quotes from reputable suppliers.

**4. Supporting documentation**  
To strengthen your application, we recommend preparing the following items:  
- **Project plan** (objectives, activities, timeline, and expected outcomes).  
- **Detailed budget** with quotes and a clear breakdown of how grant funds will be spent versus any co‑contribution or in‑kind support.  
- **Letter of auspice** from the local council (as described above).  
- **Proof of insurance** (certificate of currency) held by the council for the garden site.  
- **Organisational background** (brief history of the garden, volunteer structure, and any partnership agreements).  
- **Financial statements** (most recent 12‑month income and expenditure summary) to demonstrate fiscal management, even though formal audited accounts are not required for unincorporated groups.  
- **Letters of support** from community members, local schools, or other stakeholders that illustrate broad backing and anticipated benefit.  

All documents should be combined into a single PDF (max 25 MB) and uploaded via the online portal by the closing date. Late submissions cannot be accepted.
