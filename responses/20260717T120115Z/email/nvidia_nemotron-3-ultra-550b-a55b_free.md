Thank you for your enquiry regarding the Community Resilience Grant round. I appreciate you reaching out before investing time in a full application, and I am pleased to address each of your questions in turn.

**Eligibility of Auspiced Groups:** Yes, unincorporated groups operating under a formal auspicing arrangement with an eligible legal entity—such as a local council—are eligible to apply. The auspicing organisation must be the legal applicant, sign the funding agreement, and assume financial and reporting responsibility for the grant. As your council already holds the relevant insurances, this arrangement appears to meet our requirements, provided the council submits a letter of support confirming its willingness to act as auspicor and outlining the governance arrangements between the parties.

**Grant Amounts:** For this round, the minimum grant request is $5,000 and the maximum is $50,000. Your proposed project scope suggests you would fall comfortably within this range.

**Eligible Costs:** Infrastructure that directly enhances community resilience to climate impacts is a priority for this program. Rainwater tanks and permanent shade structures are explicitly listed as eligible capital items in the program guidelines, provided they are fixed assets that remain with the project site. Costs for installation, engineering certification, and necessary permits are also eligible. Operational expenses such as ongoing maintenance or utility bills are not funded.

**Supporting Documents:** In addition to the standard application form, you should prepare:
1. A signed letter from the council CEO or authorised delegate confirming the auspicing arrangement, insurance coverage, and commitment to financial management.
2. Your group’s most recent financial statement (a simple income and expenditure report is sufficient given your $40,000 turnover).
3. Quotes from at least two suppliers for the tanks and shade structure.
4. A brief project plan with timelines, risk management, and evidence of community consultation or need (e.g., letters of support from garden members or local stakeholders).
5. Evidence of land tenure or a licence to occupy the site for a period exceeding the grant term.
