Dear Jamie,

Thank you for reaching out and for the valuable work your volunteer‑run community garden is doing to strengthen local resilience. I am happy to clarify the eligibility criteria and application requirements for the Community Resilience Grant (CRG) round that closes on [insert date].

**1. Auspiced, unincorporated groups**  
The CRG program does allow applications from groups that are not incorporated entities, provided they are auspiced by an eligible legal entity—most commonly a local government authority, incorporated association, or registered charity. Your arrangement, whereby the local council holds the necessary insurances and formally auspices the garden, satisfies this requirement. You will need to submit a signed letter of auspice from the council confirming that they accept legal and financial responsibility for the grant funds should your application be successful.

**2. Grant size**  
For this funding round, the minimum grant amount is AU$5,000 and the maximum is AU$50,000 (GST exclusive). Projects requesting amounts outside this range will be deemed ineligible. Given your estimated turnover of approximately AU$40,000, you are well within the program’s financial thresholds, and a request for the full cost of the rainwater tanks and shade structure (including installation) would be permissible, provided it does not exceed the AU$50,000 cap.

**3. Eligible costs – infrastructure**  
Capital works that directly enhance community resilience to climate‑related stresses are explicitly eligible. This includes the purchase and installation of rainwater harvesting tanks, associated plumbing, and shade structures that mitigate heat exposure for volunteers and visitors. Operational costs (e.g., ongoing maintenance, utilities, or staff salaries) are not covered, but you may include a modest contingency (up to 10 % of the total project cost) for unforeseen installation expenses.

**4. Supporting documentation**  
To strengthen your application, please prepare the following items:

- **Auspice letter** from the local council (on council letterhead, signed by an authorised officer) confirming their role as the legal auspice and their willingness to manage the grant funds.
- **Proof of council insurance** (certificate of currency) covering the garden’s activities and the proposed works.
- **Financial summary** of the garden’s recent income and expenditure (e.g., a simple profit‑and‑loss statement or bank statements for the past 12 months) to demonstrate the AU$40,000 turnover figure you mentioned.
- **Project plan** outlining the objectives, timeline (including key milestones), and how the rainwater tanks and shade structure will improve resilience to hot summers.
- **Detailed budget** with itemised quotes from at least two suppliers for the tanks, shade structure, fittings, and labour, plus any applicable GST.
- **Risk management plan** addressing health and safety considerations during installation and ongoing use.
- **Letters of support** (optional but helpful) from community members, local schools, or other stakeholders who will benefit from the enhanced facilities.

All documents should be submitted as PDF files via the online grant portal; please ensure each file is clearly labelled (e.g., “Auspice_Letter_Council.pdf”, “Budget_Quote_Tanks.pdf”).
