Thank you for reaching out and for the clear description of your project. I’m pleased to provide the following information to help you determine whether your organization can proceed with a full application for the upcoming Community Resilience Grant round.

**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our grant guidelines explicitly state that unincorporated community groups may apply provided they are formally auspiced by a local government entity that can demonstrate legal responsibility for the group’s activities. In such arrangements the council typically holds the necessary public liability and other insurances, which satisfies our requirement for risk coverage. Therefore, your garden’s arrangement with the council should meet the eligibility criteria, even though you are not yet a registered charity.

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
We do not prescribe a fixed minimum or maximum grant size; rather, awards are determined on the basis of the overall budget, the demonstrated community impact, and the proportion of funding requested relative to total project costs. In recent rounds, successful projects have received between $5,000 and $50,000, but applications outside this range are also considered if they are justified and cost‑effective. Your projected capital budget of roughly $40,000 for the rainwater tanks and shade structure will therefore be evaluated against the total project cost, including any matching contributions you may wish to secure.

**Infrastructure eligibility**  
Capital infrastructure such as rainwater harvesting systems and permanent shade structures is expressly listed as an eligible expense under the “Environmental Resilience” component of the grant. These items must be essential to the project’s resilience objectives, provide a measurable benefit (e.g., water savings, reduced heat stress on crops), and have a useful life of at least five years. Documentation should demonstrate how each item directly supports the continuation of garden operations during hot summer periods.

**Supporting documents to prepare**  
To streamline the assessment process, we recommend assembling the following materials:

1. **Project Narrative** – a concise description of the problem, objectives, implementation timeline, and expected outcomes.  
2. **Detailed Budget** – line‑item costs for equipment, installation, contingency, and any matching funds.  
3. **Letter of Support/Auspice Confirmation** – an official letter from your council confirming the auspice relationship, insurance coverage, and any in‑kind support they provide.  
4. **Financial Statements** – the most recent audited or reviewed accounts (or, for a small operation, a simple profit‑and‑loss statement and balance sheet) showing the $40,000 turnover you mentioned.  
5. **Organizational Profile** – a short description of your volunteer team, governance structure, and any previous community projects.  
6. **Site Plans or Sketches** – showing where the rainwater tanks and shade structure will be located.  
7. **Evidence of Community Engagement** – membership lists, volunteer sign‑ups, or local survey results that illustrate demand for the project.  

If your organization does not yet have formally audited accounts, a clear, handwritten cash‑flow summary that outlines revenue sources (produce stall, membership fees, grants) and expenses will be acceptable. The key is to demonstrate financial transparency and accountability.
