Subject: Re: Eligibility Query – Community Resilience Grant  

Dear Jamie,  

Thank you for reaching out and for the detailed description of your community‑garden project. I’m pleased to clarify the points you raised regarding the Community Resilience Grant (CRG) and to provide guidance on the documentation you will need to prepare.  

**1. Eligibility of groups auspiced by a council**  
The CRG accepts applications from “unincorporated associations that are formally auspiced by a Local Government Authority (LGA) where the LGA holds the requisite insurance and is listed as the primary service provider.” Because your garden operates under the umbrella of your council and the council retains the appropriate public‑liability coverage, you meet the eligibility criteria for this grant round.  

**2. Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
For the current funding cycle, successful applicants may receive a grant ranging from **$5,000 to $25,000**. Projects that demonstrate a clear, measurable resilience benefit and a realistic budget are prioritised. While there is no fixed “minimum” award, applicants should be prepared to justify the requested amount in relation to the scope of work.  

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Infrastructure that directly enhances the site’s ability to withstand climate‑related stressors is eligible, provided it is:  

* Essential to the operation of the garden (e.g., water capture, storage, or shading);  
* Cost‑effective and proportionate to the overall project; and  
* Documented as a capital expense rather than routine maintenance.  

Rain‑water tanks and a shade structure fall squarely within these parameters and are therefore eligible cost categories.  

**4. Supporting documentation**  
To accompany your application, please prepare the following:  

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Letter of endorsement from the council | Confirms the auspicing arrangement and confirms that the council holds appropriate insurance. |
| Detailed project budget | Itemises each cost (e.g., tanks, fittings, labour, design) and justifies the requested grant amount. |
| Project timeline & work plan | Shows milestones, procurement plans, and completion dates. |
| Evidence of current funding sources | Demonstrates your $40,000 annual turnover and the proportion derived from the produce stall and membership fees. |
| Risk‑mitigation and resilience plan | Outlines how the proposed infrastructure will improve the garden’s ability to operate during extreme heat or water shortages. |
| Proof of identity for the coordinating committee | Standard identification for all grant applicants. |
