Below I address each of the five questions you raised.

**1. Scope of the audit**  
The final audit is primarily a financial audit. The auditor must verify that all expenditures are allowable, reasonable, and correctly allocated against the approved budget and that the financial statements present a true and fair view of the grant‑related activity. Technical performance metrics—such as kilowatt‑hours generated, system uptime, and other outcomes reported in your quarterly technical reports—do not form part of the statutory audit scope. However, the auditor is expected to confirm that the financial records support the reported performance figures (e.g., that revenue from feed‑in tariffs, if any, is recorded accurately). Should the auditor identify material discrepancies between the financial data and the technical data, they must note this in their findings, but a separate technical audit is not required.

**2. Auditor qualifications**  
The audit must be performed by an independent firm that is a member of a recognised UK professional body. Acceptable bodies include the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales (ICAEW), the Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA), or the Chartered Institute of Public Finance and Accountancy (CIPFA). A firm that holds a CPA qualification from another jurisdiction may be used only if it is also a member of one of the aforementioned UK bodies or if it has been formally recognised by the UK regulator as meeting equivalent standards. The key requirements are independence, professional indemnity insurance, and adherence to UK auditing standards (UK GAAS/IFRS as appropriate).

**3. Timing and submission**  
The 90‑day deadline is triggered by the **actual date of project completion**, which is defined in the award letter as the date on which the final activity (e.g., system commissioning and hand‑over to the community partner) is completed and all grant‑related deliverables are satisfied. The submission of the final technical report is a separate deliverable; you may submit it before the 90‑day window opens, but the clock does not start until the completion date is formally acknowledged by the Programme Officer and recorded in the grant’s status tracker.

**4. Documentation package**  
In addition to the audited financial statements and auditor’s report, we require the following supporting documentation to be uploaded to the portal:

* Detailed payroll registers (including NI and tax deductions) for all grant‑funded staff.  
* Full procurement file for each major purchase (purchase orders, supplier invoices, delivery receipts, and evidence of competitive bidding where the value exceeds £5,000).  
* Copies of all sub‑contractor agreements, together with proof of payment (invoice + payment voucher).  
* A reconciliation schedule that links each budget line‑item to the audited figures.  

A summary of key line items is insufficient for compliance review. Providing the complete schedules enables us to perform a rapid secondary check and expedites the release of the final disbursement.

**5. Electronic versus hard‑copy delivery**  
The audit report may be submitted electronically through the grant management portal. The electronic file must be a PDF that is password‑protected, signed electronically by the audit partner, and accompanied by a signed audit completion letter. A hard‑copy version is not required unless the auditor’s firm is unable to provide a qualified electronic signature. If, for any reason, the funder requests a physical copy later, we will notify you promptly.
