Specifically, the auditor should confirm that the kilowatt‑hours generated, system uptime, and any other key performance indicators are consistent with the data submitted in the quarterly reports and any supporting documentation (e.g., inverter logs, monitoring screenshots). While a CPA‑qualified firm is acceptable, the auditor must hold a current UK‑based accreditation and demonstrate independence in accordance with the Auditing Practices Board standards. Please ensure the selected firm can provide a written declaration of independence and any relevant ethical clearances.

3. **Timing and submission** – The 90‑day period begins on the date the final technical report is submitted, not on the last day of project activity. This is because the technical report constitutes the definitive record of project completion and triggers the start of the final disbursement review. Consequently, the audit report must be uploaded to the grant management portal no later than 90 calendar days after the portal’s receipt of the final technical report.

This includes, but is not limited to, payroll records (detailing hours and rates for any staff directly engaged on the project), procurement invoices with supporting contracts, sub‑contractor agreements, bank reconciliations, and any other source documents that underpin the financial statements. A summary of line items alone will not satisfy the audit requirement; the full suite of source documents must be made available to the auditor for inspection.
