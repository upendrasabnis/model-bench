Dear Jamie,

Thank you for reaching out and for the thoughtful work you are doing with your community garden. I am happy to clarify the eligibility requirements for the Community Resilience Grant and to outline the documentation you will need to prepare a strong application.

**Auspicing arrangements**  
The grant program permits unincorporated groups to apply provided they are auspiced by an eligible legal entity—in this case, your local council. The council must act as the formal applicant, accepting legal and financial responsibility for the funds, while you, as the garden coordinator, can be named as the project lead. A signed letter of auspice from the council (on official letterhead) that confirms they will hold the grant, manage acquittal, and provide the necessary insurance coverage is required.

**Grant size**  
The Community Resilience Grant offers funding ranging from a minimum of **$5,000** to a maximum of **$50,000** per successful applicant. Requests should be justified by a detailed budget that shows how the requested amount will directly achieve the project’s resilience outcomes.

**Eligible costs**  
Capital infrastructure that enhances community resilience to climate‑related stressors is an eligible expense. Rainwater tanks and a shade structure that enable continued operation during hotter summers fall within this scope, provided they are essential to the project’s objectives and are not primarily for commercial gain. Operational costs (e.g., stall fees, membership administration) are not eligible unless they are inseparably linked to the infrastructure installation (for example, site preparation labour that is part of the build).

**Supporting documents to prepare**  
1. **Auspice letter** from the local council confirming their role as legal applicant and detailing insurance coverage.  
2. **Project plan** (including timeline, objectives, and how the infrastructure will improve resilience).  
3. **Detailed budget** with itemised quotes for the rainwater tanks, shade structure, delivery, and any installation labour.  
4. **Evidence of community need** – e.g., minutes from garden meetings, letters of support from members or local residents, and data on recent heat‑impact incidents.  
5. **Financial information** – a simple statement of the garden’s annual turnover ($40,000) and, if available, the council’s most recent financial statements or a copy of their ABN/ACN.  
6. **Risk management plan** – outlining safety measures during installation and ongoing maintenance responsibilities.  
7. **Photos or site plans** of the proposed installation area.

Please ensure that all documents are current (dated within the last six months) and that the council’s auspice letter explicitly states their willingness to accept the grant funds and acquit them in accordance with the program’s guidelines.
