Thank you for reaching out and for your interest in the Community Resilience Grant. I appreciate the detailed overview of your volunteer‑run community garden and the thoughtful questions you have raised. I am pleased to confirm that, subject to the criteria outlined below, your project is indeed eligible for consideration.

**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our funding guidelines explicitly recognise organisations that operate under the auspices of a local government authority, provided that the council’s insurance and financial oversight arrangements are in place. As your group is covered by the council’s insurance and you can demonstrate a formal agreement (e.g., a memorandum of understanding or service‑level agreement), you meet the eligibility requirement for an unincorporated entity.

**2. Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
- **Minimum:** The program requires a minimum request of **$5,000**.  
- **Maximum:** The maximum award for a single project is **$50,000**.  
Given your proposed budget for rainwater tanks and a shade structure, you can comfortably fall within this range. If your total project cost exceeds $50,000, you may consider phasing the work or seeking co‑funding from other sources.

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Infrastructure that directly supports community resilience—such as rainwater harvesting systems, shade structures, solar panels, or flood‑proofing measures—is classified as an eligible expense. The key conditions are:  
- The works must be **newly installed** or represent a **significant upgrade**.  
- They must be **permanent** or have a lifespan of at least five years.  
- They must be **located on the applicant’s site** and be accessible to the broader community.  
Your rainwater tanks and shade structure meet all three criteria, so associated costs (materials, labour, professional fees, and project management) are eligible line items.

**4. Supporting documentation to prepare**  
To substantiate your application, please include the following:  

- A signed **letter of support** from the council confirming the auspice arrangement and insurance coverage.  
- A **detailed project budget** that breaks down costs for materials, labour, professional services, and any in‑kind contributions.  
- **Quotes or tender documents** from at least two qualified suppliers or contractors for the rainwater tanks and shade structure.  
- A **project timeline** outlining design, procurement, installation, and commissioning milestones.  
- Evidence of **community need**, such as demographic data, climate‑impact assessments, or testimonials from regular users of the garden.  
- Your **governance documents** (e.g., constitution, rules) and evidence of the group’s incorporation status with the council (e.g., registration number, council‑issued operating licence).  

**5. Additional guidance**  
- Ensure all financial statements are audited or reviewed by an independent accountant, even if the turnover is modest.  
- Provide a concise **project narrative** (maximum 1,000 words) that explains the resilience rationale, expected outcomes, and how the grant will be managed and reported.  
- Submit the application electronically through our portal before the **30‑day deadline**, and retain copies of all supporting documents for at least five years.
