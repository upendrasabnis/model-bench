Subject: Re: Question about eligibility for the Community Resilience Grant

Dear Jamie,

Thank you for your enquiry regarding the Community Resilience Grant round. I appreciate you reaching out to clarify eligibility before investing time in a full application—this is exactly the kind of due diligence we encourage. I am pleased to address each of your questions in turn.

**Eligibility of Auspiced Groups:** Yes, unincorporated groups operating under a formal auspicing arrangement with an eligible legal entity—such as your local council—are eligible to apply. The auspicing organisation must be an incorporated body (e.g., local government, incorporated association, or company limited by guarantee) that holds appropriate public liability insurance and agrees to accept legal and financial responsibility for the grant. Your council’s willingness to hold the relevant insurances on your behalf satisfies this requirement, provided a written auspicing agreement is in place at the time of application.

**Grant Amounts:** The Community Resilience Grant offers funding between **$5,000 and $50,000** per project. There is no requirement to request the maximum; applicants should apply for the amount genuinely required to deliver the proposed activities. Your project scope suggests you would fall comfortably within this range.

**Eligible Costs – Infrastructure:** Capital infrastructure that directly supports community resilience is an eligible cost category. Rainwater tanks and a permanent shade structure designed to mitigate heat impacts and ensure continued operation during extreme weather events are explicitly listed as eligible in the program guidelines. Please note that costs must be for purchase and installation only; ongoing maintenance, operational expenses, and retrospective costs incurred before a funding agreement is executed are not eligible.

**Supporting Documents to Prepare:** To streamline your application, please gather the following:
1. A signed auspicing agreement (or letter of support on council letterhead confirming the arrangement, insurance coverage, and willingness to administer funds).
2. Your group’s most recent financial statement or a simple income-and-expenditure summary reflecting the ~$40,000 annual turnover.
3. Quotes from at least two suppliers for the rainwater tanks and shade structure (itemised, GST-inclusive, and dated within the last 90 days).
4. A brief project plan outlining timelines, key milestones, volunteer roles, and risk management (including heat-safety protocols).
5. Evidence of land tenure or written permission from the landowner/council for the proposed installations.
6. Letters of support from community stakeholders (e.g., local schools, neighbourhood houses, or health services) demonstrating broader community benefit.

The application portal opens on **1 June** and closes at **5:00 pm AEST on 30 June**. I strongly recommend drafting your responses in a Word document first, as the portal does not auto-save.
