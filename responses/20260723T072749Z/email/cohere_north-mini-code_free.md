**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our grant guidelines state that the primary applicant must be a legally‑registered entity (e.g., a registered charity, incorporated association, or company limited by guarantee). However, we do have a limited provision for unincorporated groups provided they are formally **auspiced** by an eligible organisation that can act as the primary applicant and assume financial and legal responsibility for the grant. In your case, because your garden operates under the auspices of the local council and the council holds the required insurances, you may be able to submit the application through the council as the primary applicant, with your garden acting as a delivery partner. Should you wish to pursue this route, the council will need to provide a letter of endorsement confirming its willingness to be the applicant and its capacity to manage the grant funds.

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The Community Resilience Grant is designed to fund projects ranging from **$5,000 to $150,000**. While many successful applications fall within the $10,000‑$50,000 band for small‑scale infrastructure, there is no strict minimum – projects that demonstrate strong community impact and clear outcomes can be considered even if they request less than $5,000, provided the total cost aligns with the scope of work. Your proposed rainwater tanks and shade structure appear to fall comfortably within the mid‑range of the programme.

**Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Yes, capital infrastructure such as **rainwater harvesting systems and permanent shade structures** is an eligible expense category under the “Resilience and Climate Adaptation” stream of the grant. The guidelines specifically highlight water‑saving installations and climate‑resilient amenities as priority investments, especially where they directly support food‑production or community‑use spaces. Please ensure that any quotes or specifications you provide clearly link these items to the project’s resilience objectives (e.g., reducing water usage, mitigating heat stress, ensuring continued operation during extreme summer conditions).

**Supporting documents we recommend**  
To strengthen your application, we suggest preparing the following documentation:

1. **Project Narrative** – a concise description of the problem, objectives, implementation plan, and expected outcomes.  
2. **Budget and Financial Projections** – detailed cost breakdown for the tanks, shade structure, installation, and any ongoing maintenance costs, plus a simple cash‑flow forecast for the next 12 months.  
3. **Organizational Profile** – even if you are not yet incorporated, a profile of the garden’s history, volunteer base, and community impact, accompanied by the council’s endorsement letter.  
4. **Insurance and Risk Management** – a copy of the council’s insurance certificate confirming coverage for the project site and any public liability.  
5. **Community Support** – letters of support from local residents, businesses, or stakeholder groups, and evidence of membership or participation rates.  
6. **Site Plans and Drawings** – schematic layouts showing where the tanks and shade structure will be placed, and any required council approvals or permits.  
7. **Environmental Assessment** – a brief statement on how the rainwater system will reduce water consumption and any ecological considerations (e.g., stormwater harvesting benefits).  

If your council agrees to act as the primary applicant, we will also need a **letter of financial authority** from them confirming that grant funds will be administered according to our reporting requirements.
