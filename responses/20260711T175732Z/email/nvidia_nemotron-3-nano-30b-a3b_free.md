Thank you for reaching out and for the detailed overview of your community garden project. I appreciate the thoughtful questions you have raised and am happy to clarify the eligibility criteria and documentation requirements for the Community Resilience Grant (CRG).

**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
The CRG explicitly accepts applications from *unincorporated associations* that are formally *auspiced* by a local government authority, provided the auspicing body holds the necessary insurance and can demonstrate that it has delegated authority over the project’s financial and reporting obligations. Our grant guidelines list “government‑auspiced entities” as an eligible category, so long as the council’s endorsement letter confirms this arrangement and outlines the scope of its oversight.

**2. Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The current funding round offers grants ranging from **$5,000 to $50,000**. Projects that demonstrate a clear, measurable impact on climate‑related resilience are eligible for the full spectrum; however, the final award amount is contingent on the budget narrative, cost‑effectiveness, and alignment with the grant’s objectives.

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Yes—capital works that directly enhance resilience, such as the installation of rain‑water harvesting systems, shade structures, solar panels, or flood‑mitigation upgrades, are classified as eligible expenditures. The infrastructure must be *new* or a *significant upgrade* that contributes to the project’s capacity to withstand climate stressors. Routine maintenance or purely aesthetic improvements are not covered.

**4. Required supporting documentation**  
To accompany your application, we request the following:

- A signed **Letter of Support** from the council confirming its auspice relationship and insurance coverage.  
- The council’s **risk‑management or insurance policy summary** (or a statement indicating that it provides coverage on your behalf).  
- A detailed **budget breakdown** showing projected costs for the rain‑water tanks, shade structure, and any associated installation or design fees.  
- A **project timeline** (including milestones, procurement plan, and completion date).  
- Evidence of **community engagement**, such as membership lists, meeting minutes, or letters of endorsement from local stakeholders.  
- A brief **impact statement** outlining how the proposed works will improve climate resilience for the garden and its users.
