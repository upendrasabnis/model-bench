Subject: Re: Question about eligibility for the Community Resilience Grant

Dear Jamie,

Thank you for your enquiry regarding the Community Resilience Grant round. I appreciate you reaching out before investing time in a full application, and I am happy to address each of your questions in turn.

First, regarding organisational structure: unincorporated groups operating under a formal auspicing arrangement with a local government authority are eligible to apply, provided the auspicing body (in this case, your local council) meets our eligibility criteria for lead applicants—specifically, that they are a legal entity with an ABN, hold appropriate public liability insurance, and are willing to enter into a funding agreement on your behalf. You will need to provide a signed letter from the council confirming the auspicing arrangement, their acceptance of financial and legal responsibility for the grant, and evidence of their current insurance certificates.

Second, the grant round offers a minimum request of $5,000 and a maximum of $50,000 per project. Your proposed budget for rainwater tanks and a shade structure falls well within this range. Infrastructure of this nature—fixed, capital items that directly enhance the climate resilience of a community asset—is considered an eligible cost, provided the items are permanently installed, remain the property of the auspicing body or the project, and are not classified as routine maintenance or portable equipment. Please ensure your budget includes itemised quotes from at least two suppliers for each major component.

Third, with an annual turnover of approximately $40,000, you are well below the $1 million threshold that triggers a requirement for audited financial statements. For this round, you should prepare: (1) the council’s letter of support and auspicing confirmation; (2) your group’s most recent annual financial statement (unaudited is acceptable); (3) a simple project plan outlining objectives, timeline, risk management, and volunteer capacity; (4) two written quotes for each infrastructure item; (5) evidence of land tenure or a licence to occupy the garden site; and (6) letters of support from at least two community stakeholders demonstrating broader benefit.

The application portal opens on 1 June and closes at 5:00 pm AEST on 30 June. I strongly recommend drafting your responses in a Word document first, as the portal does not auto-save. If you would like, I can arrange a brief 15-minute video call next week to walk through the budget template or answer any follow-up questions.

We value the work your garden does for community cohesion and food security in a regional setting, and we look forward to potentially supporting your resilience project.
