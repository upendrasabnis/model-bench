Dear Jamie,

Thank you for reaching out and for the detailed description of your community garden project. I’m delighted to hear about your commitment to enhancing resilience in a regional setting, and I appreciate your proactive approach in seeking clarification before submitting a full application.

**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our Community Resilience Grant is designed to support projects that deliver clear public benefit and demonstrate strong community involvement. While we do require applicants to be legally registered entities (e.g., incorporated associations, charitable trusts, or registered charities) in order to enter into a grant agreement and to ensure accountability, we recognise that many effective grassroots initiatives operate under the umbrella of a local government. If your garden is formally auspiced by the council—meaning the council holds the relevant insurances, manages risk, and can act as the primary contracting party—your group may still be considered eligible. In such cases, we typically request a written “auspice agreement” from the council confirming its support, liability coverage, and willingness to sub‑grant funds if the application is successful. Should you not have a formal auspice agreement in place, we can discuss whether an alternative structure (such as becoming a provisional incorporated association) might satisfy the eligibility criteria.

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
Each grant round sets its own funding envelope, and the Community Resilience Grant does not prescribe a rigid floor or ceiling. In recent cycles, awards have ranged from approximately $5,000 to $50,000, depending on the scope, geographic impact, and the proportion of matching funds the applicant can demonstrate. Your projected costs for rainwater tanks and a shade structure appear modest relative to these historic ranges, so there is flexibility to fund a substantial portion of the total project cost, provided the budget aligns with the grant’s objectives of climate‑resilient community assets.

**Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Infrastructure such as rainwater harvesting systems and shade structures is explicitly listed as an eligible expense under the “Climate Adaptation and Resilience” category of this grant. The key considerations are that the items must be new, directly support the garden’s ongoing operation during hot weather, and be justified with clear performance metrics (e.g., water storage capacity, shade coverage area). We also accept procurement quotes, specifications, and lifecycle cost analyses to demonstrate value for money.

**Supporting documents you should prepare**  
To streamline the review process, we recommend assembling the following package:

1. **Project Narrative** – a concise description of the problem you are addressing, the proposed solution, expected outcomes, and how the project aligns with the grant’s resilience objectives.  
2. **Detailed Budget** – itemised costs including equipment quotes, installation labour, contingency, and any matching contributions.  
3. **Organizational Profile** – a brief constitution or charter, a copy of the council’s auspice agreement (if applicable), and proof of insurance coverage held by the council.  
4. **Financial Statements** – the most recent audited or reviewed accounts (or, for a small volunteer‑run operation, a simple cash‑flow summary) to confirm your $40,000 turnover and demonstrate fiscal responsibility.  
5. **Letters of Support** – endorsements from local businesses, resident groups, or other stakeholders that highlight community buy‑in.  
6. **Site Plans and Photographs** – current site layout and images that illustrate the need for water storage and shade.  
7. **Implementation Timeline** – a Gantt‑style schedule outlining key milestones, from procurement to commissioning.  
8. **Risk Assessment and Mitigation Plan** – documenting how you will manage installation risks, maintenance, and potential disruptions.

If any of these documents are not yet available, please let us know; we can provide guidance on acceptable alternatives (for example, using a provisional financial summary in place of audited statements). We also encourage you to review the full grant guidelines on our website, as they contain additional requirements such as reporting obligations and any specific environmental compliance standards.
