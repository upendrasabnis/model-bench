Thank you for your inquiry and for the important work your community garden is doing. I’m happy to clarify the eligibility criteria for the Community Resilience Grant (CRG) round and outline the documentation that will strengthen your application.

**1. Applicant status**  
The CRG is open to *registered charities* and *community organisations* that are legally recognised as separate entities (e.g., incorporated associations or companies limited by guarantee). An unincorporated group that is simply supported by a council does **not** meet the statutory definition of a separate organisation, and therefore would not be eligible to receive the grant directly.  

If the local council is willing to act as an intermediary, you could consider registering the garden as an incorporated association (or a charitable trust) under the relevant state legislation. Once registered, the council can provide the necessary insurance, and the garden can apply as a distinct entity. Alternatively, the council itself could apply on your behalf, but this would require the council’s own grant‑applying capacity and separate budgeting.

**2. Grant amount**  
The CRG offers awards ranging from $5,000 to $25,000, depending on the scope of the project and the applicant’s financial capacity. For projects that involve infrastructure such as rainwater tanks and shade structures, the typical award falls in the $10,000–$18,000 band, provided the cost is fully justified and within the applicant’s turnover threshold (the CRG caps applications at 25 % of an applicant’s annual turnover – in your case $10,000).

**3. Eligible costs**  
Infrastructure improvements that directly enhance resilience to climate change are fully eligible. This includes:
- Installation of rainwater tanks (including piping and fittings)
- Construction of shade structures (timber, steel, or approved composite frames)
- Related civil works (excavation, drainage, and site preparation)

Any cost that is not directly linked to resilience (e.g., general marketing) would need to be excluded from the budget.

**4. Suggested supporting documents**  
To prepare a strong application, please gather the following:
1. **Proof of council sponsorship** – a letter of endorsement or a council resolution confirming that the garden operates under its auspices and that the council holds the relevant insurances.
2. **Financial statements** – the most recent audited or prepared financial statements, or, if not available, a self‑prepared statement showing turnover, expenses, and a projected budget for the next 12 months.
3. **Project budget** – a detailed cost breakdown for the tanks, shade structure, and ancillary works, with supplier quotes or price estimates.
4. **Project plan** – a timeline, risk assessment, and a brief description of how the infrastructure will enhance resilience and community benefit.
5. **Supporting evidence** – any community impact studies, membership lists, or evidence of the garden’s role in local sustainability initiatives.
