Thank you for reaching out and for the clear description of your garden’s needs and operations. I’m pleased to provide the following clarifications regarding the Community Resilience Grant round that you are considering.

**Eligibility of unincorporated groups**  
Our grant program does accept applications from unincorporated community groups, provided that the group is formally **auspiced** by a legal entity that can assume financial and legal responsibility for the project. In practice, this means that a council, incorporated association, or other qualified body must act as the “lead applicant” and sign the grant agreement. Because your council already holds the necessary insurances and can manage the contractual aspects, your group meets this requirement. However, you will need to submit a formal **sponsorship agreement** from the council confirming that it will oversee the grant funds, provide any required indemnities, and report on the outcomes on your behalf.

**Grant amount – minimum and maximum**  
The Community Resilience Grant does not prescribe a strict minimum or maximum award. Instead, the funding level is determined by the scope, cost‑effectiveness, and strategic alignment of each proposal with our priority outcomes (e.g., climate resilience, community wellbeing, and sustainable resource use). Typical awards for infrastructure projects of the size you describe range from $15,000 to $50,000, but we have funded projects both below and above this band depending on the merit of the application.

**Eligibility of the proposed infrastructure**  
Yes – the installation of **rainwater harvesting systems** (including tanks, gutters, and associated plumbing) and **climate‑adapted shade structures** are explicitly listed as eligible costs under the “Infrastructure & Equipment” category of our guidelines. Both items must directly support the project’s resilience objectives (e.g., reducing water consumption during heat events and providing safe gathering spaces). We do require that all installations comply with relevant building codes, local council regulations, and, where applicable, accessibility standards.

**Supporting documents to prepare**  
When you move to a full application, we recommend assembling the following documents:

1. **Project Narrative** – a concise description of the problem, proposed solution, implementation timeline, and expected outcomes.  
2. **Budget Table** – itemised costs for equipment, installation, contingency, and any match funding.  
3. **Proof of Sponsorship** – a formal letter from your council confirming auspice, insurance coverage, and willingness to act as fiscal host.  
4. **Organizational Profile** – a brief constitution or memorandum of understanding for your volunteer group, plus the council’s endorsement.  
5. **Financial Statements** – the most recent audited or reviewed statements (or, for a small operation, a simple cash‑flow summary) demonstrating the $40,000 turnover you mentioned.  
6. **Community Support** – letters of endorsement from local residents, businesses, or partner organisations, and any evidence of volunteer participation.  
7. **Risk Assessment & Insurance** – documentation confirming that the council’s existing public liability policy covers the project activities, or a copy of the policy if you will be adding additional coverage.  
8. **Site Plans & Drawings** – sketches or scaled diagrams showing where the rainwater tanks and shade structure will be located, plus any necessary site‑specific considerations (e.g., drainage, accessibility).

We also encourage you to review the full grant guidelines on our website, paying particular attention to the **eligible activities**, **reporting requirements**, and **assessment criteria**.
