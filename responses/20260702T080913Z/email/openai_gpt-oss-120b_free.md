**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups / council‑auspiced projects**  
The CRG is open to a range of community‑led entities, including unincorporated groups, provided they have a formal relationship with a recognized public sector body that can act as a sponsor. Because your garden operates under the auspices of the local council and the council holds the required public liability and workers’ compensation insurance on your behalf, you meet the sponsor‑requirement. The council will need to complete the “Sponsor Declaration Form” and sign the “Grant Agreement” on your behalf. Please ensure that the council’s authorized representative signs both documents; otherwise the application will be returned for completion.

**2. Financial thresholds**  
The programme sets the following financial limits:

* **Maximum annual turnover:** $500,000 – your reported turnover of $40,000 is well within the limit.  
* **Maximum grant award:** $75,000 per project.  
* **Minimum grant request:** $5,000 – applications requesting less than this amount are not considered.

Given the scope of installing rainwater tanks and a shade structure, a request in the range of $15,000–$30,000 would typically be appropriate, but the exact figure will depend on the quotes you obtain.

**3. Eligible costs – infrastructure**  
Capital infrastructure that directly contributes to community resilience is eligible, provided it is:

* Required to maintain or improve the service you deliver (e.g., water security, heat mitigation).  
* Owned or controlled by the applicant or the sponsoring council after the grant is spent.  
* Supported by competitive quotations.

Rainwater tanks and a shade structure therefore qualify as eligible costs. The budget line items should be broken down as follows:

* Procurement of tanks (including delivery).  
* Installation labour (licensed contractors).  
* Shade‑structure materials and erection.  
* Minor ancillary items (e.g., mounting brackets, waterproofing).  

Operating costs such as staff salaries, routine garden maintenance, or marketing are not eligible, although a small proportion (up to 10 % of the total budget) may be allocated to project management and reporting.

**4. Supporting documentation**  
When you submit the full application, please include the following:

1. **Sponsor Declaration Form** signed by the council’s authorized officer.  
2. **Proof of council sponsorship** – a letter of support on council letterhead confirming insurance coverage and the council’s commitment to act as sponsor.  
3. **Financial statements** for the most recent financial year (income‑statement and balance sheet) to demonstrate turnover.  
4. **Project budget** – a detailed line‑item budget with cost estimates and at least three competitive quotes for the tanks and shade structure.  
5. **Project plan** – a brief narrative (maximum 1,000 words) outlining objectives, timeline, risk assessment, and how the infrastructure will improve resilience.  
6. **Organisational overview** – a description of your garden’s governance structure, volunteer base, and community engagement activities.  
7. **Risk & Safeguarding policy** – the council’s policy can be attached; otherwise a summary of how health and safety will be managed during installation.

All documents should be uploaded in PDF format, and the total file size should not exceed 10 MB.
