Grant writing is not merely a writing task; it is a strategic process of alignment. A successful grant writer does not "ask for money"; they "propose a partnership" to solve a specific problem. This guide breaks down the lifecycle of a grant into five critical phases.

## Phase 1.5: The Prospect Checklist
Before moving to the writing phase, ensure the prospect passes this test:
- [ ] The funder’s mission overlaps with our program mission.
- [ ] Their average grant size matches our requested amount.
- [ ] They have funded organizations of our size/type before.
- [ ] We have met the eligibility requirements (501(c)(3) status, geographic area, etc.).
