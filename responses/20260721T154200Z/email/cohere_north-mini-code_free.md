**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our grant program welcomes applications from a broad spectrum of community‑based organisations. An unincorporated group that operates under the auspices of a local council is indeed eligible, provided that the council can demonstrate its support for the project and, where required, act as the fiscal sponsor or guarantor for any grant funds. In practice, this usually means that the council will need to sign a brief endorsement letter confirming that it will hold the insurance and any other risk‑management responsibilities on behalf of the group. This arrangement satisfies our requirement that grant recipients have appropriate liability coverage and a clear governance structure.

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The Community Resilience Grant does not prescribe a fixed grant size. Instead, we assess each proposal against the funding envelope available for the round and the scope of the anticipated outcomes. Historically, awards have ranged from approximately $5,000 to $50,000, but we have approved projects with both smaller and larger requests when the budget justified it. Our guideline is to fund up to 50 % of the total project cost, with the remainder expected to be sourced from other public, private, or in‑kind contributions. If your preliminary cost estimate for the rainwater tanks and shade structure totals around $30,000, a grant of $15,000 would be within the typical proportion we consider.

**Eligibility of infrastructure such as rainwater tanks and shade structures**  
Infrastructure that directly enhances the resilience and sustainability of community assets is expressly allowable under the “Climate‑Adaptation and Environmental Stewardship” component of the grant. Rainwater harvesting systems and durable shade structures clearly fall within this category because they:  

1. Reduce water consumption and reliance on municipal supplies, thereby mitigating the impact of hotter summer periods.  
2. Provide protection for both volunteers and produce, extending the garden’s operational season.  
3. Contribute to broader climate‑adaptation goals by decreasing heat‑related stress on the site and promoting water‑wise practices.  

All such capital items must be itemized with clear quotations, and you should be prepared to demonstrate how each component aligns with the grant’s outcome indicators (e.g., increased water security, reduced energy use, enhanced community access).

**Supporting documents to prepare**  
To strengthen your application, we recommend compiling the following materials:  

- **Project Narrative** – a concise description of the problem, objectives, implementation timeline, and expected benefits.  
- **Budget and Financial Plan** – detailed cost breakdown for tanks, shade structure, installation, and any ancillary expenses, together with a funding split showing the grant request versus other sources.  
- **Council Endorsement Letter** – a formal statement from your local council confirming its support, its role in holding insurance, and any other relevant commitments.  
- **Organizational Profile** – a short constitution or set of operating guidelines, even if the group is unincorporated, to demonstrate governance and volunteer structure.  
- **Insurance Certificate** – a copy of the council’s liability insurance policy (or a letter confirming coverage) that names the garden as an additional insured where appropriate.  
- **Community Support Evidence** – letters of support from local businesses, neighbouring groups, or resident associations, as well as any signed agreements with stakeholders.  
- **Environmental Assessment** – a brief impact statement outlining water savings, heat‑mitigation effects, and any ecological benefits of the proposed installations.  
- **Staffing and Volunteer Plan** – a timeline indicating who will oversee installation, maintenance, and ongoing operation of the new infrastructure.  

We also advise that you review the full grant guidelines on our website (Section III – Eligibility and Section IV – Acceptable Costs) to ensure that no other criteria—such as a requirement for registered charity status—are relevant to your situation.
