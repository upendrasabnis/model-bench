Subject: Re: Question about eligibility for the Community Resilience Grant  

Dear Jamie,

Thank you for reaching out and for your interest in the Community Resilience Grant. I appreciate the clarity of your inquiry and have prepared a detailed response to each of the points you raised.

**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our funding guidelines allow applications from entities that are *formally* incorporated under the Corporations Act 2001 or that are recognized as a *registered charitable organisation*. However, we also consider applications from unincorporated groups that are **directly sponsored by a local council**, provided the council assumes responsibility for the group’s legal and insurance obligations. Since your garden operates under the auspices of the regional council and the council holds the necessary public liability insurance on your behalf, you meet the sponsorship requirement. You will need to supply a copy of the council’s sponsorship letter and evidence of the council’s insurance coverage as part of your submission.

**2. Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The current round of the Community Resilience Grant offers funding between **$5,000 and $25,000**. Projects that demonstrate a clear, measurable impact on community resilience are prioritized, and the amount awarded is matched to the scope of the proposed work. Your proposed installation of rain‑water tanks and a shade structure falls comfortably within the eligible budget range, and we anticipate a grant of approximately $12,000–$18,000 for a project of this nature, subject to the final budgeting details you provide.

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Infrastructure that directly enhances the community’s capacity to adapt to climate‑related stressors is eligible, provided it is **new, purpose‑built, and not already funded by another source**. Rain‑water harvesting systems and shade structures are expressly covered under the “resilience‑enhancing infrastructure” category, so long as they are integral to maintaining the garden’s operation during extreme weather events.

**4. Supporting documentation**  
To accompany your application, we recommend the following documents:  

- A signed sponsorship letter from the council confirming its role and insurance coverage.  
- A detailed project budget that breaks down costs for the rain‑water tanks, shade structure, site preparation, and any ancillary expenses.  
- Photographs or site plans illustrating the current garden layout and the proposed improvements.  
- Evidence of your annual turnover and financial statements for the past two financial years, demonstrating the $40,000 revenue stream you described.  
- A concise project timeline (e.g., procurement, installation, commissioning) and a risk‑mitigation plan that outlines how the project will increase community resilience to heat stress.  
- Letters of support from at least two community stakeholders (e.g., local residents, council representatives, or partner organisations).  

Please feel free to submit a draft of your application for a pre‑submission review if you would like feedback before the formal deadline. We are happy to arrange a brief phone call or virtual meeting to walk through any of the required materials in more detail.

Thank you again for considering the Community Resilience Grant. I look forward to seeing how your garden’s proposed enhancements will contribute to a more sustainable and heat‑resilient community space.
