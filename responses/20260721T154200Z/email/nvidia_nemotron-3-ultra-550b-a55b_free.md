Subject: Re: Question about eligibility for the Community Resilience Grant

Dear Jamie,

Thank you for your enquiry regarding the Community Resilience Grant round. I appreciate you reaching out before investing time in a full application, and I am happy to address each of your questions in turn.

First, regarding your organisational structure: unincorporated groups operating under a formal auspicing arrangement with a local government authority are eligible to apply, provided the auspicing body (in this case, your local council) enters into a legally binding funding agreement on your behalf. The council must hold appropriate public liability insurance and agree to accept financial and legal responsibility for the grant. You will need to provide a letter from the council confirming this arrangement, signed by an authorised delegate, along with a copy of the council’s current certificate of currency for public liability insurance.

Second, the Community Resilience Grant offers funding between $5,000 and $50,000 per project. There is no minimum total project cost, but the grant cannot exceed 80% of eligible project expenditure; the remaining 20% (or more) must be contributed by the applicant or auspicing body through cash, in-kind contributions, or volunteer labour valued at the current Australian Bureau of Statistics volunteer rate.

Third, the infrastructure you describe—rainwater tanks and a permanent shade structure—falls squarely within eligible capital works. Eligible costs include purchase and installation of water storage systems, shade sails or fixed structures, associated plumbing, engineering certification, and necessary council permits. Ineligible costs include routine maintenance, operational salaries, and retrospective expenditure incurred before a funding agreement is executed.

Finally, to streamline your application, please prepare the following supporting documents: the council auspicing letter and insurance certificate mentioned above; your group’s most recent financial statement or treasurer’s report showing the $40,000 turnover; a simple project plan with timeline, risk register, and itemised budget (including quotes from at least two suppliers for each major item); evidence of land tenure or a licence to occupy the garden site; and letters of support from at least two community stakeholders demonstrating broader benefit.

The application portal opens on 1 June and closes at 5:00 pm AEST on 30 June.
