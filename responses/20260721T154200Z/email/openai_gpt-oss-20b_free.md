Thank you for reaching out and for the important work your volunteer‑run garden is doing in our regional community. I’m happy to clarify the eligibility criteria for the Community Resilience Grant (CRG) and to outline the documentation that will strengthen your application.

**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
The CRG is open to *all community organisations* that can demonstrate a clear public benefit. Unincorporated groups that operate under the auspices of a local council are eligible, provided that the council can act as the legal bearer of the grant. In practice this means the council must sign a Memorandum of Agreement (MoA) confirming that they will manage the funds, hold insurance, and report on the project’s outcomes. As you noted, the council already holds the relevant insurances, which is a strong foundation for the MoA.

**2. Turnover limits**  
The grant’s eligibility criteria set a *maximum annual turnover of $50,000* for applicant organisations. Your reported turnover of approximately $40,000 falls comfortably within this threshold. We do not impose a minimum turnover, so your garden’s revenue stream is acceptable.

**3. Grant amount range**  
The CRG offers awards from **$3,000 to $10,000** per project, depending on the scope and cost of the proposed infrastructure. Given that rainwater tanks and a shade structure are core resilience measures, you should aim to demonstrate a cost estimate that justifies the requested amount—typically at least 30 % of the total project cost can be covered by the CRG when matched by a council or community contribution.

**4. Eligible costs**  
Infrastructure that enhances community resilience—such as rainwater harvesting systems, climate‑resilient shade structures, and related site works—are explicitly listed as eligible. The grant also covers associated labour, professional fees, and maintenance plans, provided they are directly linked to the resilience outcome.

**5. Supporting documentation**  
To prepare a robust application, please gather the following:

| Item | Purpose | Suggested Format |

| Insurance evidence | Shows risk coverage | Current council insurance policy |
| Community support letters | Demonstrates stakeholder buy‑in | Signed letters from volunteers, local businesses, or residents |
| Sustainability plan | Outlines long‑term maintenance | 1‑page narrative |
