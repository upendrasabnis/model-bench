## 1. Introduction: The Lifecycle of a Grant

Most organizations view the grant lifecycle as a straight line: *Find Opportunity → Write Proposal → Get Funded → Do Work → Submit Report.* In reality, the lifecycle is a continuous loop of **compliance, monitoring, and stewardship**. The "Acquittal Phase" is not the administrative tail of the dog; it is the mechanism by which trust is verified, public or private funds are accounted for, and future funding is secured.

**Defining the Stakes**
*   **Government Grants:** Non-compliance triggers the *False Claims Act* (US), *Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act (PGPA Act)* (Australia), or *Managing Public Money* guidelines (UK). Consequences include debarment, financial penalties, criminal prosecution, and reputational destruction.
*   **Philanthropic Grants:** While rarely carrying criminal penalties, failure to acquit properly results in "grantor fatigue," declined renewal applications, and damage to the organization’s credibility within the funding ecosystem (e.g., GuideStar/Candid profiles, GrantAdvisor reviews).

**The "Acquittal Gap"**
Research from the *Center for Effective Philanthropy* and various Auditors General consistently identifies a disconnect: **Funders ask for outcomes; grantees report on activities.** This guide bridges that gap, providing a technical framework for producing acquittals that satisfy legal requirements *and* demonstrate strategic impact.

## 2. Demystifying Acquittal Reporting: More Than Just a Receipt

### 2.1 What is an Acquittal?
**Acquittal** (used interchangeably with *Close-out Report*, *Final Financial Report (FFR)*, *Grant Completion Report*, or *Expenditure Verification*) is the formal process of demonstrating to the funder that:
1.  **Funds were spent:** Exclusively on eligible activities defined in the Grant Agreement.
2.  **Funds were spent correctly:** In accordance with the approved budget, procurement rules, and cost principles (e.g., 2 CFR 200 in the US, HM Treasury Green Book in UK).
3.  **Deliverables were met:** The project achieved the Outputs (tangible products) and progressed toward Outcomes (changes) defined in the Logic Model/Theory of Change.
4.  **Assets are accounted for:** Equipment purchased (>$5k threshold typically) is tracked, labeled, and dispositioned per funder policy.

### 2.2 Types of Acquittals
| Type | Timing | Purpose | Typical Complexity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Progress / Interim Acquittal** | Quarterly, Semi-Annually, or at Milestones | Cash flow release (reimbursement or next installment); Early risk detection. | Medium: Focus on burn rate vs. timeline, deliverable status. |
| **Final Financial Acquittal** | 30–90 days post-project end | Final reconciliation of actuals vs. budget; Return of unspent funds. | High: Requires General Ledger (GL) reconciliation, asset register, audit certs. |
| **Final Performance Acquittal** | 30–120 days post-project end | Narrative of impact; Data against KPIs; Lessons learned. | High: Requires M&E data synthesis, beneficiary verification. |
| **Special / Forensic Acquittal** | Ad-hoc (Triggered by audit finding) | Investigation of specific cost categories (e.g., travel, sub-recipients). | Very High: Requires source documentation (receipts, timesheets, contracts). |

### 2.3 The Legal Construct: The Grant Agreement as Contract
The Grant Agreement (or Deed, Contract, Award Letter) is the supreme governing document. It incorporates by reference:
*   **General Terms & Conditions (GT&Cs):** Standard boilerplate (intellectual property, indemnity, termination).
*   **Specific Conditions:** Budget schedules, reporting dates, special audit requirements, key personnel requirements.
*   **Guidelines/Policy References:** "The Grantee agrees to comply with [Funder’s] Grants Administration Guide v4.2."

> **Pro Tip:** Never sign a Grant Agreement without reading the *incorporated guidelines*. The Agreement is often 10 pages; the Guidelines are 100. The Guidelines contain the actual acquittal rules (e.g., "What constitutes a valid receipt?").

## 3. The Regulatory Landscape: Government vs. Philanthropic Requirements

While the *principles* of accountability are universal (transparency, probity, value for money), the *mechanics* differ significantly.

### 3.1 Government Funders: Compliance as Law
Government grants are public money. Compliance is not optional; it is statutory.

#### United States: The "Super Circular" (2 CFR Part 200)
*   **Cost Principles (Subpart E):** Defines Allowable vs. Unallowable Costs (e.g., lobbying, alcohol, entertainment, fines/penalties are explicitly unallowable).
*   **Procurement Standards (Subpart D):** Requires free and open competition. Micro-purchases (<$10k), Simplified Acquisition ($10k–$250k), Formal Procurement (>$250k).
*   **Single Audit (Subpart F):** If you expend >$750k in federal awards/year, you need a Single Audit (formerly A-133). The Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (SEFA) is the acquittal backbone.
*   **Indirect Costs (F&A):** Negotiated Rate Agreement (NICRA) vs. De Minimis 10% MTDC. **Critical:** You cannot charge indirect costs on equipment, capital expenditures, participant support costs, or sub-award amounts >$25k each.

#### Australia: The PGPA Act & CGRGs
*   **Commonwealth Grants Rules and Guidelines (CGRGs):** Mandate probity, value for money, and proportionality.
*   **GrantConnect:** Mandatory publishing of grant opportunities and awards (transparency).
*   **Acquittal Requirements:** Certified financial statements (often by CPA/CA), performance reports against the *Grant Activity Schedule*. Strong focus on *conflict of interest* declarations throughout the lifecycle.

#### United Kingdom: Managing Public Money & Grant Standards
*   **HM Treasury "Managing Public Money":** Regularity, Propriety, Value for Money (VfM).
*   **Grant vs. Contract Distinction:** Grants = "Gift" for public purpose (no VAT typically); Contracts = Purchase of goods/services (VAT applies). Misclassification is a major compliance risk.
*   **Subsidy Control Act 2022:** Post-Brexit state aid rules. Acquittals must prove no distortion of competition.

#### European Union: The Grant Agreement (GA) & Annotated Model Grant Agreement (AGA)
*   **Eligibility Window:** Costs incurred only during the project duration (with rare exceptions for final reporting).
*   **Actual Cost Basis:** Generally no unit costs/flat rates unless pre-approved (except Horizon Europe lump sums).
*   **Certificate on the Financial Statements (CFS):** Required for thresholds >€325k (varies by program). Must be issued by an external auditor.

### 3.2 Philanthropic Funders: Compliance as Relationship
Foundations (Private, Corporate, Community) and High Net Worth Individuals (HNWI) operate under contract law and IRS/Charity Commission regulations, but with more flexibility.

| Dimension | Government | Philanthropic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Budget Flexibility** | Rigid. Line-item transfers often require formal amendment (>10% or $25k rule). | Flexible. Often "budget agnostic" within total award; 10-20% variance tolerance without approval. |
| **Indirect Costs** | Strict policy (NICRA, De Minimis, or 0%). | Increasingly allowing "True Cost" / Full Cost Recovery (e.g., Ford Foundation, MacArthur, Gates Foundation). |
| **Reporting Format** | Prescribed forms (SF-425, PMS, GrantSolutions, specific portals). | Custom templates, Narrative heavy, "Letters of Inquiry" style updates. |
| **Audit Requirement** | Statutory (Single Audit, CFS). | Rarely required unless >$500k–$1M. Usually "Review" or "Compilation" accepted. |
| **Intellectual Property** | Government usually retains royalty-free license (Bayh-Dole Act US). | Funder often requires Open Access (Creative Commons) or specific licensing. |
| **Unspent Funds** | Must be returned to Treasury (US) / Consolidated Revenue Fund (AU/UK). | Often negotiable: "No-cost extension," "Re-budget to Phase 2," or "General Operating Support." |

### 3.3 The Hybrid Space: Sub-recipients and Pass-Through Entities
If you are a Prime Recipient passing funds to Sub-recipients (Sub-grantees), **you are the government to them.**
*   **Flow-down Clauses:** You must impose 2 CFR 200 (or equivalent) requirements on subs.
*   **Monitoring:** You are required to monitor sub-recipient risk (pre-award assessments) and collect *their* acquittals before you can submit yours.
*   **SEFA/Schedule Mapping:** Sub-award expenditures appear on *your* SEFA (US) or Financial Statements (AU/UK) as "Grants to Sub-recipients," not as your direct program costs.

## 4. The Architecture of Compliance: Building a Record-Keeping System

You cannot acquit what you cannot find. Record-keeping is not an administrative chore; it is the **infrastructure of evidence**.

### 4.1 The "Golden Rule": Contemporaneous Documentation
*Auditors do not accept reconstructions.* A timesheet signed on Friday for Monday’s work is a finding. A receipt photographed at the point of sale is evidence.

### 4.2 The Document Hierarchy (Source to Summary)
1.  **Source Documents (Primary Evidence):**
    *   Invoices/Receipts (Itemized, GST/VAT compliant).
    *   Timesheets (Daily, signed by employee *and* supervisor, coded to grant project code).
    *   Contracts/POs (Signed, with insurance certs, conflict of interest checks).
    *   Bank Statements (Showing specific deposit/withdrawal).
    *   Board Minutes (Approving budget, conflict declarations, executive pay).
    *   Beneficiary Lists / Attendance Sheets (Signed, dated, linked to activity).
2.  **Transaction Records (Accounting Layer):**
    *   General Ledger (GL) Detail / Trial Balance.
    *   Sub-ledgers (Payroll, AP, AR, Fixed Assets).
    *   Journal Entries (With narrative explanations for allocations).
3.  **Reconciliation Workpapers (The Bridge):**
    *   **Grant Ledger to GL Reconciliation:** The single most critical workpaper. Proves the "Grant Bucket" matches the "Accounting Bucket."
    *   Payroll Allocation Workpapers: How % effort was calculated.
    *   Indirect Cost Rate Application Workpapers: MTDC base calculation.
4.  **Summary Reports (The Output):**
    *   Financial Acquittal Statement (Budget vs. Actual).
    *   Performance Dashboard.
    *   Audit Certification.

### 4.3 Chart of Accounts (CoA) Design for Grants
**Failure Mode:** Using a single "Grant Expense" account code.
**Best Practice:** Segment the CoA using **Dimensions** (not just account numbers).
*   **Fund/Source:** `FED-DOE-2024`, `FOUND-SMITH-2023`, `UNRESTRICTED`.
*   **Project/Activity:** `PROJ-ALPHA`, `PROJ-BETA-ADMIN`.
*   **Cost Category (Natural Account):** `6100-Salaries`, `6200-Fringe`, `6300-Travel`, `6400-Equipment`, `6500-Subcontracts`, `6600-Indirect`.
*   **Function (Optional but recommended for US Nonprofits):** `PGM-Program`, `MGT-Management`, `FND-Fundraising` (For Form 990/Statement of Functional Expenses).

*Example GL String:* `FED-DOE-2024` - `PROJ-ALPHA` - `6100-Salaries` - `PGM-Program`.

### 4.4 Document Retention Policies
| Record Type | US (Federal) | Australia (CGRG/ATO) | UK (HMRC/Charity Comm) | Best Practice |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Financial Records / Source Docs** | 3 years from final FFR submission (2 CFR 200.334) | 5 years (CGRG) / 7 years (Tax) | 6 years (HMRC) / 6 years (Charity) | **7 Years Post-Final-Acquittal** |
| **Property/Equipment Records** | 3 years post-disposition | 5 years post-disposition | 6 years post-disposition | **Life of Asset + 7 Years** |
| **Personnel / Timesheets** | 3 years (FLSA/Payroll) | 7 years (Fair Work) | 3-6 years | **7 Years** |
| **Audit Reports / Findings** | Indefinite / 3 years | Indefinite | Indefinite | **Permanent** |
| **Correspondence (Funder Approvals)** | 3 years | 5 years | 6 years | **Permanent (Email Archiving)** |

> **Critical Note:** If litigation, claim, or audit is *started* before the retention period expires, records must be retained until **all issues are resolved and final action taken** (2 CFR 200.334).

### 4.5 Digital vs. Physical: The "Original" Debate
*   **US/UK/AU:** Electronic records are legally equivalent to paper *if* they meet authenticity, integrity, and accessibility standards (e.g., ISO 15489, IRS Rev. Proc. 97-22).
*   **Requirement:** Audit trail showing *who* scanned, *when*, and that the image is a true copy. Simple photos on a phone are risky; use a Document Management System (DMS) with OCR and version control.

## 5. Financial Acquittals: Line-Item Accuracy, Budget Variances, and In-Kind Valuation

The Financial Acquittal is a mathematical proof: **Σ(Actual Eligible Costs) ≤ Approved Budget Total.**

### 5.1 The Reconciliation Process (Step-by-Step)
1.  **Lock the Period:** Close the accounting period in the ERP. No more journals to the grant fund.
2.  **Extract GL Detail:** Pull every transaction for the Grant Fund/Project codes for the project dates.
3.  **Filter for Eligibility:** Remove unallowable costs found in GL (e.g., the $200 team lunch coded to "Meetings," the parking fine, the lobbying expense).
4.  **Allocate Shared Costs:** Apply approved methodology for Rent, Utilities, Insurance, IT (Occupancy/Indirect).
5.  **Reconcile Payroll:** Match Timesheet Hours → Payroll Register → GL Salary Expense. *Variance must be $0.*
6.  **Reconcile Sub-recipients:** Confirm Sub-recipient invoices paid = Sub-recipient expenses reported + Cash on Hand at Sub.
7.  **Calculate Indirect Costs:** Apply Negotiated Rate to Modified Total Direct Cost (MTDC) Base. Verify base exclusions (Equipment, Participant Support, Sub-award >$25k).
8.  **Compare to Budget:** Map to Acquittal Template:** Map your CoA natural accounts to the Funder’s Budget Categories (Personnel, Fringe, Travel, Equipment, Supplies, Contractual, Other, Indirect).
9.  **Calculate Variance:** `Budget - Actual = Variance`. Explain variances >10% (or funder threshold).

### 5.2 Budget Variances: The "Materiality" Threshold
*   **Government:** Usually requires prior approval for:
    *   Scope change.
    *   Key Personnel change.
    *   **Budget deviation > 10% or $25,000 (whichever is less) on a specific category** (2 CFR 200.308).
    *   *Acquittal Strategy:* If you didn't get approval, disclose the variance in the acquittal with a "Reason for Variance" narrative. Do not hide it. Hiding it = Fraud risk.
*   **Philanthropy:** Often 20% flexibility. Report variance; ask for carryover.

### 5.3 Common Cost Eligibility Traps (The "Unallowable" List)
| Cost Category | Government (2 CFR 200 / PGPA) | Philanthropy (Typical) | Acquittal Defense Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Alcohol** | **Strictly Unallowable.** | Usually Unallowable. | Never code to grant. Use "Unrestricted" fund code. |
| **Lobbying** | **Unallowable** (Federal funds). | Often Restricted (IRS 501c3 rules). | Track separately. Zero charge to grant. |
| **Entertainment** | **Unallowable.** | Rarely Allowed. | Code "Meals" only if working meal (agenda/minutes attached). |
| **Fines/Penalties** | **Unallowable.** | Unallowable. | Pay from Unrestricted. |
| **Fundraising** | **Unallowable.** | Unallowable (Grant funds for fundraising). | Functional allocation to "Fundraising" cost center. |
| **Foreign Travel** | Fly America Act (US) / Economy Class mandates. | Usually Economy. | Document waiver approval *before* booking. |
| **Equipment (>$5k/unit)** | Capitalize. Title usually vests in Grantee (US) or Funder (AU/UK). | Varies. | Maintain Asset Register. Acquit *depreciation* or *full cost* per agreement. |
| **Participant Support** | Stipends, Tuition, Travel for *trainees* (not staff). **Excluded from Indirect Base.** | Often allowed. | Separate GL Account Code (e.g., 6700-PartSupport). Critical for Indirect calc. |

### 5.4 In-Kind Contributions (Matching / Cost Share)
Many grants require "Match" (e.g., 20% Non-Federal Share).
*   **Valuation Rules (2 CFR 200.306 / ATO Guidelines):**
    *   **Volunteer Time:** Value at *comparable replacement cost* (what you would pay to hire someone), not "market rate for a CEO." Use Independent Sector rate (US) or ABS wage data (AU) only for *unskilled* labor. Skilled labor (Doctor, Lawyer, IT Pro bono) = their *actual* billing rate.
    *   **Donated Goods:** Fair Market Value (FMV) at time of donation. Require donor letter stating FMV.
    *   **Donated Space:** Rental value of comparable space. Requires independent appraisal or comparable listings.
*   **Documentation:** **In-kind must be documented to the same standard as cash.** Timesheets for volunteers. Appraisals for goods/space.
*   **Acquittal Reporting:** Report on a separate schedule: `Cash Expenditures | In-Kind Match | Total Project Cost`. Do not net them in the General Ledger (Track in Memo/Statistical accounts).

### 5.5 Program Income
*   **Definition:** Revenue generated *by* the grant-funded activity (e.g., workshop fees, publication sales, clinic fees).
*   **Treatment Options (Defined in Agreement):**
    1.  **Deduction:** Reduce grant drawdown by income amount.
    2.  **Addition:** Add to project budget (increases scope).
    3.  **Cost Sharing/Match:** Count toward match requirement.
*   **Acquittal:** Must track Program Income in a separate GL account. Report Gross Income and Disposition.

## 6. Performance Acquittals: Measuring Impact, Outputs, and Outcomes

Financial acquittal proves *honesty*; Performance acquittal proves *competence*.

### 6.1 The Logic Model Framework
Your acquittal must map directly to the Logic Model submitted in the proposal.
*   **Inputs:** Funds, Staff, Partners (Covered in Financial Acquittal).
*   **Activities:** Workshops held, Reports written, Wells drilled.
*   **Outputs (Quantitative):** *#* of people trained, *#* of wells drilled, *#* of reports delivered. **Counting.**
*   **Outcomes (Qualitative/Quantitative):** *%* increase in income, *%* reduction in water-borne illness, Policy changed. **Measuring Change.**
*   **Impact:** Long-term systemic change (Rarely acquitted within grant period).

### 6.2 Data Quality Standards for Acquittal
**The "Beneficiary Verification" Test:** Can you produce a list of every individual counted in "Output: 500 Farmers Trained" with Name, Date, Location, Signature/Thumbprint?
*   **Government:** Increasingly requires **Unit Record Data** (de-identified) submission, not just aggregates.
*   **Philanthropy:** Requires "Stories of Change" + Aggregate Data.

### 6.3 Handling "Failed" Targets
*   **Scenario:** Target: 100 Jobs Created. Actual: 45.
*   **Bad Acquittal:** "We created 45 jobs. Economy was bad." (Excuse).
*   **Good Acquittal:**
    1.  Report 45 transparently.
    2.  **Variance Analysis:** "Recruitment delayed 3 months due to Partner X license delay (see correspondence dated...)."
    3.  **Mitigation:** "Extended training curriculum to include digital skills based on employer feedback."
    4.  **Revised Projection:** "Pipeline shows 80 committed hires for Q1 Next Year."
    5.  **Lesson Learned:** "Future proposals will include 6-month lead time for partner onboarding."

### 6.4 Qualitative Evidence: The Narrative Arc
Funders read the narrative *first*, numbers *second*.
*   **Structure:** Context → Challenge → Action → Result → Evidence → Reflection.
*   **Evidence Attachments:** Photos (geo-tagged), Testimonials (signed release forms), Media Clippings, Policy Briefs, Dataset DOIs.
*   **Gender/Disability/Inclusion (GEDSI) Disaggregation:** Mandatory for most Govt/Big Philanthropy. Report: `Total | Male | Female | Non-Binary | PWD | Indigenous | Youth`.

## 7. The Audit Gauntlet: Preparing for Financial and Performance Audits

An audit is not a "gotcha" moment; it is a verification of your internal controls. There are three distinct audit types you will face.

### 7.1 Type 1: The Single Audit / Statutory Audit (The Big One)
*   **Trigger:** Expenditure thresholds ($750k US, $500k AU State, Statutory requirement UK/EU).
*   **Scope:** Financial Statements + Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (SEFA) + Compliance with 12 Compliance Requirements (Activities Allowed, Allowable Costs, Cash Management, Eligibility, Equipment, Matching, Period of Performance, Procurement, Program Income, Reporting, Subrecipient Monitoring, Special Tests).
*   **Output:** Auditor’s Report (Unmodified/Modified/Adverse), Schedule of Findings and Questioned Costs (SFQC).
*   **Materiality:** Usually ~3-5% of Federal Expenditures or $25k (whichever lower) for Major Programs.

### 7.2 Type 2: The Funder-Specific Audit / Desk Review
*   **Trigger:** High-risk grantee, Random selection, Specific program requirement (e.g., NIH, NSF, EU Horizon CFS).
*   **Scope:** Deep dive into *one specific grant*.
*   **Methodology:** Auditor requests "PBC" (Prepared by Client) list. You upload to portal.
*   **Focus:** Cost allowability, Time & Effort reporting, Procurement compliance, Sub-recipient flow-down.

### 7.3 Type 3: Performance / Value-for-Money (VfM) Audit
*   **Scope:** Economy (Minimizing cost), Efficiency (Maximizing output/input), Effectiveness (Achieving outcomes).
*   **Methodology:** Site visits, Beneficiary interviews, Data analytics, Process mapping.
*   **Output:** Management Letter with "Recommendations" (not "Findings").

### 7.4 The "Prepared by Client" (PBC) List: Your Survival Kit
When the audit notification arrives, you have 10-15 business days to upload. **Do not start preparing then.** Maintain a **Permanent Audit File (PAF)** updated quarterly.

**Standard PBC Checklist:**
1.  **Organizational:** Org Chart, Board Roster, Conflict of Interest Policy, Insurance Certs, NICRA/Indirect Rate Agreement.
2.  **Governance:** Board Minutes (approving budget, audit, executive comp), Audit Committee Charter.
3.  **Financial Systems:** Accounting Manual, Procurement Policy, Travel Policy, Segregation of Duties Matrix.
4.  **Grant Specific:**
    *   Signed Grant Agreement + Amendments.
    *   Approved Budget vs. Actual (Final).
    *   **Reconciliation: GL to Acquittal (The "Lead Schedule").**
    *   Payroll: Timesheets (Sample), Effort Certifications, Payroll Register.
    *   Procurement: 3 largest procurements (RFQ/RFP, Evaluation Scorecards, Contract, Insurance).
    *   Sub-recipients: Risk Assessments, Monitoring Reports, Sub-acquittals.
    *   Equipment: Asset Register, Disposition records.
    *   Bank: Reconciliations for Grant Bank Account (or allocation proof).
    *   Program Income: Ledger + Deposit slips.
    *   In-Kind: Volunteer logs, Appraisal letters.

### 7.5 Managing Audit Findings: The "Corrective Action Plan" (CAP)
If you get a Finding (Non-compliance, Significant Deficiency, Material Weakness, Questioned Costs):
1.  **Don't Panic. Don't Argue.** Read the finding. Agree on the *facts*.
2.  **Root Cause Analysis:** Use "5 Whys." *Symptom:* Missing receipts. *Root Cause:* No corporate card policy enforcement; staff use personal cards and lose receipts.
3.  **CAP Structure:**
    *   **Finding Reference:** #2024-001.
    *   **Description:** $15,000 in travel costs lacked itemized receipts.
    *   **Corrective Action:** Implement Concur/Expensify mandatory e-receipt policy; Auto-reject non-itemized charges; Training session Jan 15.
    *   **Responsible Person:** CFO / Controller.
    *   **Completion Date:** March 31, 2025.
    *   **Verification:** Internal Audit to test 25 transactions in Q2.
4.  **Submit on Time.** Late CAP = High Risk designation for next year.

## 8. The Top 10 Compliance Failures and How to Avoid Them

Based on analysis of Auditors General reports (US GAO, AU ANAO, UK NAO) and Foundation feedback reports.

### 1. The "Time & Effort" Black Hole (Personnel Costs)
*   **Failure:** Salaries charged 100% to Grant, but employee works on 3 programs. No timesheets. Or timesheets filled out annually ("Post-certification").
*   **Regulation:** 2 CFR 200.430 / ATO TR 2017/D6. **After-the-fact (ATF) certification is prohibited for Federal funds.** Must be contemporaneous (at least monthly).
*   **Fix:** Implement **Positive Time Reporting** (Daily timesheets) for split-funded staff. Use "Effort Certification" forms semi-annually for 100% funded staff. *System:* Payroll integration (e.g., ADP, Paycom, Ceridian) with Grant Costing module.

### 2. Procurement "Sole Source" Abuse
*   **Failure:** "We only use Vendor X." No quotes. No Sole Source Justification (SSJ) memo. Contract signed *before* grant award (Pre-award cost risk).
*   **Regulation:** 2 CFR 200.319 (Competition) / 200.320 (Methods). Micro-purchase <$10k (no quotes needed but equitable distribution required). Simplified >$10k (3 quotes). Formal >$250k (RFP/IFB).
*   **Fix:** **Procurement Threshold Cheat Sheet** posted in finance office. Mandatory SSJ Form for any non-competitive award >$10k. Legal review for contracts >$50k.

### 3. Indirect Cost (F&A) Miscalculation
*   **Failure:** Charging Indirect on Equipment ($50k microscope), Participant Stipends ($100k), or Sub-award ($200k pass-through).
*   **Regulation:** MTDC Base Exclusions (2 CFR 200.68 / 200.414).
*   **Fix:** **Automated MTDC Calculator** in ERP. Tag accounts: `MTDC_Include = Yes/No`. Monthly reconciliation of Indirect Pool vs. Base.

### 4. The "Unspent Funds" Shell Game
*   **Failure:** Spending frantically in Month 12 on "Supplies" (buying 5 years of toner) to zero out budget. Or holding cash in separate bank account earning interest not reported.
*   **Regulation:** Period of Performance (2 CFR 200.309 / 200.403). Costs must be incurred *during* the period. "Incurred" = Goods received/Services rendered.
*   **Fix:** **Accrual Accounting at Year-End.** Only accrue *bona fide* obligations (PO issued, service contract signed). Return unspent cash *immediately* with Final FFR. Report Interest Income as Program Income.

### 5. Sub-recipient "Pass-Through" Blindness
*   **Failure:** Prime sends money to Sub. Sub sends invoice. Prime pays. Prime never checks Sub's invoices, timesheets, or audit. Sub misses Single Audit threshold.
*   **Regulation:** 2 CFR 200.331 (Requirements for Pass-through Entities). **Prime is liable for Sub's questioned costs.**
*   **Fix:**
    1.  Pre-award Risk Assessment (Financial stability, Audit history, Internal controls).
    2.  Sub-award Agreement with Flow-down clauses.
    3.  **During Award:** Desk reviews of Sub invoices (sample 25%). Site visits for High Risk.
    4.  **Post Award:** Collect Sub's Audit Report / Single Audit. Issue Management Decision on Findings within 6 months.

### 6. Conflict of Interest (COI) Non-Disclosure
*   **Failure:** PI hires spouse’s consulting firm. Board member’s company wins equipment bid. No COI form on file.
