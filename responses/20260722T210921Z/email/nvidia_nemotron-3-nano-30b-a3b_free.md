Subject: Re: Question about eligibility for the Community Resilience Grant  

Dear Jamie,  

Thank you for reaching out and for the detailed overview of your volunteer‑run community garden. I’m pleased to clarify the points you raised regarding the upcoming Community Resilience Grant round.  

**1. Eligibility of groups auspiced by a council**  
Our funding guidelines expressly include “unincorporated community groups that operate under the auspices of a local council” provided that the council’s insurance and financial oversight cover the group’s activities. Because your garden is administered through the council and the council holds the necessary public liability and asset insurance, you meet the core eligibility criteria. Please note that the council must be listed as the “umbrella organisation” on the application form and must sign off on the project budget.  

**2. Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The current funding round allocates grants ranging from **$5,000 to $25,000**. Applications that request less than $5,000 will not be considered, while projects seeking up to $25,000 may be funded, subject to the overall budget envelope and merit of the proposal.  

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Infrastructure that directly enhances community resilience—such as rainwater harvesting systems, shade structures, solar panels, or flood‑mitigation works—is eligible for funding. The key requirement is that the work must be **permanent, publicly accessible, and demonstrably reduce vulnerability to climate‑related stressors**. Your proposed rainwater tanks and shade canopy fall squarely within this definition, provided they are tied to the garden’s ongoing operation and community benefit.  

**4. Supporting documentation**  
To accompany your application, please include:  

- A signed **Letter of Support** from the council confirming its auspicing role and insurance coverage.  
- A **Project Plan** outlining objectives, timeline, and expected outcomes.  
- A **Detailed Budget** that breaks down costs for the rainwater tanks, shade structure, site preparation, and any associated professional fees.  
- Evidence of **financial capacity** (e.g., recent audited financial statements or a council‑verified statement of assets) showing your annual turnover aligns with the $40,000 figure you quoted.  
- Photographs or site plans of the existing garden and proposed works.  

If you would like a template for any of these items, I can forward them to you promptly.  

Should you decide to proceed, the submission portal will open on **1 October** and close on **31 October**. I encourage you to submit a draft for a pre‑submission review; this can help ensure that all requirements are met before the final deadline.  

Please feel free to contact me directly should any further clarification be needed. I look forward to supporting your garden’s resilience initiatives.
