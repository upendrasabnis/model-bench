**Eligibility of Unincorporated Groups Auspiced by a Council**  
We welcome applications from unincorporated community groups that operate under the auspices of a local government body, provided the council can confirm that it will be the legal recipient of the grant funds and will manage the associated reporting and compliance requirements. In such cases, the council typically acts as the “lead organisation” and is responsible for executing the grant agreement, maintaining insurances, and ensuring that the project aligns with any council policies. To proceed, we will need a brief letter of support from your council confirming their willingness to act in this capacity, as well as a copy of the council’s insurance certificate (or a statement that the council holds the required coverage on your behalf).  

**Grant Amount – Minimum and Maximum**  
Our Community Resilience Grant does not prescribe a fixed minimum or maximum award. Instead, we evaluate each proposal on its merit, community impact, and alignment with the program’s strategic objectives. Historical awards have ranged from approximately $5,000 to $150,000, depending on the scale and complexity of the project. For a rainwater‑tank and shade‑structure initiative of the size you describe, a grant in the $30,000‑$60,000 range would likely be appropriate, though the final amount will be determined during the assessment phase.  

**Eligibility of Infrastructure Costs**  
Yes, capital infrastructure such as rainwater harvesting systems and permanent shade structures is an eligible cost category under the grant. We consider these expenditures as “resilience‑enhancing improvements” that directly support the organization’s ability to continue operations during extreme weather events. Eligible costs typically include:  

1. Purchase and installation of rainwater tanks (including plumbing and storage).  
2. Design, construction, or procurement of shade structures (e.g., pergolas, netting, or permanent awnings).  
3. Associated site works such as excavation, foundations, and minor earthworks.  
4. Project management and professional fees directly related to the infrastructure.  

Please note that we do not fund recurring operational expenses (e.g., water bills, maintenance contracts) unless they are explicitly justified as part of the resilience plan.  

**Supporting Documents to Prepare**  
To strengthen your application, we recommend assembling the following documentation:  

- **Project Proposal Form** (the standard template available on our grants portal).  
- **Budget Breakdown** detailing each infrastructure item, quantities, unit costs, and any contingency.  
- **Letters of Support** from the council (confirming auspice status and insurance) and, if applicable, from partner organisations or community stakeholders.  
- **Organizational Profile** – a one‑page overview of your garden’s mission, volunteer base, and community reach.  
- **Financial Statements** – most recent audited or reviewed financial statements (or, for a small volunteer group, a simple statement of income and expenses for the past 12 months).  
- **Risk Assessment and Mitigation Plan** – outlining potential construction, safety, and environmental risks, together with mitigation strategies.  
- **Implementation Timeline** – a Gantt‑style chart or bullet‑point schedule showing key milestones from procurement through to operational handover.  
- **Evidence of Insurance** – a certificate confirming that the council holds public liability and any necessary work‑site insurance for the project.  

If you have any specific questions about formatting the budget or tailoring the risk assessment to an infrastructure project, please let us know, and we can provide additional guidance.
