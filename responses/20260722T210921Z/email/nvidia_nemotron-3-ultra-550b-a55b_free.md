Subject: Re: Question about eligibility for the Community Resilience Grant

Dear Jamie,

Thank you for your enquiry regarding the Community Resilience Grant round. I appreciate you reaching out to confirm eligibility details before investing time in a full application—this is exactly the kind of due diligence that helps both applicants and our assessment panels. I am pleased to address each of your questions in turn.

**Auspicing Arrangements:** Unincorporated groups operating under a formal auspicing agreement with a local government authority are absolutely eligible to apply, provided the auspicing body (in this case, your local council) holds an Australian Business Number (ABN), appropriate public liability insurance, and agrees to accept legal and financial responsibility for the grant. We will require a signed auspicing agreement or letter of support from the council’s authorised officer confirming these arrangements at the time of application.

**Grant Amounts:** For this round, the minimum request is $5,000 and the maximum is $50,000 (GST exclusive). Your projected budget for rainwater tanks and a shade structure appears to fall comfortably within this range.

**Eligible Costs:** Capital infrastructure that directly enhances community resilience—such as water security installations and heat-mitigation structures—is explicitly listed as an eligible expenditure category. Costs may include materials, contractor labour, necessary permits, and reasonable project management fees. Ongoing operational costs (e.g., utilities, maintenance) are not eligible.

**Supporting Documents:** In addition to the auspicing agreement, please prepare: (1) a project plan with timeline and risk register; (2) a detailed budget with at least two quotes for each major cost item; (3) evidence of land tenure or council permission for the infrastructure; (4) your group’s most recent financial statement (even if unaudited); and (5) letters of community support demonstrating the garden’s reach and volunteer base.

The application portal opens on 1 June and closes at 5:00 pm AEST on 30 June. I strongly recommend drafting your responses in a Word document first, as the portal does not auto-save. If you would like, I can arrange a brief 15-minute video call next week to walk through the budget template or answer any follow-up questions.

We value the work your garden does for local food security and social cohesion, and I look forward to potentially seeing your application.
