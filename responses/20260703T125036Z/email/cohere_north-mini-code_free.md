Dear Jamie,

Thank you for reaching out and sharing an overview of your community garden project. I’m pleased to provide the information you requested and to express our interest in supporting initiatives that enhance local resilience and sustainability.

**Eligibility – unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our grant program welcomes applications from both incorporated not‑for‑profit entities and unincorporated groups that have a formal auspicing arrangement with a public or private body capable of administering funds on your behalf. In your case, the fact that your local council holds the required insurances and can act as the fiscal sponsor should satisfy the eligibility requirement for an unincorporated group. However, we will need a written auspice agreement from the council confirming that it will be responsible for the disbursement of grant funds, for any required reporting, and for ensuring compliance with our grant conditions.

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The Community Resilience Grant is not limited to a fixed amount; awards are determined on a case‑by‑case basis according to the scope of the proposed work, the broader community impact, and the availability of funding in the current round. Past applications of similar size to yours have ranged from approximately $15,000 to $75,000, but we encourage you to propose a budget that reflects the true cost of your rainwater‑tank and shade‑structure installation. If your total project cost falls below our usual minimum of $10,000, we can still consider a partial grant or a multi‑year funding approach.

**Eligibility of the proposed infrastructure**  
Infrastructure such as rainwater harvesting systems and shade structures is explicitly listed as an eligible cost under the “Climate‑Resilient Community Assets” category of this grant. These improvements directly support the continuity of food‑production activities during extreme heat events and align with the grant’s overarching goals of building community resilience to climate variability.

**Supporting documents to prepare**  
To strengthen your application we recommend assembling the following items:

1. **Project description** – a concise narrative outlining the problem you are addressing, the objectives of the rainwater tanks and shade structure, and the expected outcomes.  
2. **Budget and breakdown** – detailed cost estimates for equipment, installation, contingency, and any ongoing maintenance.  
3. **Auspice agreement** – a formal letter from your local council confirming its willingness and capacity to act as fiscal sponsor, including contact details for the responsible officer.  
4. **Insurance certificate** – the council’s current public liability and professional indemnity certificates, noting that they will cover the project activities.  
5. **Financial statements** – the most recent audited or reviewed accounts (or a statement of cash flows) demonstrating your $40,000 annual turnover.  
6. **Community support** – letters of endorsement from local businesses, schools, or resident groups, as well as any membership sign‑up sheets.  
7. **Implementation timeline** – a Gantt‑style chart or work‑plan spreadsheet outlining key milestones, deliverables, and responsible parties.  

We recommend that you compile these documents in a single PDF packet and upload it to our online portal once you are ready to submit a full application. Should you have any further questions about formatting or the content of any of the above items, please do not hesitate to contact me directly.

We look forward to reviewing your proposal and to the possibility of partnering with your garden to help safeguard its operations against the challenges of a warming climate.
