Subject: Re: Question about eligibility for the Community Resilience Grant  

Dear Jamie,

Thank you for reaching out and for your interest in the Community Resilience Grant. I’m pleased to provide clarification on the points you raised so that you can assess the fit of your project before committing to a full application.

**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our funding guidelines allow organisations that are *not* formally incorporated or registered charities to apply, provided they are operating under the auspices of a local government entity that holds the appropriate insurance and has a formal service‑level agreement with the group. Since your garden is administered by the council and the council retains the necessary public liability coverage, you meet the eligibility criteria for this round.

**2. Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The current funding round allocates grants ranging from **$5,000 to $25,000**. Projects that demonstrate a clear link to climate‑resilience outcomes and fit within the budget ceiling are prioritized. Your proposed investment in rainwater tanks and a shade structure falls comfortably within this band, though the final award will be contingent on the overall merit of the application.

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Capital‑cost items that directly enhance the capacity of the organisation to withstand climate‑related stresses are eligible. Rainwater harvesting systems, shade structures, and related site‑improvement works are specifically listed as acceptable expenditures, provided they are essential for the ongoing operation of the community garden.

**4. Supporting documentation to prepare**  
To substantiate your application, please include the following:  

- A copy of the council’s written endorsement confirming the auspicing arrangement and detailing the insurance coverage.  
- Evidence of your current financial statements (the $40,000 turnover) and a brief budget narrative outlining how the grant will be used.  
- Project plans or design sketches for the rainwater tanks and shade structure, with cost estimates and a timeline.  
- A risk‑mitigation or climate‑impact assessment that demonstrates how the proposed works will increase resilience to extreme heat and water scarcity.  
- Any relevant community‑engagement evidence, such as letters of support from neighbours or local stakeholders.

If you would like a sample checklist or templates for these documents, please let me know and I will forward them to you.
