**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups under council auspices**  
The grant is open to any community‑based organisation that is not a for‑profit entity, including unincorporated groups that operate under the auspices of a local council. The key requirement is that the applicant can demonstrate a formal relationship with the council, such as a written letter of support or a statutory agreement that confirms the council’s insurance and governance responsibilities. If you are not yet a registered charity, you can still apply on behalf of the council‑sponsored group, provided you can supply the council’s statutory documents and proof of their insurance coverage.

**2. Grant amount limits**  
The maximum award for a single project is $15,000, while the minimum is $2,500. The amount you may receive will be determined by the scope of your proposal and the available funding for the round, so it is advisable to estimate the cost of the rainwater tanks and shade structure and include a detailed budget in your application.

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Infrastructure such as rainwater tanks, shade structures, and associated civil works are fully eligible. We encourage you to include a breakdown of costs, supplier quotes, and any engineering drawings that support the technical feasibility of the project.

**4. Supporting documents to prepare**  
- A formal letter of support from your local council, signed by an authorised officer, confirming insurance and governance arrangements.  
- A copy of any council‑issued licence or statutory agreement that covers the garden.  
- Proof of your annual turnover (eFig: recent bank statement, or a simple turnover statement).  
- Detailed project description, objectives, and expected outcomes.  
- Itemised budget with supplier quotes for the tanks and shade structure.  
- Project timeline and a simple monitoring plan.  
- Any environmental or community impact assessments, if applicable.
