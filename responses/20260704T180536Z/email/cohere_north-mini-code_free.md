### 2. Qualitative Update (≈ 420 words)

The past six months have been productive but also revealed a few technical bottlenecks that required pragmatic adjustments. Early in the project we anticipated that the custom single‑guide RNA (sgRNA) library would be the rate‑limiting step; however, the synthesis vendor’s turnaround time was longer than projected, prompting us to implement a **dual‑pipeline approach**. While the primary vendor continues to produce the full‑scale library, we have initiated an in‑house oligonucleotide synthesis of a reduced‑size pilot set (12 guides) to keep the downstream cloning workflow moving. This has allowed us to generate the first set of CRISPR‑Cas9 constructs for the “proof‑of‑concept” targets, which we have already validated in a preliminary transient‑expression assay.

A second challenge emerged during the optimization of the **cell‑based reporter assay**. The original design relied on a doxycycline‑inducible Cas9 expression system, but we observed variegated induction across different cell lines, leading to high background and reduced signal‑to‑noise ratios. After a week of troubleshooting, we switched to a **constitutive Cas9‑knock‑in** approach using a CRISPR‑mediated homology‑directed repair (HDR) strategy, which has yielded a more uniform reporter expression. This change required a modest re‑validation of the assay conditions, but the new format now demonstrates a Z′ factor of 0.71, meeting our high‑throughput screening criteria.

Regarding preliminary scientific observations, the initial screening of the pilot construct set has identified **three off‑target sites** with modest activity in a non‑target genomic locus. These findings are being fed back into the guide‑design algorithm, and we are now incorporating a more stringent specificity filter (CFD score < 0.2) for the remaining library. Additionally, a subset of constructs exhibits **synthetic lethality** when combined with a small‑molecule inhibitor of the DNA‑damage response, suggesting a potential combinatorial therapeutic angle that could be explored in the later stages of the project.

Overall, the adjustments have kept us on track to meet the 12‑month milestones, albeit with a slight re‑allocation of resources toward assay robustness and guide specificity. I am confident that with continued support we will achieve the full‑scale HTS campaign by month 9 and generate a robust data set for the final deliverable.
