Thank you for reaching out and for your interest in the Community Resilience Grant. I have reviewed the details of your project and can confirm the following points to assist you in preparing a strong application.

**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
The Community Resilience Grant program explicitly permits applications from unincorporated community groups that are formally *auspiced* by a local government authority, provided that the auspicing body holds the necessary insurance and can demonstrate fiscal oversight. Your arrangement with the council meets these criteria, so you are eligible to apply. However, the application must be submitted under the council’s name, and the council must be listed as the lead applicant on the form.

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The current funding round offers grants ranging from **$5,000 to $25,000**. Projects that fall outside this range are not eligible for consideration. Given that your proposed work—installing rainwater tanks and a shade structure—fits comfortably within this budgetary band, you can aim for the upper tier if you can demonstrate a clear need for the full amount.

**Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Infrastructure that directly enhances the community’s capacity to withstand climate‑related stresses is eligible, including water‑capture systems and shading solutions. Both rainwater tanks and a shaded communal area qualify as “resilience‑building infrastructure” under the program’s guidelines, provided they are integral to the garden’s ongoing operation and not merely decorative.

**Supporting documentation to prepare**  
To substantiate your application, please include the following:  

1. **Letter of auspicing** from the council confirming their role, insurance coverage, and financial oversight.  
2. **Budget breakdown** itemising the costs of the rainwater tanks, shade structure, and any associated installation or design fees.  
3. **Project timeline** outlining procurement, construction, and expected completion dates.  
4. **Community need statement** describing how the garden serves residents and why climate‑resilience measures are essential.  
5. **Evidence of turnover** (e.g., recent financial statements or audited reports) demonstrating the $40,000 annual revenue and its sources.  
6. **Risk mitigation plan** identifying potential challenges (e.g., supply chain delays) and how they will be managed.
