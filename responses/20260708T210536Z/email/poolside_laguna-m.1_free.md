Regarding eligibility, unincorporated groups operating under the auspices of a local council **are eligible** to apply, provided the council serves as the legal entity responsible for the grant agreement and financial management. Since your group operates under council oversight and benefits from their insurance coverage, this arrangement meets our requirements. However, we recommend confirming with your council’s administrative office that they are prepared to act as the grant recipient if your application is successful.

Your annual turnover of $40,000 falls well within the acceptable range for this grant program. There is no minimum turnover threshold, and the vast majority of applicants operate with similar or higher budgets. That said, please ensure your financial statements clearly reflect your revenue sources, as this will strengthen your application.

For the project scope, **infrastructure improvements such as rainwater tanks and shade structures are eligible costs**, provided they directly align with the grant’s objective of enhancing community resilience. These installations clearly support your garden’s sustainability and ability to continue serving the community during extreme weather, which fits our funding priorities.

There is no specified minimum or maximum grant amount for this round; awards are determined based on the project’s scale, demonstrated need, and available funding. Applications requesting between $5,000 and $25,000 are typical, but proposals outside this range will still be considered.

In terms of supporting documents, please prepare the following:
- A letter from your local council confirming their auspice arrangement and willingness to manage funds.
- Financial statements or a budget outlining your current income and expenses.
- Detailed quotes or cost estimates for the rainwater tanks and shade structure.
- A project plan explaining how the infrastructure will improve operational resilience, including timelines and community benefits.
- Supporting letters from community members or stakeholders highlighting the garden’s impact.
