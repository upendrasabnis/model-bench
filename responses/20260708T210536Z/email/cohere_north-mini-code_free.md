Thank you for reaching out and for the clear description of your project. I’m pleased to provide the following information to help you determine whether your organisation can apply for the upcoming Community Resilience Grant round.

**Eligibility of unincorporated groups**  
Our grant programme welcomes applications from both incorporated and unincorporated community groups, provided the applicant can demonstrate a clear governing body and accountability mechanisms. In the case of unincorporated groups that are auspiced by a local council, eligibility is generally accepted, **provided the council formally endorses the project and agrees to act as the fiscal sponsor**. This arrangement allows the council to manage any insurance, risk‑management, and financial reporting requirements on behalf of the group. If you are able to obtain a written statement of auspice from your council confirming that it will hold the grant funds, assume financial liability for the project, and manage any required insurances, your group will meet the eligibility criteria.

**Grant amount – minimum and maximum**  
The Community Resilience Grant does not prescribe a strict minimum or maximum award. Instead, we assess proposals against the fund’s objectives and the scope of the work proposed. The typical grant size for infrastructure‑focused projects ranges from **$15,000 to $75,000**, but we have funded projects outside this band where the justification and impact were compelling. Your projected costs for rainwater tanks and a shade structure appear to sit comfortably within the mid‑range of this band, though the final amount will depend on the detailed budget you submit.

**Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Yes – infrastructure such as rainwater harvesting systems and shade structures is explicitly listed as an eligible expense under the “Climate‑Resilient Community Infrastructure” category of this round. Both items must be essential to the operation of the garden and aligned with the grant’s broader goal of enhancing community resilience to extreme weather events. We recommend that you itemise each component (e.g., tank capacity, material specifications, installation labour) and provide a clear rationale for how each element contributes to sustained operation during hot summers.

**Supporting documents to prepare**  
When you move to the full application stage, we suggest assembling the following documentation:

1. **Project Plan** – a one‑page summary outlining the problem, proposed solution, implementation timeline (including key milestones), and anticipated outcomes.  
2. **Budget and Narrative** – a detailed budget table with line items for equipment, installation, contingency, and any staff time; a narrative that explains how each cost relates to the project goals.  
3. **Letters of Support** – at least two letters from community stakeholders (e.g., local businesses, resident groups) confirming the need and value of the project.  
4. **Council Auspice Documentation** – a formal letter from your council confirming the auspice relationship, its willingness to act as fiscal sponsor, and that it will retain the necessary insurance and risk‑management coverage.  
5. **Organizational Profile** – a brief description of your group’s composition, volunteer base, and any past community projects you have delivered.  
6. **Financial Statements** – the most recent audited or reviewed financial statements (or, for small groups, a simple income‑and‑expenditure summary) demonstrating the $40,000 annual turnover you referenced.  
7. **Site Plans / Drawings** – scaled site plans indicating where the rainwater tanks and shade structure will be located, along with any relevant site constraints.  
8. **Risk Assessment** – a basic risk register for the installation phase, outlining health‑and‑safety, environmental, and operational considerations.

We encourage you to gather these documents in advance, as they will streamline the application process and allow us to evaluate your proposal efficiently.
