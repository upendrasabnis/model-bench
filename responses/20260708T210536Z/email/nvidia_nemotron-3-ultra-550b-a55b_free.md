Thank you for your enquiry regarding the Community Resilience Grant round. I appreciate you reaching out before investing time in a full application, and I am pleased to address each of your questions in turn.

First, regarding your organisational structure: unincorporated groups auspiced by a local government authority are eligible to apply, provided the auspicing body (in this case, your local council) meets our eligibility criteria for lead applicants—specifically, that they are a legal entity with an ABN, hold appropriate public liability insurance, and are willing to enter into a funding agreement on your behalf. Your council’s willingness to hold insurances and act as auspice is a positive indicator. You will need to provide a formal letter of support from the council confirming this arrangement, along with their ABN and insurance certificates of currency.

Second, the grant round offers funding between $5,000 and $50,000 per project. There is no minimum project budget, but the maximum grant amount cannot exceed 80% of total eligible project costs, with the remaining 20% to be contributed as cash or in-kind support (volunteer labour, donated materials, etc.). Given your projected turnover of $40,000, you fall well within the small-to-medium organisation bracket for this program.

Third, capital infrastructure such as rainwater tanks and permanent shade structures are considered eligible costs, provided they are directly linked to the project’s resilience outcomes—namely, maintaining food production and community access during extreme heat events. You will need to supply itemised quotes from at least two suppliers for each major cost item, along with a brief justification of how each component addresses the identified climate risk.

Finally, key supporting documents to prepare include: your council’s auspice agreement letter; your group’s constitution or rules of operation; a current bank statement; a project plan with timeline and risk management strategy; two quotes per major expense; letters of community support; and evidence of land tenure or permission to install infrastructure on the site.

I strongly recommend reviewing the Program Guidelines and Frequently Asked Questions on our website, as they contain detailed definitions of eligible expenditure and assessment criteria.
