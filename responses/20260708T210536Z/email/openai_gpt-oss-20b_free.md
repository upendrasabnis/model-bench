**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our grant criteria explicitly allow applications from “unincorporated community groups, clubs, or committees that are supported by a local government authority.” The key requirement is that the applicant must be a legal entity capable of receiving funds, signing contracts, and reporting outcomes. Because your garden is not yet a registered charity, we will need to confirm that the council can act as a fiscal sponsor. The council’s current insurance cover does not automatically satisfy this requirement; however, if the council can provide a formal letter of sponsorship—confirming that they will receive the grant funds on your behalf, carry out the necessary accounting, and submit all required reporting—your application will be considered.

**2. Grant amount limits**  
The Community Resilience Grant offers funding in the range of $5,000 to $20,000 per project. The exact amount awarded is contingent on the overall budget, the cost–benefit analysis, and the priority of the project within the funding round. With your projected turnover of $40,000, an award between $5,000 and $10,000 would be realistic for the infrastructure you describe.

**3. Eligible infrastructure costs**  
Installation of rainwater tanks and shade structures is explicitly listed as an eligible cost under the *Infrastructure and Resilience* sub‑category. We do, however, require that the infrastructure be:

- **Public‑benefit oriented** – Demonstrating how the garden will continue to serve the community during hotter months.  
- **Sustainable and low‑impact** – Materials that promote water conservation and reduce heat islands are preferred.  
- **Documented** – Detailed ARISING cost estimates, supplier quotations, and a maintenance plan are mandatory.
