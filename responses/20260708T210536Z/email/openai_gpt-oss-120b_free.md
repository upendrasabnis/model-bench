**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
The CRG is open to a broad range of community‑based organisations, including unincorporated groups that operate under the auspices of a registered local government authority. To be eligible, the group must:  

* Be a not‑for‑profit entity that delivers a community benefit;  
* Have a formal relationship (e.g., a memorandum of understanding) with the sponsoring council that confirms the council’s role as the fiscal sponsor and insurer; and  
* Provide evidence that the council will retain financial and legal responsibility for the grant funds.  

Because your garden is already covered by the council’s insurance and you can supply a signed MOU confirming the council’s sponsorship, you meet the organisational eligibility requirements.

* Minimum award – $5,000 (to ensure the project can achieve a measurable outcome).

Given your stated annual turnover of $40,000, there is no upper turnover ceiling for the CRG, so the amount you request will be assessed on the merit of the proposal and the demonstrated community impact.

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs (rainwater tanks and shade structure)**  
The CRG specifically targets projects that increase community resilience to climate‑related stressors. Capital works that improve water security, heat mitigation, or overall garden sustainability are classified as eligible expenditures. Therefore:

* Rainwater tanks (including purchase, delivery, installation, and associated plumbing) are eligible.  
* A permanent shade structure (e.g., a timber frame with UV‑filtering fabric) is also eligible, provided it is directly linked to the garden’s operational continuity during hot periods.  

Both items must be quoted from a licensed supplier and be reasonable in relation to the overall project budget.
