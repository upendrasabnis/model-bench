Thank you for reaching out and for the thoughtful work you are doing with the community garden. I appreciate your diligence in confirming eligibility before committing resources to a full application, and I am happy to provide the information you requested.

**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
The Community Resilience Grant is open to incorporated organisations, registered charities, and unincorporated groups that are formally auspiced by an eligible sponsor—such as a local council, a registered charity, or another incorporated body that holds the necessary insurances and can accept financial responsibility on behalf of the group. Because your garden operates under the auspices of your regional council, which already holds the relevant public liability and volunteer insurance, you meet the auspicing requirement. You will need to provide a letter of support or auspice agreement from the council confirming that they will act as the legal and financial sponsor for the grant, should your application be successful.

**Grant size**  
For this funding round, the minimum award is $5,000 and the maximum is $50,000 (GST exclusive). Applications are assessed on a competitive basis, and the amount awarded will reflect the scope, budget, and demonstrated need of the proposed project. Your estimated budget for rainwater tanks and a shade structure should fall comfortably within this range, but please ensure that your detailed budget does not exceed the $50,000 ceiling.

**Eligible costs**  
Capital infrastructure that directly enhances community resilience to climate‑related stresses is an eligible expense. This includes the purchase and installation of rainwater harvesting systems, shade structures, solar‑powered irrigation, and similar adaptations that increase the usability and sustainability of community spaces. Ongoing operational costs (e.g., staff wages, routine maintenance, or consumables) are not eligible unless they are expressly tied to the delivery of the funded infrastructure (for example, a short‑term project manager to oversee installation). Therefore, the rainwater tanks and shade structure you propose are permissible line items, provided they are itemised with quotes or supplier estimates.

**Supporting documentation to prepare**  
To strengthen your application, please gather the following documents:

1. **Auspice agreement or letter of support** from the regional council, confirming their role as legal and financial sponsor and acknowledging the insurance coverage they provide.  
2. **Detailed project budget** (Excel or PDF) that itemises each cost (tanks, pumps, fittings, shade structure materials, labour, any contingency) and shows total requested funds within the $5k–$50k range.  
3. **Quotes or supplier estimates** for the rainwater tanks and shade structure (at least two independent quotes where possible).  
4. **Project plan** outlining the timeline (e.g., procurement, installation, commissioning), milestones, and how the infrastructure will increase the garden’s capacity to operate during hot summers.  
5. **Evidence of community need and support**, such as letters from members, a brief summary of weekly produce stall patronage, membership numbers, and any council or community‑group endorsements.  
6. **Organisational information** (even though you are unincorporated) – a brief description of the garden’s mission, governance structure (e.g., volunteer coordinator, regular meetings), and how funds will be managed under the council’s auspice.  
7. **Financial statements** (if available) showing the $40,000 annual turnover, or a simple income‑expenditure summary for the past 12 months to demonstrate fiscal responsibility.

Once these items are compiled, you can complete the online application form, upload the documents, and submit before the closing date (which, as you noted, is next month). I recommend allowing a few days for any technical issues that might arise during submission.
