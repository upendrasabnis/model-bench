**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**

The grant programme is designed for organisations that can demonstrate a formal legal status and the ability to manage the award responsibly. In practice this means the applicant must be an Iliad‑registered charity, a not‑for‑profit company, a community interest company, or a public body such as a local council. An unincorporated volunteer group, even if it operates under the auspices of a council, is **not** eligible to apply directly. However, many of our grantees use the council as the legal applicant and include the volunteer group as a key partner. If your council is willing to act as the applicant, the project can be funded and systems in place to ensure the council can sign contracts, hold the funds, and report on outcomes.

**Grant size**

The Community Resilience Grant offers awards ranging from $5,000 to $50,000, depending on the scale and impact of the project. The exact amount is determined during the review stage, after we assess the budget, risk, and community benefit. Because your garden’s turnover is about $40,000, an award of $10,000–$20,000 would likely be appropriate to cover the rainwater tanks and shade structure while leaving room for complementary activities.

**Eligible costs**

Infrastructure that directly supports the resilience of the garden—such as rainwater tanks, shade sails, irrigation upgrades, and solar lighting—is explicitly listed as an eligible cost. Costs must be capital in nature, demonstrably improve the garden’s ability to withstand climate stresses, and be supported by a clear maintenance plan.

**Supporting documentation**

To strengthen your application, please gather the following:

1. **Council endorsement** – a formal letter of support from the council, confirming that it will act as the legal applicant and will provide the necessary insurance and oversight.  
2. **Proof of legal status** – the council’s registration details, or a copy of the community garden’s constitution if you are in the process of incorporating.  
3. **Financial statements** – the garden’s most recent audited or administrated accounts, and a projected cash flow for the next 12 months.  
4. **Project budget** – a detailed line‑item budget that includes the cost of tanks, shade structure, installation, and ongoing maintenance.  
5. **Project plan** – a narrative describing the problem (heat stress, water scarcity), the solution, expected outcomes, and how you will monitor and report on progress.  
6. **Risk assessment** – an assessment of potential risks (e.g., storm damage, tenant changes) and mitigation strategies.  
7. **Community benefit evidence** – membership numbers, attendance at the produce stall, and any volunteer hours logged.
