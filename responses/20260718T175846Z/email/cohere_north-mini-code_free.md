**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our grant program expressly welcomes applications from unincorporated community groups that are formally auspiced—or “hosted”—by a local government entity. When a council holds the necessary insurances, holds any required permits, and serves as the legal “host” for the group’s activities, the group is considered an eligible applicant. In such cases, the council’s involvement is documented in a formal auspice agreement, which we will need to see as part of the submission package. This arrangement ensures that the community garden can benefit from the grant while maintaining the administrative and liability protections that the council provides.

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
There is no fixed minimum grant size; applications are evaluated on the merit of the proposed outcomes, relevance to the grant’s objectives, and the proportion of funding requested relative to the overall project cost. The maximum grant award for any single application in this round is $150,000, although most successful projects receive between $30,000 and $80,000. Your projected costs for rainwater tanks and a shade structure appear well within this range, but we will discuss the exact budget breakdown during the final review stage.

**Eligibility of infrastructure such as rainwater tanks and shade structures**  
Yes, capital infrastructure that directly enhances the resilience and sustainability of community operations is fully eligible. Specifically, rainwater harvesting equipment (tanks, gutters, filtration if required) and durable shade structures that protect both produce and volunteers are considered allowable expenses. We do ask, however, that you include clear justification for how each item will contribute to the project’s resilience goals—such as reducing water reliance during drought periods or mitigating heat stress for staff and visitors.

**Supporting documents we recommend preparing**  
To strengthen your application, we suggest assembling the following documentation:

1. **Auspice Agreement** – A written agreement with your local council confirming that it will hold insurance, permits, and any necessary legal responsibilities on your behalf.  
2. **Organizational Profile** – A brief constitution or set of operating principles, a list of volunteer roles, and any existing governance policies.  
3. **Financial Statements** – Most recent audited or reviewed financial statements (or, for a small operation, a simple profit‑and‑loss summary and balance sheet) demonstrating the $40,000 annual turnover you mentioned, together with source‑of‑funds documentation.  
4. **Project Plan** – Detailed specifications for the rainwater tanks (capacity, source, integration with existing systems) and the shade structure (size, materials, placement), along with a timeline and milestones.  
5. **Budget Sheet** – Itemized cost breakdown, including equipment, installation, contingency, and any staff or volunteer hours required.  
6. **Community Impact Statement** – Evidence of local need (e.g., climate data on rising summer temperatures), a description of the target beneficiary group, and measurable outcomes you expect to achieve.  
7. **Letters of Support** – Written endorsements from the council, local businesses, or community members confirming backing for the project.  
8. **Insurance Certificate** – Confirmation that the council’s insurance coverage extends to the activities you will undertake with the grant funds.
