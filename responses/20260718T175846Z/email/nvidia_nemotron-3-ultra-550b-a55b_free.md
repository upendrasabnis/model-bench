Dear Jamie,

Thank you for your enquiry regarding the Community Resilience Grant round. I appreciate you reaching out before investing time in a full application, and I am happy to provide clarification on each of your questions.

**Eligibility of Auspiced Groups:** Unincorporated groups operating under a formal auspicing arrangement with an eligible legal entity—such as a local government authority—are eligible to apply, provided the auspicing organisation meets the program’s eligibility criteria (e.g., is an incorporated not-for-profit, local government body, or other eligible entity). The auspicing organisation must submit the application on your behalf, sign the funding agreement, and accept legal and financial responsibility for the grant. Please ensure you have a written auspicing agreement in place that clearly outlines the roles, responsibilities, and liability arrangements between your group and the council.

**Grant Amounts:** For this round, the minimum grant request is $5,000 and the maximum is $50,000. Your project budget should fall within this range. There is no requirement for matched funding, though evidence of in-kind contributions or volunteer labour strengthens the application.

**Eligible Costs:** Infrastructure that directly builds community resilience to climate impacts is a priority for this grant. Rainwater tanks and shade structures that enable continued operation during extreme heat and water restrictions are considered eligible capital expenditure. Eligible costs include purchase, delivery, installation, and any necessary site preparation or plumbing work. Ongoing maintenance costs, however, are not eligible.

**Supporting Documents:** You should prepare the following:
1. A signed letter from the council confirming the auspicing arrangement and their willingness to administer the grant.
2. The council’s current Certificate of Currency for Public Liability and Workers’ Compensation insurance.
3. A detailed project budget with at least two quotes for each major cost item (tanks, structure, installation).
4. A simple project plan with timelines, risk management, and volunteer coordination details.
5. Evidence of community need and support (e.g., letters of support, usage data from the produce stall, membership numbers).
6. Your group’s most recent financial statement or a summary of income and expenditure for the last financial year.

The application portal opens on 1 June and closes at 5:00 pm AEST on 30 June. I strongly recommend starting the application early to allow time for council sign-off. If you would like, I can arrange a brief phone call to walk through the budget template or answer any further questions.
