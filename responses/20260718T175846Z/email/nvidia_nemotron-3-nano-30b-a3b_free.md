**Eligibility of groups auspiced by a council**  
Our funding guidelines allow organisations that are not incorporated societies or charities to apply, provided they are formally auspiced by a local government entity that holds the necessary insurance and can verify the group’s activities. Since your garden operates under the umbrella of the municipal council and the council holds the required public liability coverage, you meet this criterion. However, you will need to supply a copy of the council’s written confirmation of auspice, along with evidence that the council is listed as the primary point of contact for any insurance or compliance matters.  

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The current funding round offers grants ranging from **$5,000 up to $50,000**. There is no separate tier for “small” projects; the amount awarded is determined by the scope, impact, and cost‑effectiveness of the proposed work. Your rain‑water tank and shade‑structure plan fits comfortably within this band, and we anticipate that a request in the $15,000–$20,000 range would be well positioned for approval, assuming the budget aligns with the project’s specifications.  

**Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Capital infrastructure that directly supports the resilience of the operation is an eligible cost category. This includes, but is not limited to, water‑capture systems, shading structures, solar panels, and other physical assets that enhance the site’s ability to continue functioning under adverse conditions. The rain‑water tanks and shade structure you propose qualify as eligible expenditures, provided they are documented as essential to maintaining garden productivity during hotter summers.  

**Supporting documentation to prepare**  
To strengthen your application, please include the following items:  

1. A signed letter from the council confirming your auspice status and outlining the scope of the partnership.  
2. A detailed project budget that breaks down costs for the rain‑water tanks, shade structure, associated installation, and any ancillary works.  
3. Evidence of community benefit, such as membership numbers, volunteer hours, and produce‑stall revenue projections.  
4. Copies of any relevant insurance certificates held by the council on your behalf.  
5. A concise project timeline (e.g., design, procurement, installation, monitoring) to demonstrate feasibility within the grant period.
