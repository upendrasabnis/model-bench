Thank you for your email and for your thoughtful questions regarding the Rural Health Innovation Grant (RHIG‑2026‑03). We appreciate the work your organization, the Valley Community Health Partnership (VCHP), is doing to advance health equity in eastern Montana and are pleased to see your strong interest in this funding opportunity. Below, I have addressed each of your eligibility-related queries in detail to ensure your application is fully informed and compliant with program requirements.  

First, concerning the three-year operational history requirement: the grant guidelines specify that applicants must demonstrate at least three years of continuous operational presence in the target community. Importantly, *informal or pre-incorporation activity may be considered part of this history*, provided it is verifiable through documented evidence—such as service logs, funding records, partnership agreements, or community testimonials—that confirms sustained program delivery throughout the period in question. Since VCHP delivered mobile clinic services to the same geographic area from 2019 to 2023, and your formal incorporation in 2023 represents a continuation—not a new start—of those efforts, your combined operational history meets the eligibility threshold. Please include a brief narrative and supporting documentation in your application to substantiate this continuity.  

Second, regarding the median household income requirement: the grant does **not** require *all* counties in the service area to fall below the national poverty line. Instead, eligibility is determined by the *proportion of the total served population* residing in areas where the median household income is at or below the poverty threshold. As long as a **minimum of 75% of the individuals served** by the proposed project reside in such areas, the application will be considered eligible. Since your service area includes a mix of communities—with some at or below and others above the threshold—you should calculate and report the percentage of served individuals meeting the income criterion in your LOI and full application.  

Third, on co-funding: in-kind contributions—including donated staff time, pro bono services, or equipment—are indeed acceptable toward the 30% co-funding requirement, *provided they are quantifiable, verifiable, and directly tied to project implementation*. In your case, the regional health insurer’s contribution of service hours would qualify, but you must submit a formal letter of support from the insurer specifying the scope of services, the estimated fair market value of the donated hours (based on standard hourly rates for similar expertise), and confirmation that the contribution is non-cash and will be secured prior to grant award. Please retain time logs, signed agreements, and valuation documentation for audit purposes.  

Finally, regarding the LOI deadline: the **official submission deadline remains July 31, 2026, at 5:00 PM MT**. There has been no extension, and submissions received after this date will not be reviewed. We recommend submitting well in advance to avoid technical issues with the online portal.
