Thank you for your interest in the Rural Health Innovation Grant (RHIG) and for reaching out with such detailed questions. I am happy to provide clarification on each of the points you raised so that the Valley Community Health Partnership (VCHP) can prepare a fully compliant application.

**1. Operational‑history requirement**  
The guideline stating a “minimum of three years of operational history in the target community” is intended to ensure that awardees have an established presence and demonstrated capacity in the area they will serve. The three‑year window may be satisfied by either a formally incorporated nonprofit or by a predecessor entity that performed the same core activities in the same geographic region. Because VCHP operated continuously as an unincorporated community health collective from 2019 through 2023, that period can be counted toward the three‑year requirement, provided you include documentation (e.g., annual reports, service logs, IRS 501(c)(3) determination letter for the post‑2023 period, and any relevant state registrations) that clearly shows the continuity of mission, leadership, and service delivery. In short, you do not need to have been a registered nonprofit for the entire three years, but you must be able to substantiate the uninterrupted operation.

**2. Median‑income eligibility**  
The poverty‑criterion is applied at the project‑level rather than at the county‑level. The grant requires that the *overall* population served by the project have a median household income below the national poverty line. In practice this means that the weighted average of the incomes of all individuals or households within your service footprint must fall below the threshold. Consequently, it is acceptable if some counties are slightly above the line, provided that the combined population you intend to serve (e.g., 60 % or more of the total) meets the income requirement. When you complete the application, please include a concise demographic table that shows the median income for each county and the weighted average for the entire project area, along with the source of the data (e.g., U.S. Census ACS 2022).

**3. In‑kind co‑funding**  
Co‑funding limits apply to the *total value* of the contribution, whether cash or in‑kind, as long as the value can be reasonably quantified. The insurer’s donation of service hours can therefore be counted toward the 30 % ceiling, provided you assign a fair market value to those hours (e.g., using the prevailing rate for comparable telehealth services). For in‑kind contributions we require:  
   - A signed letter of commitment from the partner specifying the nature of the contribution, the estimated dollar value, and the duration of the support.  
   - A brief description of how the services will be integrated into the project budget.  
   - Documentation of the methodology used to calculate the fair market value (e.g., rate sheets, vendor quotes).  

Please ensure that the total of all co‑funding—cash plus in‑kind—does not exceed 30 % of the approved budget.

**4. LOI deadline**  
The official deadline for the Letter of Intent remains **July 31, 2026, 11:59 p.m. Mountain Time**. The suggestion that the date had been extended to August 7 was based on an internal discussion for a separate funding stream and does not apply to RHIG‑2026‑03. Submissions received after July 31 will be marked as late and, in most cases, will not be considered. If extenuating circumstances arise, you may request a brief extension in writing, but approval is not guaranteed.
