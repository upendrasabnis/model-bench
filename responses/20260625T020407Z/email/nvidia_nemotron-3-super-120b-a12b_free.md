Dear Dr. Karim,

Thank you for your thoughtful inquiry and for the Valley Community Health Partnership’s interest in the Rural Health Innovation Grant (RHIG‑2026‑03). I am pleased to provide the clarifications you requested, addressing each point in turn.

**1. Operational History Requirement**  
The three‑year operational history criterion is intended to demonstrate sustained engagement with the target community, regardless of the legal form of the organization. Your period of activity as an unincorporated community health collective from 2019 to 2023, delivering mobile clinic services within the same geographic area, will be counted toward the requirement. When you submit your application, please include a brief narrative (no more than one page) outlining the scope, frequency, and outcomes of those services, together with any supporting documentation (e.g., service logs, partnership agreements, or letters from community stakeholders) that verifies the continuity of operation. Formal incorporation in 2023 does not reset the clock; the cumulative experience satisfies the guideline.

**2. Poverty‑Threshold Population Criterion**  
Eligibility is based on the proportion of the *served* population that falls below the national poverty line, not on a county‑by‑county basis. It is acceptable if at least 60 % of the individuals your project will directly serve have a median household income below the poverty threshold. In your project description, clearly delineate the target beneficiary group (e.g., patients utilizing the mobile clinics or telehealth platform) and provide demographic data — such as census‑tract income statistics or survey results — that substantiate the 60 % figure. Areas with incomes above the threshold may still be included in your service area, provided they do not dilute the overall poverty‑population proportion below the required level.

**3. In‑Kind Co‑Funding**  
In‑kind contributions are permissible toward the 30 % co‑funding limit, provided they are directly related to the project activities and can be reasonably valued. The health insurer’s offer of service hours (e.g., technical support, staff time, or platform access) may be counted as in‑kind co‑funding. To include this in your budget, you must submit:  
- A signed letter of commitment from the insurer detailing the specific services to be provided, the estimated number of hours, and the hourly rate used for valuation.  
- A brief description of how these services integrate into the project work plan.  
- Any relevant contracts or memoranda of understanding that outline the terms of the donation.  
The total valuation of all in‑kind contributions, combined with any cash co‑funding, must not exceed 30 % of the overall project budget.
