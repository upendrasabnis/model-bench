Dear Jamie,

Thank you for reaching out and for the clear description of your project and organisational structure. I’m pleased to provide the following information that will help you determine whether your group can proceed with a full application for the upcoming Community Resilience Grant round.

**Eligibility of Unincorporated Groups Auspiced by a Council**  
Our grant programme welcomes applications from a broad spectrum of community‑based organisations. Unincorporated groups that operate under the auspices of a local government entity are indeed eligible, provided that the council can demonstrate legal responsibility for the funds, any liabilities, and the delivery of the project outcomes. In practice, this means the council must sign a formal “auspice agreement” confirming that it will act as fiscal sponsor, manage any grant disbursements, and assume accountability for reporting and compliance. If you do not already have such an agreement in place, we can provide a standard template that many of our previous grantees have used successfully. Please ensure that the agreement clearly outlines the scope of the council’s responsibilities, the reporting relationship, and any conditions regarding the use of the grant funds.

**Minimum and Maximum Grant Amounts**  
The Community Resilience Grant does not prescribe a strict minimum or maximum award size. Instead, we evaluate each proposal on its own merits and allocate funds that are proportionate to the projected impact, total project cost, and the organisation’s capacity to deliver. Our previous grants have ranged from roughly $5,000 to $150,000, but we are happy to discuss your budget and help you identify a realistic funding target that aligns with the scope of installing rainwater tanks and a shade structure. If you anticipate total eligible costs of around $30,000–$40,000, a grant in the $25,000–$35,000 range would typically be appropriate, leaving you to cover any shortfall through other community fundraising or in‑kind contributions.

**Eligibility of Infrastructure Costs**  
Yes, capital infrastructure such as rainwater harvesting systems and permanent shade structures is fully eligible under the “Climate‑Resilient Community Projects” component of the grant. The key is that the items must be directly attributable to enhancing the sustainability and resilience of your garden’s operations, particularly in the face of increasing summer temperatures. Both the purchase, delivery, and installation of the tanks (including any necessary plumbing and storage containers) and the design, fabrication, and erection of a weather‑rated shade structure meet our criteria. We recommend that you itemise each component in your budget breakdown, noting any ancillary costs such as site preparation, foundation work, and necessary permits.

**Supporting Documents to Prepare**  
To strengthen your application, we suggest assembling the following documentation:

1. **Project Narrative** – A concise description of the problem you are addressing (e.g., water scarcity during hot summers), the proposed solution, expected outcomes, and how the project aligns with the grant’s resilience objectives.  
2. **Budget and Budget Narrative** – A detailed spreadsheet that separates capital costs (tanks, shade structure, installation, permits) from operating costs, with explanations for each line item.  
3. **Auspice Agreement** – A signed agreement from your local council confirming its role as fiscal sponsor, including a clause that the council will retain relevant insurances and liability coverage.  
4. **Organizational Profile** – A brief overview of your volunteer group, its mission, current activities, and the annual turnover figure you mentioned.  
5. **Financial Statements** – The most recent audited or reviewed financial statements (or, for a small volunteer‑run operation, a simple profit‑and‑loss statement and balance sheet) demonstrating the $40,000 turnover and the sources of income.  
6. **Letters of Support** – Written endorsements from the council, neighbouring community groups, or local businesses that can attest to the community need and the viability of the project.  
7. **Site Plans or Sketches** – A simple diagram showing the proposed location of the rainwater tanks and shade structure within the garden.  
8. **Risk Assessment and Safety Plans** – Documentation outlining how you will manage installation safety, ongoing maintenance, and any potential environmental impacts.  

We also recommend that you review the full grant guidelines on our website (Section 4 – Eligibility and Section 5 – Required Documentation) to ensure you do not overlook any additional requirements such as proof of public liability insurance (which your council already holds) or compliance with local building codes.

Finally, should you decide to move forward, please submit a short pre‑application form (available on the portal) no later than two weeks before the official deadline. This will allow us to confirm that your project meets all technical criteria before you invest time in preparing the full dossier.
