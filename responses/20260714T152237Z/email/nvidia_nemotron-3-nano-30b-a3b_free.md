Dear Jamie,

Thank you for reaching out and for the thoughtful description of your community‑garden project. I’m pleased to provide clarification on each of the points you raised so that you can move forward with confidence.

**1. Eligibility of groups auspiced by a council**  
Our funding guidelines allow organisations that are not formally incorporated to apply, provided they are operating under the auspices of a local government authority that holds the necessary public‑liability insurance and can act as a fiscal agent. In such cases the council’s registration number and a copy of the council’s insurance certificate must be included in the application package. Since your garden is already recognised by the council and insured through its policies, you meet this eligibility criterion.

**2. Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
The Community Resilience Grant round offers awards ranging from **$5,000 up to $50,000**. Projects that demonstrate a clear public benefit and a realistic budget within this band are prioritised. Your proposed work – the installation of rainwater tanks and a shade structure – comfortably fits within the scope of eligible projects and could be funded at any level up to the maximum, depending on the overall budget you submit.

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Capital‑cost items that directly enhance the capacity of the organisation to serve the community are eligible, including **water‑capture systems, shade structures, and associated civil works**. These are classified as “essential infrastructure” and are routinely funded under the grant, provided they are properly cost‑tracked and linked to the project’s resilience objectives.

**4. Supporting documentation to prepare**  
To strengthen your application, please include the following items:

* A copy of the council’s letter confirming your auspiced status and confirming that the council’s insurance covers your activities.  
* The council’s registration number and a copy of its public‑liability insurance certificate.  
* A detailed project budget that breaks down the cost of the rainwater tanks, shade structure, site preparation, and any related professional fees.  
* A concise project plan outlining objectives, timelines, and how the infrastructure will improve resilience to extreme weather.  
* Evidence of community support, such as letters of endorsement from the council, local residents, or partner organisations.  
* Proof of your annual turnover (e.g., audited financial statements or a financial summary) to demonstrate the $40,000 turnover figure.
