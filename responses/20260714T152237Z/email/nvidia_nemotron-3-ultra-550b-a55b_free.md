Dear Jamie,

Thank you for your enquiry regarding the Community Resilience Grant round. I appreciate you reaching out before investing time in a full application, and I am happy to address each of your questions in detail.

**Eligibility of Auspiced Groups**
Yes, unincorporated groups operating under a formal auspicing arrangement with an eligible legal entity—such as a local council—are eligible to apply. The auspicing organisation must be the legal applicant, sign the funding agreement, and assume financial and reporting responsibility for the grant. As your council already holds the relevant insurances, they would need to provide a letter of support confirming their willingness to auspice the project, along with a copy of their current public liability insurance certificate and their ABN.

**Grant Amounts**
For this round, the minimum grant request is $5,000 and the maximum is $50,000. Your projected budget for rainwater tanks and a shade structure appears to fall comfortably within this range. Please note that we cannot fund 100 per cent of project costs; a minimum 20 per cent contribution (cash or in-kind) from the applicant or auspicing body is required.

**Eligible Costs**
Infrastructure that builds climate resilience is a priority for this program. Rainwater harvesting systems and permanent shade structures are explicitly listed as eligible capital expenditure, provided they are fixed assets that remain with the garden site. Costs for site preparation, plumbing, engineering certification, and installation labour are also allowable. Ongoing operational costs (e.g., water rates, maintenance) are not eligible.

**Supporting Documents to Prepare**
In addition to the auspicing letter and insurance certificate mentioned above, you should gather:
- A simple project plan with timeline and risk management strategy.
- Two quotes for each major expenditure item (tanks, structure, installation).
- Evidence of land tenure or a long-term lease/licence for the garden site.
- Your most recent financial statement or treasurer’s report showing the $40,000 turnover.
- Letters of support from community partners or the council demonstrating broader community benefit.

Your annual turnover of $40,000 is well within the program’s thresholds for small community organisations.
