### Style & Quality Guidelines

1. **Clarity & Precision** – Avoid jargon unless defined; use active voice.  
2. **Evidence‑Based** – Cite Federal Register notices, OMB Circulars (e.g., A‑110, A‑133), agency strategic plans, and recent GAO reports. Use footnotes or bracketed citations.  
3. **Action‑Oriented** – Every major section ends with a **“Quick‑Start Checklist”** (5‑7 bullet points).  
4. **Visual Hierarchy** – H1 = Article Title, H2 = Major Sections, H3 = Sub‑sections, H4 = Step details.  
5. **Tables** – Use markdown tables; keep column count ≤7 for readability.  
6. **Call‑out Boxes** – Use `> **Pro Tip:** …` or `> **Key Takeaway:** …` formatting.  
7. **Accessibility** – Provide alt‑text descriptions for any figures (even if not rendered).  
8. **Length Management** – Aim for ~12,500 words; if a section runs long, condense with summary tables.
