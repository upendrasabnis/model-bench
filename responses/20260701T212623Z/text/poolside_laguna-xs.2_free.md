## Section 2: Major Federal Grant Programs for Southwest Climate Resilience

### 2.1 EPA Clean Water State Revolving Fund (SRF)

**Program Overview:**
• Administered through state agencies with federal backing
• Focuses on water infrastructure resilience
• Supports green infrastructure and climate adaptation

**Eligibility Criteria:**
• Public water systems and states
• Municipalities and tribal entities
• Nonprofits partnerships with eligible entities
• Priority for disadvantaged communities

**Award Sizes & Funding Sources:**
• Typical range: $1 million - $100 million+
• Low-interest loans and grants available
• State-specific allocation formulas

**Application Cycles:**
• Annual cycles with varying deadlines by state
• Continuous intake for emergency situations

**Strategic Fit for Climate Resilience:**
• Drought-resistant water infrastructure
• Flood management systems
• Wastewater treatment modernization

### 2.2 USDA Rural Development Climate-Smart Programs

#### 2.2.1 Environmental Quality Incentives Program (EQIP)
**Eligibility:**
• Agricultural producers in rural areas
• Tribes and tribal organizations
• Nonprofits for conservation partnerships

**Award Size:** Varies by practice; average $5,000-$50,000 per project

**Timeline:** Year-round applications with ranking periods

#### 2.2.2 Rural Energy for America Program (REAP)
**Focus:** Renewable energy and energy efficiency

**Award Size:** Up to 50% cost share, maximum $2 million

### 2.3 DOE Office of Science Programs

#### 2.3.1 Atmospheric and Geospace Sciences Program
**Research Focus:** Climate modeling and regional impacts

**Award Size:** $100,000-$2 million for research projects

#### 2.3.2 Biological and Environmental Research

### 2.4 HUD Community Development Block Grants (CDBG)

**Eligibility:**
• Entitlement communities (metropolitan cities, counties)
• Consortium arrangements allowed
• Non-entitlement communities through states

**Award Size:** Formula-based allocations starting at ~$50,000

**Climate Resilience Applications:**
• Infrastructure hardening
• Housing affordability and resilience
• Community facility improvements

### 2.5 FEMA Pre-Disaster Mitigation (PDM) Grant Program

**Eligibility:**
• States, tribes, territories
• Local governments through state submissions
• Priority for repetitive loss properties

**Award Size:** 
• Planning grants: $25,000-$200,000
• Project grants: $1 million-$50 million+

**Application Timeline:**
• Annual notice of funding opportunity
• Typically falls in fall/winter cycle

## Section 5: Common Pitfalls and Mitigation Strategies

### Overlapping Funding Challenges

**Problem:** Multiple applications for same project components
**Mitigation:**
• Create comprehensive project database
• Establish inter-departmental coordination
• Develop unified funding strategy
• Maintain detailed tracking systems

### Cost-Share Calculation Errors

**Common Issues:**
• In-kind valuation miscalculations
• Indirect cost allocation problems
• Matching fund documentation gaps

**Prevention Strategies:**
• Use standardized valuation methodologies
• Maintain contemporaneous documentation
• Engage finance professionals early
• Implement regular audit checkpoints

### Environmental Review Delays

**Typical Bottlenecks:**
• NEPA compliance requirements
• Section 106 historic preservation reviews
• Endangered species act consultations
• Tribal consultation processes

**Mitigation Approaches:**
• Initiate environmental review concurrently with application
• Engage regulatory agencies early
• Develop phased implementation plans
• Build review timelines into project schedules
