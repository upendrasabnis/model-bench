**1. Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
The grant is open to “community organisations” that have a statutory or formal basis of operation. While a registered charity is preferred, the policy explicitly allows for unincorporated groups that are formally endorsed by a local council, provided that the council can confirm that it holds all relevant insurances and that the group operates under its auspices. In practice, this means you will need a formal endorsement letter from your council (including a copy of the council’s minutes approving the endorsement) and evidence that the council remains the legal insurer of the garden.

**2. Minimum/maximum grant amount**  
The Community Resilience Grant is divided into two funding brackets:

| Grant bracket | Minimum award | Maximum award |
|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| Small‑scale projects | $2,000 | $8,000 |
| Medium‑scale projects | $8,001 | $15,000 |
| Large‑scale projects | $15,001 | $25,000 |

Your proposed rainwater tanks and shade structure fall squarely in the medium‑scale bracket; we would expect an award of between $8,000 and $15,000, subject to the overall budget and cost‑justification.

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
Infrastructure that enhances resilience to climate impacts is an eligible cost category, provided it is directly tied to the grant’s objectives. The rainwater tanks and shade structure temanually qualify as “resilient infrastructure” that reduces heat stress and preserves produce during high‑temperature periods.

**4. Supporting documents**  
To strengthen your application, please prepare the following:

1. **Council endorsement letter** (signed by the council mayor/administrator, dated, and referencing the council minutes).  
2. **Proof of insurance** (a copy of the council’s liability policy covering the garden).  
3. **Detailed budget** (itemised cost for tanks, shade structure, installation, contingency, and ongoing maintenance).  
4. **Project narrative** (one‑page description of the need, objectives, and expected outcomes).  
5. **Financial statements** (the last two years of financial records, or a simple turnover statement showing the $40,000 figure).  
6. **Letters of support** (from local growers, schools, or community groups who will benefit).  
7. **Timeline** (key milestones from procurement to commissioning).  
8. **Photographic evidence** (current garden layout and site conditions).
