Thank you for your email and for your interest in the Community Resilience Grant program. I’m happy to provide clarity on the eligibility requirements and application process for your community garden project.

Firstly, **unincorporated groups operating under council auspices are indeed eligible** to apply for this grant. We recognise that many community-led initiatives operate in this way, and as long as your local council is prepared to act as the responsible governing body and oversee financial accountability, your arrangement meets our criteria. You will need to include a letter from the council confirming their support and auspicing role as part of your application documentation.

Regarding funding levels, we offer grants between **$5,000 and $50,000**, depending on the scale and impact of your project. Your proposed work—installing rainwater tanks and a shade structure—sounds well within this range, and would likely fall into our mid-tier category.

Importantly, **infrastructure projects like yours are strongly encouraged** and qualify as eligible costs. We specifically seek initiatives that build long-term community resilience, particularly in the face of climate challenges. Both the rainwater tanks and shade structure directly support your garden’s sustainability and continued operation, making them excellent candidates for funding.

For your application, please prepare the following supporting documents:  
- Completed application form  
- Project budget and timeline  
- Letter of support from your local council  
- Evidence of your group’s activities and community engagement (e.g., photos, attendance records)  
- Quotes or estimates for the proposed infrastructure  
- Any relevant environmental or sustainability plans

I encourage you to review the full guidelines on our website for detailed templates and further examples. If you’d like to discuss your proposal in more detail before applying, feel free to contact me directly.
