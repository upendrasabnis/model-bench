**1. Eligibility of groups auspiced by a council**  
The Community Resilience Grant is open to incorporated not‑for‑profit organisations, registered charities, and public‑sector entities. While unincorporated groups can sometimes be eligible when they are formally **auspiced by a local council**, the applicant must demonstrate that the council has assumed full legal responsibility for the project’s administration, insurance, and financial oversight. In practice, this means the council must provide a written **Letter of Support** confirming that:  

- The council holds the appropriate public liability and workers‑compensation insurances on behalf of the group;  
- The group is listed on the council’s registers of community organisations; and  
- The council will act as the primary point of contact for any reporting or audit obligations.  

If your council can furnish such documentation, you may proceed under the council’s auspice. However, because the grant program requires a legally recognised entity to receive funding, you will need to identify a **lead applicant** (either the council itself or an incorporated body acting as trustee) who will sign the formal grant agreement.  

**2. Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
For the current funding round, the allowable request range is **$5,000 to $50,000**. Projects that demonstrate a clear, measurable impact on community resilience—particularly those that involve physical infrastructure—are more likely to receive funding at the higher end of the spectrum.  

**3. Eligibility of infrastructure costs**  
The grant explicitly funds **eligible capital works** that enhance the capacity of a community organisation to respond to climate‑related stresses. Rainwater harvesting systems, shade structures, and associated site preparation are considered **eligible infrastructure costs** provided they meet the following criteria:  

- The work must be **permanent** (i.e., not easily disassembled) and installed on council‑owned or council‑controlled land;  
- The design must incorporate resilience outcomes such as water security, temperature mitigation, or reduced storm‑water runoff;  
- The project must be accompanied by a maintenance plan that outlines how the infrastructure will be sustained after the grant period.  

**4. Recommended supporting documents**  
To strengthen your application, please consider preparing the following items:  

- A **Letter of Support** from the council confirming its auspicing role and insurance coverage;  
- A **Project Proposal** (2–3 pages) outlining objectives, expected outcomes, and the resilience benefits of the rainwater tanks and shade structure;  
- A **Detailed Budget** that breaks down capital costs, any matched funding, and ongoing maintenance expenses;  
- **Quotes or tenders** from reputable suppliers/installers for the proposed works;  
- Evidence of **community need**, such as demographic data, stakeholder letters, or a brief impact survey of garden users;  
- A **Maintenance and Monitoring Plan** that shows how the infrastructure will be inspected, serviced, and reported on during and after the grant term.  

Should you need assistance with any of these documents or clarification on the auspicing requirements, please feel free to contact me directly. I am happy to arrange a brief call or provide template letters to streamline the preparation process.
