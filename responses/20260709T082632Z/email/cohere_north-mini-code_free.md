Thank you for reaching out and for your interest in the Community Resilience Grant. I’m pleased to confirm that there are a few points you raised that merit clarification, and I hope the information below helps you decide whether to proceed with a full application.  

**Eligibility of unincorporated groups auspiced by a council**  
Our grant programme is designed to support a broad spectrum of community‑based initiatives. While we do give preference to incorporated non‑profit entities, unincorporated groups that operate under the formal auspice of a local government are eligible to apply, provided the council can confirm that it will act as the fiscal host for any awarded funds. In practice this means we will require a written auspice agreement (or a letter of support) from your council that outlines the council’s responsibility for insurance, liability, and any necessary procurement processes. If such an agreement is in place, we can proceed with the application using your group’s name, with the council listed as the administering body.  

**Minimum and maximum grant amounts**  
There is no rigid minimum or maximum grant size; awards are determined on the basis of project merit, alignment with programme objectives, and the proportion of total project costs that the grant can reasonably cover. In recent funding rounds, grants have ranged from approximately $5,000 to $150,000, with many successful projects receiving amounts in the $20,000‑$60,000 bracket. Your projected budget for rainwater tanks and a shade structure will be compared against these benchmarks, and we can discuss realistic funding levels once we see a detailed cost estimate.  

**Eligibility of infrastructure such as rainwater tanks and shade structures**  
Capital infrastructure that directly enhances the resilience and sustainability of community operations is fully eligible. Rainwater harvesting systems, permanent shade structures, and associated site works (e.g., foundations, plumbing, permits) are considered allowable costs, provided they are essential to the project’s objectives and are documented with clear quotations and installation plans. We do require evidence that the items are not merely decorative or ancillary, but serve a functional purpose for the garden’s ongoing operation during extreme weather events.  

**Supporting documentation you should prepare**  
To present a strong application we recommend assembling the following items:  

1. **Project narrative** – a concise description of the problem you are addressing, the proposed solution, expected outcomes, and how the project aligns with the grant’s resilience focus.  
2. **Detailed budget** – itemized costs for equipment, installation, permits, and any contingency funds, with quotations where available.  
3. **Letters of support** – one from your council confirming the auspice arrangement and any other partner organisations (e.g., local schools, businesses).  
4. **Insurance and liability documentation** – a copy of the council’s insurance certificate, or a statement confirming that the council will maintain coverage for the duration of the project.  
5. **Financial statements** – the most recent audited or reviewed accounts (or a set of cash‑flow statements if you are a small volunteer group) demonstrating your $40,000 turnover and how it is derived.  
6. **Site plans or sketches** – showing the location of the rainwater tanks and shade structure within the garden.  
7. **Implementation timeline** – a realistic schedule with key milestones and target completion dates.  

Please note that while we have listed these as recommended best practices, the exact checklist will be detailed in the official application guidelines that you will receive after you submit a preliminary inquiry.
